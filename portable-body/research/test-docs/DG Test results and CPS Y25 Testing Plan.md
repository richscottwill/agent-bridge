DG Test results and CPS Y25 Testing Plan
PURPOSE
This document presents the results of our Google Demand Generation (DG) campaigns in the US, UK, and JP, demonstrating
the effectiveness of this approach in driving brand awareness and search interest in Amazon Business. Key highlights include a
+76% in “Amazon Business” search intent in the US, +135% in the UK, and +142% in JP; indicating potential for driving customer
acquisition. Learnings from this test will inform future testing of Demand Generation ads to increase enterprise-level intent.
DEMAND GEN OVERVIEW
Google DG campaigns provide an efficient way to reach potential Amazon Business (AB) customers within enterprise
organizations. By utilizing Google’s Discovery Feed, Gmail, and YouTube placements, we can engage target audiences with
visually compelling ads. DG ads’ strength lies in its advanced targeting capabilities, including lookalike segments that identify
prospective CPS customers who look like our current Enterprise customers and haven’t yet interacted with AB. Combined with
flexible bidding strategies optimized for clicks and highly tailored ad creative, DG ads deliver efficiency gains, resulting in +409%
clicks with 71% less spend than our SSR accounts for the same period. (DG CPC $0.3, SSR CPC $3.4, CPS CPC $11.0)
RESULTS OVERVIEW
Brand Lift: This study measured how effectively our video ads influenced perceptions of our brand. While the US market showed
limited lift in ad recall and consideration, the UK and JP markets showed positive results across all studies.
This variance likely due to the use of a lookalike audience based on existing AB registrants, a targeting strategy necessitated by
privacy regulations. For future US campaigns, we will test broader targeting approaches to drive brand lift. Overall, viewers
exposed to our ads demonstrated a 2% higher likelihood of brand recall, consideration, and potential purchase intent. However,
this effect was less pronounced in the US market. (Data for each market below)

Search Lift: We also examined the impact of our DG ads on search behavior, specifically their ability to drive searches for
relevant keywords. Searches for "Amazon Business" were higher across all target markets after viewing the DG ads: +78% in the
US, +135% in the UK, and +142% in JP. These regional differences can be attributed to varying levels of brand awareness and
market penetration. The higher lift in the UK and JP highlights the value of DG in less developed markets, where it can build
brand awareness more rapidly and drive customer acquisition. In the US, DG ads reinforce our market leadership and contributes
to sustained growth. These results support continued investment in DG as a core component of our marketing strategy.

FUTURE STATE
CPS: Building on the results from our DG campaigns in driving brand search, we propose leveraging DG to generate interest and
demand for CPS. We will initially focus on the US and UK markets with a total Y25 budget of $721K ($250K US, $471K UK). In
the US, we will continue to use lookalike audiences based on both our entire registrant audience as well as test an enterprise-
only lookalike audience. We will refine our targeting in both markets by adding audience layers (see Appendix) to pinpoint
enterprise users and drive qualified traffic to dedicated CPS landing pages, increasing enterprise consideration.
We will refine our targeting in both markets by adding audience layers (see Appendix) to pinpoint enterprise users and drive
qualified traffic to dedicated CPS landing pages, increasing enterprise consideration. Additionally, we will leverage lookalike

well as test an enterprise-only lookalike audience. For next steps, the CPS team will provide necessary landing pages and
creative assets, while the PS team will finalize the targeting strategy and implementation.
