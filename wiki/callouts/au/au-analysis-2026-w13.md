# AU W13 Analysis Brief

## Registration summary

AU drove 207 registrations (-16% WoW), with -13% spend WoW. CPA rose slightly to $118 (+3% WoW). March is projected to end at ~$125K spend and ~1,030 registrations, at $121 CPA. (vs. OP2: -15% spend, -5% registrations)

## Why registrations changed

Registrations fell -16% WoW primarily on CVR compression (-12% WoW) with a secondary contribution from clicks (-4% WoW). Both segments declined: Brand fell -18% on -14% CVR (8.54% to 7.37%) with -5% clicks, and NB fell -13% on -10% CVR (2.87% to 2.58%) with -4% clicks. The CVR decline was broad-based, not segment-specific, which points to a site-level or landing page factor rather than a traffic quality issue.

The most likely cause is the Polaris URL migration, which was completing during W13 (Alexis mapping received 3/17, migration executing 3/24-25). The daily pattern supports this: Mon-Tue (3/23-24, pre/early migration) delivered 53 and 50 regs, then Wed-Sat (3/25-28, during/post migration) collapsed to 27, 22, 23, and 8. The mid-week drop aligns with the migration window. The .co.uk URL mismapping (flagged 3/15) may also have contributed, though resolution was underway during W13.

NB CPC continued its decline to $4.81 (-10% WoW), now down 29% from the W6 peak of $6.82. This is the 7th consecutive week of NB CPC declines. Despite the lower CPC, NB CPA held flat at $187 because the CVR decline offset the cost savings. Brand CPA rose to $41 (+10% WoW) as the CVR reversion from W12's 8.54% peak erased the efficiency gain.

Spend fell to $24K, the lowest weekly spend in the 13-week trend and 32% below the 8-week average of $36K. The spend compression is now a combination of bid strategy efficiency (lower CPCs) and the migration disruption (lower click volume during the transition).

## Trend context

This is the 6th consecutive week of declining registrations (328 in W7 to 207 in W13, -37% cumulative). The W10-W12 stabilization range of 240-251 broke in W13, dropping to 207. However, the W13 break is likely migration-driven rather than a new structural decline. The daily pattern (strong Mon-Tue, weak Wed-Sat) is inconsistent with organic demand deterioration, which would show uniform weakness across all days.

NB CPC has declined 7 consecutive weeks ($6.82 to $4.81), the longest streak in AU's history. The bid strategies are consistently finding cheaper traffic. The question is whether CVR can recover once the URL migration settles, which would unlock the CPA improvement that the CPC decline has been building toward.

Brand CVR reverted from W12's 8.54% (an 8-week high) to 7.37%, which is closer to the W6-W11 average of ~7.5%. W12's Brand CVR spike was likely an outlier; W13's level is the more sustainable baseline.

Total CVR at 3.73% is the lowest since W5 (3.44%). If this is migration-related, it should recover in W14 once the new Polaris URLs are fully live and indexed.

## Relevant actions and events

1. Polaris URL migration completing (3/24-25): This is the primary suspect for the W13 CVR drop. During migration, some traffic likely landed on transitional or incorrectly mapped pages. The daily pattern (strong pre-migration, weak during/post-migration) is consistent with this explanation. Migration should be complete by W14.

2. .co.uk URL mismapping (flagged 3/15, resolution ongoing): Some AU keywords were redirecting to .co.uk URLs. This was being resolved during W13 but may have contributed to the CVR decline, particularly for affected NB keywords.

3. NB CPC decline streak (7 weeks): $6.82 to $4.81 (-29%). The Adobe bid strategies continue to compress costs. Once CVR normalizes post-migration, the lower CPC base should translate to improved CPA.

4. Brandon sync (3/23): Focus on outlier keyword analysis (top 20 by CPC/CPA), not broad removal. Lena's 3 priorities confirmed: (1) keyword CPC/CPA investigation, (2) keyword-to-product mapping, (3) Polaris migration. CPC caps are a "two-way door" until OCI.

5. Alexis sync (3/24): Two-campaign structure proposal (product-intent vs business-intent). Biweekly keyword review cadence agreed. MCS page replication from MX model discussed. Negative keywords for non-selling categories (perishables, alcohol) confirmed paused.

6. New acquisition promo sitelink live: "20% off first order, up to AU$50." This was live during W13 but any impact is masked by the migration disruption.

## Monthly projection

MTD through W13 (28/31 days): 944 regs, $115K spend.

Remaining 3 days (Mar 29-31): 1 weekend day (Sun), 2 weekdays (Mon, Tue).

W13 daily rates were distorted by the migration disruption. Using a blended W12-W13 approach, discounted for the migration settling period:
- Weekend day: ~20 regs (between W12's 28 avg and W13's 16 avg, reflecting partial recovery)
- Weekday: ~32 regs (W13 Mon-Tue averaged 52, but Wed-Fri averaged 24; post-migration weekdays should normalize to ~30-34)

Projected remaining regs: (1 x 20) + (2 x 32) = 84
Projected total regs: 944 + 84 = ~1,030
Projected remaining spend: 3 days x $3.4K/day = ~$10K
Projected total spend: $115K + $10K = ~$125K
Projected CPA: $121

vs. OP2 ($148K spend, 1,082 regs, $136 CPA): -15% spend, -5% regs, -11% CPA. The spend gap widened from W12's projection (-13% to -15%) due to the continued spend compression. Registration shortfall increased from -4% to -5%. CPA improvement deepened from $124 to $121.

The efficiency story remains intact: we're trading ~5% volume for 11% CPA improvement. The W13 dip is likely transient (migration-driven), not structural. If Polaris URLs stabilize in W14, April should start from a cleaner baseline.

## Flags

1. Registration decline streak extended to 6 weeks: 328 to 207 (-37% cumulative). The W10-W12 stabilization broke in W13. Frame as migration disruption overlaying the post-promo baseline, not a new structural decline. The daily pattern (strong Mon-Tue, weak Wed-Sat) supports a migration explanation.

2. AU is the second-worst WoW performer globally (-16%): Only DE (-46%, likely data lag) was worse. Most markets recovered in W13 (WW +4.3%, US +8%, CA +19%, UK +14%, JP +29%). The AU underperformance needs a clear attribution to the Polaris migration to avoid concern in WBR.

3. CVR at 8-week low (3.73%): Both Brand and NB CVR declined. If this persists in W14 after migration completes, it signals a deeper issue beyond URL disruption.

4. Spend at 13-week low ($24K): 32% below 8-week average. The bid strategies plus migration disruption are compressing spend faster than planned. March will come in ~$125K vs $148K OP2 (-15%). This is fine if CPA improves, but the underspend gap is widening.

5. Saturday anomaly (8 regs): This is the lowest single-day total in the available data. Could be migration-related (new URLs not yet indexed for weekend searchers) or noise. Monitor W14 weekend performance.

6. NB CPA stuck at $187: Despite 7 weeks of CPC declines (29% cumulative), NB CPA has not improved since W12 ($187 both weeks). The CVR decline is fully offsetting the CPC savings. NB CPA needs CVR recovery to break below $180.

AU registrations fell to 207 (-16% WoW) as the Polaris URL migration completed mid-week, likely disrupting conversion rates. CVR dropped -12% WoW across both Brand and NB, with the daily pattern showing strong Mon-Tue performance (53, 50 regs) before collapsing Wed-Sat (27, 22, 23, 8) during the migration window. NB CPC continued its 7-week decline to $4.81 (-29% from W6 peak), but the CVR drop offset cost savings, holding NB CPA flat at $187. March is projected at ~1,030 regs and $125K spend ($121 CPA) vs OP2 of 1,082 regs and $148K ($136 CPA), trading 5% volume for 11% CPA improvement. The W13 dip is likely transient; W14 should show recovery as the new URLs stabilize.
