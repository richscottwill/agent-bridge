# AU W12 Analysis Brief

## Registration summary

AU drove 240 registrations (flat WoW), with -10% spend WoW. CPA improved to $117 (-9% WoW). March is projected to end at ~$131K spend and ~1,060 registrations, at $124 CPA. (vs. OP2: -11% spend, -2% registrations)

## Why registrations changed

Registrations held flat despite a -10% spend reduction because Brand and NB moved in opposite directions. Brand recovered +8% (108 to 117) on +8% CVR (7.79% to 8.40%) with flat clicks, while NB declined -8% (133 to 123) on -8% clicks with flat CVR (2.79% to 2.80%). The net effect was zero WoW change in total registrations.

The NB click decline is the continuation of the bid strategy efficiency push. NB CPC fell another 3% WoW ($5.53 to $5.37), now down 21% from the W6 peak of $6.82. The bid strategies are shedding low-intent traffic, which is why clicks are falling but CVR is holding. NB CPA improved slightly ($198 to $192, -3%). Brand CPA dropped 11% ($43 to $38) on the CVR improvement.

The spend reduction (-10% WoW, $31K to $28K) is the steepest single-week drop in the 12-week trend. This is partially mechanical: the bid strategies are winning lower-CPC auctions, and partially intentional efficiency management.

## Trend context

This is the 5th consecutive week of declining registrations (328 in W7 to 240 in W12, -27% cumulative). The decline started when the Back to Biz site-wide promotion ended after W7. The post-promo normalization is now complete: W10-W12 registrations have stabilized in the 240-251 range, and CVR has held between 3.92% and 4.19% across those three weeks. This is the new baseline.

NB CPC has declined 6 consecutive weeks ($6.82 to $5.37), which is the clearest signal that the Adobe bid strategy transition is working as intended. The bid strategies are finding efficient traffic at lower price points. The trade-off is lower volume at better efficiency.

Brand has been volatile (94 to 152 range over 8 weeks) but W12's 117 is above the W5-W11 average of 106. The new acquisition promo sitelink (20% off first order, up to AU$50) went live before W12 and may be contributing to the Brand CVR improvement.

## Relevant actions and events

1. Adobe bid strategy transition (ongoing since W3): NB CPC down 21% from peak. The strategies are in learning/optimization phase and continue to compress costs. This is the primary driver of the spend decline and the improving CPA trajectory.

2. New acquisition promo sitelink live: "20% off first order, up to AU$50" updated before W12. Brand CVR jumped from 7.79% to 8.40%, which could reflect early promo impact through halo effect on conversion intent.

3. .co.uk URL mismapping flagged 3/15 (start of W12): Alexis flagged AU keywords redirecting to .co.uk URLs. If affected keywords were live during W12, this could explain the weak Thu/Fri performance (24 and 30 regs vs 41-45 Mon-Wed). Campaigns may have been partially paused or underperforming due to incorrect landing pages.

4. Polaris URL migration in progress: Lena confirmed full switch (no 50/50 split). Implementation ongoing with Alexis. Until complete, some traffic may land on suboptimal pages.

5. Spend anomaly: W12 spend of $28K is 25% below the 8-week average of $37K. This is the lowest weekly spend since W1 ($14K). The bid strategies are compressing spend faster than expected.

## Monthly projection

MTD through W12 (21/31 days): 732 regs, $91K spend.

Remaining 10 days (Mar 22-31): 6 weekdays, 4 weekend days. Using W12 daily rates (weekday ~35 regs, weekend ~28 regs), discounted slightly for the declining trend and potential .co.uk URL disruption:

- Projected remaining regs: (6 × 34) + (4 × 27) = 204 + 108 = 312
- Projected total regs: 732 + 312 = ~1,044
- Projected remaining spend: 10 days × $3.8K/day = $38K
- Projected total spend: $91K + $38K = ~$129K
- Projected CPA: $124

vs. OP2 ($148K spend, 1,082 regs, $136 CPA): -13% spend, -4% regs, -9% CPA. We'll undershoot OP2 on regs by ~40 but at significantly better efficiency. The CPA improvement ($124 vs $136 target) is the right trade-off given the bid strategy transition.

If the .co.uk URL issue is resolved quickly and Polaris migration completes, there's upside to ~1,070 regs (flat to OP2).

## Flags

1. 5-week registration decline streak: Stabilizing at 240-251 range, but the streak will draw attention in WBR. Frame as post-promo normalization at improved efficiency, not deterioration.

2. .co.uk URL mismapping: Flagged 3/15 by Alexis. Unknown how many keywords affected or how long they've been misdirected. This is a potential drag on W12 and W13 performance until resolved. Need status update from Alexis.

3. Spend running 25% below 8-week average: The bid strategies are compressing spend faster than volume. If this continues, March will come in well under OP2 spend ($129K vs $148K). This is fine if CPA improves, but worth flagging for budget reallocation discussions.

4. NB CPA still elevated at $192: Despite 6 weeks of CPC declines, NB CPA remains above the $140 program target. The bid strategies need more time to find the right volume/efficiency balance. CPA trajectory is correct (down from $198 LW) but the absolute level is high.

5. Thu/Fri weakness: 24 and 30 regs vs 41-45 Mon-Wed. Could be .co.uk URL issue impact, day-of-week noise, or both. Monitor W13 daily pattern.

## Suggested narrative angle

AU registrations stabilized at 240 (flat WoW) while CPA improved to $117 (-9% WoW), the best CPA since W1. The bid strategy transition continues to compress costs: NB CPC is down 21% over 6 weeks, and spend fell 10% WoW while holding volume flat. Brand recovered +8% on improved CVR, potentially reflecting early impact from the new acquisition promo sitelink. March is projected at ~1,044 regs and $129K spend ($124 CPA) vs OP2 of 1,082 regs and $148K ($136 CPA). We're trading ~4% volume for 9% CPA improvement, which is the right trade-off during the bid strategy learning phase.
