# ES W13 Analysis Brief

## Registration summary
645 regs (flat WoW), $38K spend (+17% WoW), $59 CPA (+17% WoW). Brand 364 (-8% WoW), NB 281 (+12% WoW). Projected: 2,900 regs, $171K spend vs OP2 2,395 regs, $127K spend (+21% regs, +35% spend).

## Why registrations changed
Flat headline masks a segment divergence. Brand fell -8% on CVR decline (-11%, 2.60% vs 2.92%) despite +4% clicks. NB grew +12% on +13% clicks (12,528 vs 11,094) with CVR flat (2.24% vs 2.25%). The NB click expansion offset the Brand decline. Spend rose +17% WoW while regs were flat, pushing CPA up +17%. Brand CPA jumped +22% ($35 vs $29), reversing W12's improvement. NB CPA rose +6% ($91 vs $86). The spend increase is buying NB clicks but not improving overall volume efficiency.

## Trend context
ES has been in a 623-711 range for 9 weeks. W13 at 645 is mid-range, essentially at the 8-week average of 649. W11's 711 remains the outlier high; the market has reverted to its baseline. Brand has been declining: 473 (W5) to 364 (W13), a 23% drop over 9 weeks. NB has been growing: 205 (W5) to 281 (W13), a 37% increase. The mix is shifting toward NB, similar to UK's pattern but from a lower base. CVR at 2.43% is the second-lowest in the 8-week window (after W10's 2.38%).

## Relevant actions and events
1. OCI E2E launched 2/26, now 4 weeks in. Revenue data exclusion added 3/23-3/31 for Generic and AMZ campaigns. Same pattern as FR and IT: bidding algorithm loses conversion feedback for the final week.
2. NB budget increases on 3/16: Generic $1.7K to $2K, AMZ $700 to $900, GA Top $100 to $150, GA $100 to $150. These are modest increases that partially explain the +13% NB click growth.
3. AGL (amazon.co.uk) continues causing CPC pressure on Brand terms. Brand CVR fell -11% WoW while clicks grew +4%, suggesting the competitor is inflating auction costs and degrading click quality. Brand CPA at $35 is still efficient but trending up from W12's $29.
4. Cross-market context: ES is the only EU5 market that was flat WoW. UK (+14%), IT (+8%), FR (+4%) all recovered from W12 softness. ES did not participate in the recovery.

## YoY assessment
+12% YoY regs (645 vs 577). +84% YoY spend ($38K vs $21K). Brand +16% YoY, NB +7% YoY. NB CPA +82% YoY ($91 vs $50). The spend-to-reg ratio is poor: spending 84% more for 12% more regs. NB CPA has nearly doubled YoY. LY W13 was +5% WoW; TY's flat performance is below the seasonal pattern, suggesting the underlying trajectory is softer than last year.

## Monthly projection
Projected 2,900 regs vs OP2 2,395 (+21%). Projected $171K spend vs OP2 $127K (+35%). Remaining 3 days: 2 weekdays, 1 weekend day. W13 daily rates: weekday ~109, weekend ~50. Remaining: 2(109) + 1(50) = 268. MTD 2,638 + 268 = 2,906, rounded to 2,900. Spend: MTD $155K + ~$16K remaining = $171K. CPA: $59. ES remains the strongest EU5 market vs OP2 on regs (+21%) but the spend overshoot has widened significantly from +27% (W12) to +35%. This is the largest spend overshoot in EU5.

## Flags
- Spend overshoot critical: +35% vs OP2 spend, up from +27% at W12. The reg beat (+21%) does not justify the spend premium. Efficiency is deteriorating.
- Brand erosion: 364 Brand regs is the lowest in the 9-week window. The 23% decline from W5 (473) is structural, not seasonal. AGL CPC pressure is the likely driver.
- AGL coordination: amazon.co.uk competing on ES Brand terms remains an internal Amazon issue. The Brand CVR decline (-11% WoW) and CPA increase (+22%) suggest the pressure is intensifying.
- CVR compression: 2.43% blended CVR is near the 8-week low. Both Brand (-11%) and NB (flat) CVR are weak.
- OCI data exclusion: Revenue data excluded 3/23-3/31 for Generic and AMZ. Bidding is unoptimized for the final week.

## Suggested narrative angle
ES was flat WoW as NB growth (+12%) offset Brand decline (-8%). Spend rose +17% on NB click expansion, pushing CPA to $59 (+17%). Brand continues its multi-week erosion under AGL pressure (364 regs, 9-week low). Monthly projection: +21% regs vs OP2 on +35% spend. The spend overshoot is the largest in EU5 and widening. AGL coordination and OCI maturation are the two levers.
