# ES W12 Analysis Brief

## Registration summary
657 regs (-8% WoW), $33K spend (-19% WoW), $50 CPA (-12% WoW). Brand 405 (-8% WoW), NB 252 (-9% WoW). Projected: 2,850 regs, $161K spend vs OP2 2,395 regs, $127K spend (+19% regs, +27% spend).

## Why registrations changed
Clicks drove the decline (-10% WoW) while CVR was flat (+2%). Brand clicks fell -13% (13,472 vs 15,429) but Brand CVR improved +6% (3.01% vs 2.84%), partially offsetting the click loss. NB clicks fell -7% with CVR flat (-2%). The click decline across both segments suggests reduced auction participation or impression share loss rather than a demand-side issue. Spend dropped -19%, more than regs (-8%), because CPA improved: Brand $28 vs $34 (-19%), NB $85 vs $92 (-7%).

## Trend context
ES has been in a narrow 625-714 range for 8 weeks. W12 at 657 is mid-range. W11 (714) was the 8-week high; W12's -8% is a reversion toward the mean (659 avg). The trend is flat with no clear direction. CPA has been the volatile metric: $49 (W5) → $68 (W10) → $50 (W12). The CPA spike in W9-W10 ($63-$68) has now fully corrected.

## Relevant actions and events
1. OCI E2E launched 2/26. Three weeks in, no measurable lift yet. Full impact projected Jul 2026.
2. amazon.co.uk (Amazon Global Logistics) causing CPC pressure on ES Brand terms. +41% CPC observed W8-W10 (2/13-3/4). The Brand click decline (-13% WoW) may reflect reduced competitiveness in the auction as CPC pressure persists. However, Brand CPA actually improved (-19%), suggesting the algorithm is pulling back on expensive clicks.
3. Cross-market pattern: ES's -8% decline is consistent with the EU5-wide softness (4 of 5 markets down). ES's decline is moderate, between FR (-3%) and IT (-12%).
4. LY W12 was -12% WoW. TY's -8% is better than the seasonal pattern, suggesting the underlying trajectory is healthier than last year.

## YoY assessment
+20% YoY regs (657 vs 548). +42% YoY spend ($33K vs $23K). Brand +38% YoY, NB -1% YoY. NB CPA +42% YoY ($85 vs $60). Brand is driving all the YoY growth. NB is flat on regs and significantly more expensive. The NB efficiency gap is a concern, though OCI maturation should help.

## Monthly projection
Projected 2,850 regs vs OP2 2,395 (+19%). Projected $161K spend vs OP2 $127K (+27%). Remaining 10 days: 6 weekdays, 4 weekend days. Used W12 daily rates (weekday ~110, weekend ~46) with flat seasonal assumption. LY W13 was +5% WoW, but OCI E2E learning phase offsets any seasonal uptick. The ingester's 2,951 linear projection overstates because it doesn't account for the heavy weekend discount in ES (46-47 regs/day vs 94-129 weekday). ES is the strongest EU5 market vs OP2 on regs (+19%) but also the biggest overspender (+27%). The overspend is funding Brand growth (+38% YoY) that's driving the reg beat.

## Flags
- Spend overshoot: +28% vs OP2 is the largest in EU5. ES is buying growth aggressively. Sustainable only if the reg beat holds.
- AGL CPC pressure: amazon.co.uk on ES Brand terms is an internal Amazon entity competing with Amazon Business. This is a coordination issue, not a competitive threat. Escalation path exists.
- NB stagnation: NB regs are flat YoY (-1%) at +42% higher CPA. NB is not growing in ES. Brand is carrying the market.
- No data lag issues. Daily breakdown shows normal pattern with Sun/Sat as lows (46-47) and Mon-Fri as highs (94-129).

## Suggested narrative angle
ES declined -8% WoW on click compression across both segments, but CPA improved to an 8-week low of $50 (-12% WoW). AGL CPC pressure on Brand terms persists. Monthly projection: +23% regs vs OP2 on +28% spend. Brand is the growth engine; NB is flat YoY and needs OCI maturation.
