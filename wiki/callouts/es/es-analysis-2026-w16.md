# ES W16 Analysis Brief

## Registration summary
613 regs (+3% WoW), $38,094 spend (-5.5% WoW), $62.14 CPA (-8.3% WoW). Brand 364 (+3% WoW), NB 249 (+3% WoW). W15's +26% rebound held and extended — ES is the only EU5 market where spend actually fell while volume grew.

## Why registrations changed
Pure efficiency gain, not volume expansion. Clicks fell a few percent (24,763) and impressions softened, but blended CVR jumped to 2.48% (+15% WoW), lifting regs on lower spend. Brand CVR recovered ~6% and NB CVR ~8% after W15's post-Easter click surge — W15 bought traffic, W16 converted it. Brand and NB moved in lockstep (+3% each), and both segments cut CPA: Brand $40 (-9% WoW, vs $43), NB $95 (-8% WoW, vs $104). This is the cleanest conversion-led story in EU5 — volume flat to down, efficiency up, costs down, regs up.

## Trend context
ES extended the W15 rebound that was called out as needing validation. 8-week trend: W10 480 → W11 500 → W12 470 → W13 473 → W14 473 (Easter -21.4%) → W15 595 → W16 613. The W15 jump was real; W16 confirms a step-change from the 470-500 baseline to a 600+ range. ie%CCP 59.1% vs 64.4% LW (-5pp WoW) — ES is ceding some auction share, consistent with the click softness, but converting what it gets at higher efficiency. W16 is a clean seasonality week (next holiday May 1 Día del Trabajador, W18).

## Relevant actions and events
- W15 callout flagged "ES click sustainability, +35% Brand clicks needs validation" as the W16 watch item. W16 answer: click volume did not sustain (slight decline), but CVR recovery more than offset it. Validated.
- OCI E2E now ~7 weeks in. The CVR recovery across both Brand and NB in a single week is consistent with bidding algorithm maturation finding better-converting traffic as revenue signal stabilizes.
- ie%CCP dropped 5pp WoW (64.4% → 59.1%). Worth watching — suggests ES is getting less aggressive in the auction. Paired with CPA improvement, this reads as intentional discipline rather than a competitive loss.
- AGL CPC pressure on Brand terms: Brand CPA fell to $40 (-9% WoW) despite the ongoing AGL dynamic. Brand CVR recovery was the unlock.

## YoY assessment
+35% regs (613 vs 453 LY), +52% spend, +12% CPA ($62 vs $55 LY). This is the mildest CPA deterioration in EU5 — FR and IT are running materially worse YoY. Brand regs +50% YoY at $40 vs $28 LY (+42% Brand CPA worsening YoY, driven by AGL pressure and a higher mix). NB regs +18% YoY at $95 vs $87 LY (+10% NB CPA worsening). CVR -1% YoY. The YoY CPA gap narrowed this week vs recent trend — W16 efficiency gain pulled ES closer to LY performance. Structural story intact: OCI E2E plus AGL pressure are the known drivers; tactical execution is improving within those constraints.

## Monthly projection
Projected 2,492 regs, $158,000 spend, $63 CPA for April. vs OP2 1,964 regs / $108K / $55 CPA: +27% regs, +46% spend, +14% CPA. 18/30 days elapsed through W16. Remaining 12 days: 9 weekdays, 3 weekend days. W16 daily rates (weekday ~102, weekend ~55): 9(102) + 3(55) = 918 + 165 = ~1,083. MTD ~1,409 + 1,083 = ~2,492. Spend: MTD ~$87K + ~$71K remaining at current run-rate = ~$158K. Efficiency is improving late in the month — if W16 CPA holds, April CPA lands $63 vs the MTD $62 trajectory. Strong OP2 reg beat on elevated but improving spend.

## Recommended W17 spend
$38,000-$40,000 — hold or lean in slightly. W16 spent $38,094 at $62 CPA (-8% WoW). ES is the cleanest efficiency story in EU5 this week: CVR recovery is real, spend can stay at current levels without CPA deterioration. Slight lean-in (to $40K) is defensible given the +27% OP2 reg beat and improving CPA trajectory. Avoid spend cuts — nothing in the data supports pulling back.

## Flags
- ie%CCP dropped 5pp WoW (64.4% → 59.1%). Not a red flag given CPA improvement, but monitor for auction-share erosion if the pattern continues.
- Clicks and impressions softened (-few%) while regs grew. Entirely CVR-driven. If CVR reverts in W17, regs will follow down — efficiency gains need a second week of confirmation.
- Brand CPA YoY +42% remains the structural sore spot. The WoW improvement ($43 → $40) is within noise; AGL pressure unchanged.
- Spend overshoot vs OP2 at +46% is the largest deviation in the ES forecast, but the reg beat (+27%) is proportionally strong enough to defend.

## Suggested narrative angle
ES extended the W15 rebound and converted it into pure efficiency: +3% regs on -5.5% spend, $62 CPA (-8% WoW). CVR recovery across Brand (+6%) and NB (+8%) did the work — clicks actually fell. This is EU5's cleanest conversion-led story and the mildest YoY CPA deterioration (+12%, vs FR/IT running worse). April tracking +27% regs vs OP2 on +46% spend. W17 watch: whether CVR sustains a second week or reverts to the 2.1-2.3% baseline.
