# MX W12 Analysis Brief

## Registration summary

MX drove 330 registrations (-16% WoW), with -10% spend WoW, and 90% ie%CCP. CPA came in at $61 (+7% WoW). March is projected to end at ~$92K spend and ~1,430 registrations, at $64 CPA. (vs. OP2: +63% spend, +66% registrations)

## Why registrations changed

The -16% WoW decline was CVR-driven (-11% WoW) more than click-driven (-6% WoW), and it hit both segments. Brand CVR fell from 6.79% to 6.15% (-9%), and NB CVR fell from 1.57% to 1.38% (-12%). Clicks declined modestly on both sides (Brand -7%, NB -6%).

W11's performance was elevated. NB CVR of 1.57% was flagged in the W11 callout as above the normal 1.1-1.3% range, likely boosted by the negative keyword restructuring that improved query routing. W12's NB CVR of 1.38% is still above the 10-week average of ~1.2%, suggesting the negative keyword work created a durable improvement, but the initial spike has normalized.

Brand CVR of 6.15% is within the normal 4-6% range and above the 12-week average of ~5%. The -9% WoW decline is a reversion from W11's 6.79%, which was itself elevated.

The Benito Juarez holiday (Mar 17, Monday of W12) contributed to the volume decline. The daily breakdown shows Monday at 44 regs vs Thursday at 69, consistent with a holiday suppression effect on the first day of the week. Applying the principles' 15-25% holiday impact estimate to one day of seven: ~3-5% of the weekly decline is attributable to the holiday.

## Trend context

W12's 330 regs sits between W10 (298) and W11 (393) in the 12-week trend. The pattern over the last 5 weeks (W8-W12) shows alternating spikes and dips: 368, 264, 298, 393, 330. The spikes (W8, W11) correlate with CVR jumps, while the dips (W9, W12) show CVR normalization. The underlying trend is upward: the "dip" weeks are getting higher (264 to 298 to 330).

NB regs of 122 are flagged as 20% above the recent average of 102. Despite the WoW decline, NB is performing above its baseline, which supports the thesis that negative keyword restructuring created a structural improvement in query routing.

NB CPC declined slightly WoW ($1.90 to $1.81, -5%), breaking a 5-week upward trend. This could be noise or the bid strategies finding more efficient placements after the negative keyword changes improved campaign targeting.

ie%CCP improved from 85% to 90%, moving toward the 100% target. The improvement came from the spend reduction (-10% WoW) more than registration gains. At current trajectory, ie%CCP should reach 95-100% by month-end as the MTD ratio continues to improve.

## Relevant actions and events

1. Negative keyword restructuring (W11): Added negatives to prevent Brand Phrase terms from surfacing in Brand Exact or NB, and Generic terms from surfacing in Product/Vertical campaigns. W12 is the first full week post-implementation. NB CVR normalized from the initial spike but remains above the pre-change baseline (1.38% vs ~1.2% avg), confirming a durable routing improvement.

2. Benito Juarez holiday (Mar 17): Monday of W12. Suppressed registrations on that day. Daily data shows Mon at 44 regs, below the weekday average of 53. Estimated 10-15 regs lost to the holiday.

3. Brand coverage scaling: Brand spend continues at ~$4.2K/wk (vs ~$470/wk LY). The 10x increase in Brand investment is the primary driver of the +271% YoY Brand registration growth.

4. ie%CCP at 90%: Up from 85% LW. The 100% target is within reach for March given the MTD trajectory. NB spend decisions should continue to be framed against this constraint.

## YoY assessment

YoY performance remains strong: +90% regs (330 vs 174) on -35% spend ($20K vs $31K). CPA improved from $178 to $61 (-66% YoY).

Brand drove the bulk of the growth: +271% YoY (208 vs 56 LY). This is entirely from increased Brand coverage and investment. NB grew +3% YoY (122 vs 118), modest but achieved on -50% CPA ($131 vs $260) and significantly less spend. The NB efficiency story is the structural narrative: -27% CPC and +42% CVR YoY from bid strategy maturity and negative keyword work.

The WoW pattern was similar both years: TY -16% vs LY -12%. Both years had the Benito Juarez holiday in W12, so the seasonal pattern is consistent. The difference is that TY's baseline is 90% higher on 35% less spend.

LY W13-W16 averaged 176 regs/week. If TY maintains the ~90% YoY lift, W13 should land around 330-350 regs, consistent with the current baseline.

## Monthly projection

MTD through W12 (21/31 days): 1,021 regs, $63K spend.

Remaining 10 days (Mar 22-31): 6 weekdays, 4 weekend days. Using W12 daily rates (weekday ~53 regs, weekend ~32 regs), with a slight discount for the declining trend from W11's spike:

- Projected remaining regs: (6 × 50) + (4 × 30) = 300 + 120 = 420
- Projected total regs: 1,021 + 420 = ~1,441
- Projected remaining spend: (6 × $3.2K) + (4 × $2K) = $19.2K + $8K = $27K
- Projected total spend: $63K + $27K = ~$90K
- Projected CPA: $63
- Projected ie%CCP: ~95% (improving from 90% as MTD ratio stabilizes)

vs. OP2 ($57K spend, 859 regs, $66 CPA): +59% spend, +68% regs, -5% CPA. MX will significantly exceed OP2 on registrations while beating the CPA target. The overspend vs OP2 is justified by the ie%CCP trajectory toward 100%.

The ingester's linear estimate of 1,492 regs is slightly aggressive. The W11 spike inflates the daily run rate. Using W12's normalized rates with weekday/weekend weighting gives ~1,441, which accounts for the post-spike normalization.

## Flags

1. W11 to W12 normalization is expected, not concerning: The -16% WoW decline reverses W11's +33% spike. The underlying trend (dip weeks getting higher: 264 to 298 to 330) is positive.

2. NB CVR settling post-negative keywords: 1.57% (W11) to 1.38% (W12). Still above the 1.1-1.3% pre-change range. Monitor W13 to confirm the new baseline is ~1.3-1.4%.

3. ie%CCP at 90%, improving: On track to reach 95-100% by month-end. No NB budget adjustments needed unless ie%CCP stalls.

4. NB CPA at $131: High in absolute terms but -50% YoY and within the efficiency framework. CCP guidance is $30 NB, so each NB registration generates $30 CCP against $131 cost. The ie%CCP math works because Brand CCP ($80) at $20 CPA carries the blended ratio.

5. Benito Juarez impact was modest: ~10-15 regs lost, consistent with a single-day holiday. No structural concern.

## Suggested narrative angle

MX registrations normalized to 330 (-16% WoW) after W11's negative-keyword-driven spike, with ie%CCP improving to 90%. The decline was CVR-driven as both Brand and NB conversion rates reverted from elevated W11 levels, compounded by the Benito Juarez holiday on Monday. YoY performance remains strong at +90% regs on -35% spend. NB CVR settled at 1.38%, still above the pre-restructuring baseline of ~1.2%, confirming a durable improvement from the W11 negative keyword work. March is projected at ~1,441 regs and $90K spend ($63 CPA) vs OP2 of 859 regs and $57K ($66 CPA). ie%CCP is on track to reach 95-100% by month-end.
