# US W13 Analysis Brief

## Registration summary
8,796 regs, +8% WoW. Spend $583K (-3% WoW). CPA $66 (-9% WoW). Brand 2,758 (+9% WoW). NB 6,038 (+7% WoW). Overall CVR 5.26% (+4% WoW). March projection: 35,400 regs at $2.66M spend ($75 CPA) vs OP2 30,517 regs at $2.74M ($90 CPA). +16% regs, -3% spend vs OP2.

## Why registrations changed
Broad-based recovery. Both CVR (+4% WoW) and clicks (+3% WoW) contributed. This is the first week since W6 where both levers moved in the same direction.

Brand and NB both improved:
- Brand regs +9% WoW. Clicks surged +7% (66.9K vs 62.8K) and CVR ticked up +2% (4.12% vs 4.03%). Brand CPA dropped to $62 from $71 (-13% WoW), the lowest since W5. The click recovery suggests Walmart IS pressure eased for a second consecutive week. Brand CPA at $62 is notable: two weeks below $72 after months in the $65-77 range.
- NB regs +7% WoW. CVR jumped +6% (6.02% vs 5.67%), a 12-week high matching the W2 level. Clicks were essentially flat (+1%). NB CPA fell to $68 from $74 (-8%). NB CPC continued its decline to $4.12, the lowest in the 12-week trend, confirming OCI is still compressing costs.

Net: CVR-led week with a click tailwind. NB CVR at 6.02% is the headline metric: it signals OCI bid optimization has fully recovered from the W6-W8 trough.

## Trend context
W13 is the strongest week since W6 (8,873 regs) and breaks the 8,000-8,300 range that held for W9-W12:
- W5-W6: ~8,850 baseline
- W7: sharp drop to 7,747 (CVR collapsed, spend spiked to $718K)
- W8-W12: gradual recovery, 7,554-8,322 range
- W13: 8,796, approaching the pre-trough baseline

CPA has now declined 6 consecutive weeks: $93 (W7) → $84 → $82 → $79 → $73 → $66. This is the longest CPA decline streak in the available data. The $66 is the best CPA since W3 ($67).

CVR at 5.26% is the highest since W6 (4.93%) and approaching the W1-W5 peak range (5.48-6.37%). NB CVR at 6.02% specifically has returned to the W2-W5 level after spending W6-W12 in the 5.01-5.67% range.

Spend at $583K is the lowest since W5 ($607K) and continues the normalization from the W6-W7 spike ($701-718K). More regs on less spend is the efficiency story.

## Relevant actions and events
- OCI at 100% on NB continues to drive structural YoY NB gains. NB CVR at 6.02% (12-week high) and NB CPC at $4.12 (12-week low) are both OCI outcomes. The bid optimization is simultaneously finding cheaper clicks and better-converting traffic.
- Walmart Brand pressure potentially easing. Brand CPA at $62 is the second consecutive week below $72 (W12 was $71, but W13's $62 is a sharper drop). Brand clicks +7% WoW suggests Walmart may have pulled back IS, allowing more impressions at lower CPCs. Two weeks is suggestive but not conclusive.
- ps_kw parameter appended account-level on 3/18. This tracking change went live during W13. No expected performance impact, but worth monitoring for any URL-related disruption.
- No US holidays in W13. Clean performance read with no suppression factors.

- Brand: +22% YoY. Growth despite Walmart competitive pressure.
- WoW pattern: TY +8% vs LY +4%. Both years saw a W13 recovery from W12, but TY's bounce is stronger.

LY W14-W17 showed 4,747-5,752 regs at $102-112 CPA. TY is running at 1.7x LY volume at 0.6x LY CPA. The structural gap remains OCI.

## Flags
- CPA streak at 6 weeks: $93 → $66. The longest consecutive CPA decline in the data. At some point this normalizes. If Walmart ramps IS back up, Brand CPA could snap from $62 to $75+ in a single week and break the streak.
- NB CVR at 12-week high (6.02%): This is the strongest signal that OCI optimization has matured. If this holds in W14, it confirms a new NB efficiency baseline rather than a one-week spike.
- Brand CPA at $62: Two consecutive weeks of Brand CPA relief ($71, $62). If Walmart IS data confirms a pullback, this could be a structural shift. If not, expect reversion to $70+ in W14.
- Spend compression: $583K is -17% from the W7 peak ($718K). The team is delivering more regs on less spend, but continued spend decline could eventually cap volume growth.
- Saturday strength: 1,199 regs on Saturday is unusually high (W12 Saturday was 832). Check if this is a data artifact or genuine weekend demand shift.

Brand CPA dropped to $62, potentially reflecting eased Walmart pressure. March tracking +16% regs vs OP2 at -3% spend. YoY: +78% regs on +4% spend.
