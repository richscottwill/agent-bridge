# US W16 Analysis Brief

## Registration summary
9,109 regs (-3% WoW from W15's 9,386). Spend $679K (flat, +0.1%). CPA $74.54 (+3% WoW). CVR 5.40% (-2%). A modest step down from the W14-W15 peak, but well above the W10-W13 baseline (7,526–8,574). Third-strongest week of 2026 behind W14 (9,522) and W15 (9,386). No US holidays — Apr 15 (Tax Day) is not federal and has no historical impact. This is a clean read.

## Why registrations changed
The dip is entirely NB-driven. A Brand/NB composition shift, not a top-line collapse:

- **NB: 6,068 regs (-10% WoW from 6,755).** NB clicks -2% (102,964 vs 105,900), NB CVR softened -8% (5.89% vs 6.38%), and NB CPA rose +10% to $81.40 from $73.68. NB spend essentially flat (-1%). The driver is CVR reverting off the W14-W15 high (6.31-6.38%) toward the pre-W14 baseline (5.55-5.95%). This is not a click-volume problem — NB traffic is converting less well.
- **Brand: 3,041 regs (+16% WoW from 2,631), the strongest Brand week in the 12-week trend.** Brand clicks +2%, Brand CVR +13% (4.64% vs 4.10%, a 12-week high), Brand CPA dropped -11% to $60.85 (lowest in the dataset). Brand spend +3%.

Net mechanics: Brand added 410 regs WoW; NB lost 687. That's a -277 reg net, which is the -3% headline exactly. CVR diverged sharply by type — Brand CVR +13%, NB CVR -8% — unusual and warrants campaign-level diagnosis.

## Trend context
Regs trend W9-W16: 7,996 → 7,526 → 8,275 → 8,124 → 8,574 → 9,522 → 9,386 → 9,109. W16 continues the step-up from the W10-W13 low band but softens from the W14 peak. The W14-W15 level (~9,450) does not appear to be a new floor — W16 says the step-change was real but not yet stable.

CPA trend W11-W16: $78 → $74 → $68 → $70 → $72 → $75. Three consecutive weeks of small CPA creep from the W13 trough ($68). Not alarming in absolute terms ($75 is still below every week in W6-W11), but the direction reversed after a 6-week improvement streak.

Brand CVR at 4.64% is a clear high; NB CVR at 5.89% is mid-range. The question for W17 is whether NB CVR continues drifting back toward 5.5% (erasing the W14 gains) or stabilizes here.

## Relevant actions and events
- **OCI remains at 100% on NB.** NB CPC rose to $4.80 from $4.70 (+2%) — first uptick in several weeks. Combined with softer NB CVR, this is the first sign that OCI's efficiency gains may be bumping against a ceiling. Not a reversal, but the compression has paused.
- **Walmart Brand IS pressure continues to ease.** Brand clicks +2% WoW, Brand CPC flat at $2.82, Brand CVR at a 12-week high. Brand CPA at $61 is the lowest in the data and the 4th consecutive week below $70 (W13 $65 → W14 $64 → W15 $69 → W16 $61). The "Walmart pullback" thesis from W13 is holding.
- **No US holidays in W16.** Apr 15 (Tax Day) is not a federal holiday and has no historical impact. Next US holiday is Memorial Day May 25.
- **W15 callout watch items revisited:**
  - "CVR uplift holding" — partially: Brand CVR extended gains, NB CVR reverted.
  - "NB click volume flat for 2 weeks" — now -2% WoW, monitoring required.
  - "Identify which NB campaigns drove W14 CVR step-change" — still open; W16 data reinforces the urgency since the gain wasn't stable.
- **Impressions +4% WoW** (1,098K vs 1,053K) with clicks -1%, so CTR fell -5% to 15.4%. Mostly NB-side. Auction dynamics shifted slightly unfavorably on NB.

## YoY assessment
W16 2026 vs W16 2025: regs +92% (9,109 vs 4,735), spend +28% ($679K vs $530K), CPA -33% ($75 vs $112), CVR +112% (5.40% vs 2.55%). NB is the structural story: NB regs +135% YoY (6,068 vs 2,582), NB CPA -43% ($81 vs $143), NB CVR +195% (5.89% vs 2.00%). Brand regs +41% YoY (3,041 vs 2,153) at $61 vs $75 LY (-19%).

Impressions -56% YoY (1.10M vs 2.49M) — structural. Auction supply contracted materially YoY, yet we're delivering nearly 2x the registrations on that reduced inventory. The YoY WoW pattern: TY W15→W16 was -3%; LY W15→W16 was -5% (4,985 → 4,735). Both years softened; TY's is less severe.

## Recommended W17 spend
**$680K — hold flat.**

Rationale: US strategy is balanced with OCI mature and Walmart IS managed. W16 shows efficiency is still strong (CPA $75, below every week in W7-W11) but NB CVR softened off the peak. Pushing more spend into a softer NB CVR environment would worsen CPA; pulling back gives up Brand momentum. Hold spend flat, let Brand continue compounding, and diagnose NB before adjusting. At $680K × 2 remaining weeks (W17, W18 partial) + MTD through W16, April spend lands consistent with my $2.89M projection.

## Flags
- **NB CVR reversion.** 5.89% in W16 vs 6.38% W15 and 6.31% W14. W14-W15 was a genuine step-up from the W7-W13 baseline (5.0-5.95%), but W16 shows it wasn't stable. Needs campaign-level breakdown before W17 to identify whether specific campaigns regressed or the softening was broad.
- **NB CPA $81 — first time above $80 since W9** ($81.5). Three-week creep: $69 (W13) → $72 (W14) → $74 (W15) → $81 (W16). The W15→W16 jump is the sharpest of the sequence.
- **Brand CPA at $61 is a new dataset low.** Combined with Brand CVR 12-week high and Brand regs 12-week high, Brand performance is unambiguously strong. Risk: if Walmart IS returns, Brand could snap back to $70+ in a single week.
- **Impressions +4% WoW with clicks -1%** — CTR fell -5%. Not a major signal week-over-week but worth tracking if it extends.
- **Best day Thu 4/16 (1,523 regs), worst day Mon 4/13 (1,009).** Monday was unusually soft; spend was also low ($56K vs weekday avg $107K), suggesting budget pacing rather than demand. Check if any campaign paused.

## Suggested narrative angle
W16 reads as a NB-driven soften inside a still-strong week, not a broad reversal. 9,109 regs is the third-best week of 2026; Brand performance hit new dataset highs (CPA $61, CVR 4.64%, regs 3,041 all bests). The NB CVR revert from W14-W15's 6.3% back to 5.9% tells us that uplift wasn't a new baseline — it was a good two weeks. April is tracking +25% regs / +2% spend vs OP2, and the YoY story compounds: +92% regs on +28% spend, NB CPA -43% YoY. The W17 watch item is whether NB CVR stabilizes at ~5.9% (our new baseline since OCI maturity) or drifts further toward the pre-W14 5.5% floor.
