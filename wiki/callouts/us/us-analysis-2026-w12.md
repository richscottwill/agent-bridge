# US W12 Analysis Brief

## Registration summary
8,322 regs, flat WoW. Spend $599K (-7% WoW). CPA $72 (-7% WoW). Brand 2,645 (+5% WoW). NB 5,677 (-2% WoW). Overall CVR 5.13% (+4% WoW). March projection: 34,400 regs at $2.65M spend ($77 CPA) vs OP2 30.5K regs at $2.7M ($89 CPA). +13% regs, -3% spend vs OP2.

## Why registrations changed
Flat regs mask a CVR-vs-volume divergence. CVR improved +4% WoW (5.13% vs 4.93%), but total clicks fell -4% (162K vs 169K). The CVR gain absorbed the traffic loss.

Brand and NB diverged:
- Brand regs +5% WoW. CVR jumped +8% (4.21% vs 3.90%) despite -3% clicks. Brand CPA dropped to $68 from $75, the lowest since W5. This is a CVR-driven recovery, likely reflecting a Walmart pullback week. Walmart IS has ranged 37-55% this quarter; a dip toward the low end would explain both the click decline (fewer auction losses) and CVR improvement (less ad clutter).
- NB regs -2% WoW. CVR improved +2% (5.70% vs 5.57%) but -5% click decline outweighed it. NB CPA still fell to $74 from $79. NB CPC dropped to $4.22, the lowest in 12 weeks, suggesting OCI bid optimization is tightening spend without sacrificing conversion quality.

Net: CVR-driven week. Both segments converted better, but traffic pulled back enough to cap NB volume.

## Trend context
W12 sits in a recovery band after the W7 trough (7,754 regs, 4.49% CVR, $93 CPA). The trajectory:
- W5-W6: ~8,850 baseline
- W7: sharp drop to 7,754 (CVR collapsed, spend spiked to $718K)
- W8-W10: gradual recovery, 7,568-8,047 range
- W11-W12: stabilized at ~8,330, CVR back above 5%

CPA has declined 5 consecutive weeks: $93 (W7) → $84 → $81 → $79 → $77 → $72. Best CPA since W5 ($69). This is not noise; it's a sustained efficiency trajectory driven by OCI NB optimization and spend discipline.

CVR recovered from 4.49% (W7) to 5.13%, approaching the W1-W5 peak range (5.48-6.37%). Spend normalized from the W6-W7 spike ($701-718K) back to $599K.

## Relevant actions and events
- OCI at 100% on NB continues to drive the structural YoY NB gains. NB CVR at 5.70% reflects OCI bid optimization working as intended.
- Walmart Brand IS in the 37-55% range is the persistent Brand CPA headwind. W12 Brand CPA at $68 is a relief from $75 LW, but still elevated vs the ~$40 pre-Walmart baseline. The improvement likely reflects a Walmart pullback week rather than a structural fix.
- Polaris LP rollout in progress. The +8% Brand CVR WoW could partially reflect early Polaris impact, but one week is insufficient to attribute.
- Spend at $599K is the lowest since W5 ($607K). Bid cap discipline on Brand is working as intended.

## YoY assessment
+76% regs YoY (8,322 vs 4,737). Spend only +9% ($599K vs $548K).
- NB: +118% YoY. NB CPA dropped 47% ($74 vs $138). This is OCI. NB transformed from an expensive, low-volume channel into the primary growth engine.
- Brand: +24% YoY. Solid growth despite Walmart pressure driving Brand CPA from ~$40 to $65-77.
- WoW pattern: TY flat vs LY -2%. No seasonal divergence.

LY W13-W16 showed 4,748-5,067 regs at $108-113 CPA. TY is running at 1.7x LY volume at 0.65x LY CPA. The structural gap is OCI.

## Flags
- Brand CPA volatility: $68 this week vs $75 last week. Walmart IS fluctuations drive 10-15% Brand CPA swings WoW. The $68 is encouraging but not a new baseline until confirmed over 2-3 weeks.
- NB click decline: -5% WoW on NB clicks (99.5K vs 104.4K). Two consecutive weeks of NB click decline. If this continues, CVR gains alone won't sustain NB volume.
- Thursday-Saturday weakness: Thu 1,183, Fri 1,024, Sat 832. The back half of the week was notably weaker. Check if this signals W13 softness or is a one-week pattern.
- CPA streak at risk: 5 weeks of consecutive CPA improvement. If Walmart ramps IS back toward 55% in W13, Brand CPA could snap back to $75+ and break the streak.
