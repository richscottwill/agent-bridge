# JP W16 Analysis Brief

## Registration summary
544 regs, -7% WoW. Spend $35,538 (-12% WoW). CPA $65 (-6% WoW). Brand 542 (-6% WoW), NB 2 (noise). Second consecutive week of modest volume reversion from the W14 fiscal year-end peak (598), settling into a post-FY baseline in the 540-580 range that sits 20-25 regs above the pre-FY W10-W11 band. CPA efficiency continues to compound.

## Why registrations changed
The -7% reg decline is CVR-driven, not volume-driven. Total clicks fell only -1.5% WoW (26,721 vs 27,116) and Brand clicks were essentially flat (-0.6%). CVR compressed: blended 2.04% vs 2.15% (-5%), Brand 2.09% vs 2.22% (-6%). Regs moved proportionally with CVR while click volume held — typical post-peak softening as the W14 FY-end demand spike cleared.

The efficiency story extends from W15: Brand CPC dropped another -10% WoW ($1.34 vs $1.48), a second consecutive week of double-digit CPC decline. Spend fell -12% on near-flat Brand click volume, driven almost entirely by cheaper auction prices — not budget pulls. Brand CPA improved to $64 (-4% WoW), the lowest single-week Brand CPA since W5 ($33 during the pre-MHLW period, which is not a fair comparison; excluding MHLW-era weeks, this is the lowest of 2026). NB is effectively dormant at 2 regs on 791 clicks (0.25% CVR); the APAC MCC access blocker remains unresolved and NB is not a meaningful lever.

## Trend context
8-week regs trend: W9: 403 → W10: 525 → W11: 514 → W12: 423 → W13: 544 → W14: 598 → W15: 583 → W16: 544.

W14 was the JP fiscal year-end peak (Mar 29 – Apr 4, straddling Mar 31). The W15 (-2%) and W16 (-7%) declines are reversion to a new post-FY baseline, not a concerning reversal. That baseline (540-580 range) sits meaningfully above the pre-FY W10-W11 range (514-525), so the broader uptrend since the MHLW trough is intact.

CPA has now fallen three consecutive weeks: $87 (W13) → $77 (W14) → $70 (W15) → $65 (W16). This is the cleanest weekly CPA improvement streak of 2026, and the $65 print is the lowest blended CPA of the post-MHLW era. The driver is consistent: Brand CPC compression without corresponding volume loss. If the trend holds one more week, W17 should print in the low-$60s.

## Relevant actions and events
- **W14 FY-end peak cleared**: Budget and bid lifts from 3/16-3/18 (Yahoo Brand Plus 40K→65K Yen, Google Brand Plus +$220 across portfolio) pulled demand forward into W13-W14. W15-W16 softening is natural reversion, not a break in the trend.
- **Brand CPC continues to fall**: $1.48 → $1.34 across two weeks. No competitive retreat is evident in the click data; this looks like a post-FY auction lull combined with quality score improvements from the late-March posture. Not budget-driven.
- **Golden Week approaches (W18)**: Apr 29 – May 5 is GW 2026. Measured LY impact: W18 2025 was -18.6% vs W17. W17 2025 was already -8% vs W16 as the pre-GW lull began. Expect TY to follow a similar pattern; W17 should soften modestly and W18 will take a ~15-20% hit.
- **Change log quiet since 3/23**: No structural platform changes in ~4 weeks. Current performance reflects steady-state execution of late-March posture.
- **OCI still blocked**: APAC MCC access has not cleared; NB remains at effective zero.

## YoY assessment
Regs +9% YoY (544 vs 500). Cost +17% YoY ($35.5K vs $30.4K). Blended CPA +8% YoY ($65 vs $61). Brand drives the mix: Brand regs +9% YoY (542 vs 496), Brand cost +29% YoY ($34.7K vs $26.8K), Brand CPA +18% YoY ($64 vs $54). We are buying ~9% more volume at ~17% more spend — YoY efficiency is worse even as WoW efficiency improves.

The YoY CPA erosion is structural: JP lost MHLW efficiency in early February, and the post-MHLW auction has not recovered LY's CPA floor. W16 2025 ran at $54 Brand CPA on a cheaper auction; TY's $64 is consistent with the new post-MHLW baseline. The three-week WoW improvement suggests we're walking back toward LY, but the gap has not closed. This should be framed as narrative honesty, not a new flag.
