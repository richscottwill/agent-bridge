# IT W13 Analysis Brief

## Registration summary
1,357 regs (+8% WoW), $97K spend (+25% WoW), $72 CPA (+16% WoW). Brand 930 (+2% WoW), NB 427 (+23% WoW). Projected: 5,800 regs, $409K spend vs OP2 5,563 regs, $384K spend (+4% regs, +7% spend).

## Why registrations changed
NB drove the growth. NB surged +23% on a +23% click expansion (24,711 vs 20,025) with CVR flat (1.73%). Brand grew +2% on +8% CVR (3.03% vs 2.81%) despite -5% clicks. The NB click surge is the largest single-week NB click increase in the 12-week window. However, flat NB CVR at 1.73% (matching W12's 8-week low) means the click expansion is adding volume without improving conversion quality. Spend rose +25% WoW, significantly outpacing the +8% reg growth, pushing CPA up +16%.

## Trend context
IT's oscillation pattern continues: W10 dip (1,153) to W11 bounce (1,443) to W12 giveback (1,255) to W13 recovery (1,357). The 4-week range is 1,153-1,443, a 290-reg swing. W13 at 1,357 is above the 8-week average of 1,302. The NB story is more interesting: NB regs at 427 are the highest since W11's 416, and NB clicks at 24,711 are a 12-week high. Brand at 930 is mid-range (804-1,048 over 8 weeks). CPA at $72 is a step up from W12's $62 but below the W8-W10 peak range ($74-$86).

## Relevant actions and events
1. Brand Core max CPC raised from $19 to $24 on 3/23 (80% of impressions were limited). This is a significant bid increase that should improve Brand impression share and volume in W14.
2. OCI E2E launched 2/26, now 4 weeks in. Revenue data exclusion added 3/23-3/31 for Generic campaign. Similar to FR, the bidding algorithm loses conversion feedback for the final week.
3. NB budget increases in W12: GA $600 to $900 (3/16), AMZ max $2.5K to $3K then $3.5K (3/16-3/18), Generic min $1.5K to $2K, max $2K to $2.5K then $3.5K (3/16-3/19). These explain the +23% NB click surge.
4. Shopify/mondoffice at >10% NB Generic IS. NB CVR flat at 1.73% (matching the 8-week low) despite the click surge. The competitor is capping CVR improvement.
5. Cross-market context: IT's +8% WoW recovery is solid, second to UK's +14%. The NB click expansion mirrors FR's pattern (+16% FR, +23% IT), both driven by budget increases.

## YoY assessment
+2% YoY regs (1,357 vs 1,326). +73% YoY spend ($97K vs $56K). Brand +7% YoY, NB -7% YoY. NB CPA +191% YoY ($162 vs $56). The NB CPA inflation is the most severe in EU5: nearly 3x LY. Brand is carrying the YoY growth. LY W13 was +8% WoW, matching TY's +8% exactly, suggesting the recovery is seasonal rather than structural.

## Monthly projection
Projected 5,800 regs vs OP2 5,563 (+4%). Projected $409K spend vs OP2 $384K (+7%). Remaining 3 days: 2 weekdays, 1 weekend day. W13 daily rates: weekday ~205 (avg of Mon-Fri), weekend ~167 (avg of Sun/Sat). Remaining: 2(205) + 1(167) = 577. MTD 5,208 + 577 = 5,785, rounded to 5,800. Spend: MTD $367K + ~$42K remaining = $409K. CPA: $71. IT has shifted from on-plan (W12: +2% regs, -1% spend) to slightly over on both dimensions. The +25% WoW spend increase is the driver; if this pace holds through month-end, the spend overshoot widens.

## Flags
- Spend acceleration: +25% WoW spend is the largest single-week increase in the 12-week window. CPA rose to $72 from $62. The NB budget increases are driving volume but at declining efficiency.
- NB CVR stuck: 1.73% for two consecutive weeks, matching the 8-week low. The click expansion is not improving conversion quality. Shopify/mondoffice competition is the structural cap.
- NB CPA: $162 is +191% YoY, the worst NB CPA inflation in EU5. OCI maturation is the only lever.
- Brand Core bid increase: Max CPC $19 to $24 should unlock impression share. Watch W14 for Brand volume response.
- OCI data exclusion: Revenue data excluded 3/23-3/31 for Generic. Same risk as FR: unoptimized bidding in the final week.

## Suggested narrative angle
IT recovered +8% WoW on a NB click surge (+23%), though NB CVR remained stuck at the 8-week low (1.73%). Brand grew modestly (+2%) on CVR improvement. Spend rose +25% WoW, pushing CPA to $72 (+16%). Monthly projection: +4% regs vs OP2 on +7% spend. Brand Core max CPC raised to $24 (from $19) to address impression limitations. NB CPA at $162 (+191% YoY) remains the primary concern.
