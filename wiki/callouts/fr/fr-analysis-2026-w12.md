# FR W12 Analysis Brief

## Registration summary
1,163 regs (-3% WoW), $61K spend (-15% WoW), $52 CPA (-12% WoW). Brand 493 (-10% WoW), NB 670 (+3% WoW). Projected: 4,950 regs, $296K spend vs OP2 4,694 regs, $254K spend (+5% regs, +17% spend).

## Why registrations changed
FR's -3% WoW decline is mild and driven entirely by Brand (-10%, 493 vs 549). NB actually grew +3% (670 vs 651). The Brand decline came from clicks (-12%, 22,423 vs 25,440) while Brand CVR held (+2%). NB improved on CVR (+6%, 2.93% vs 2.77%) despite a small click decline (-3%). The spend drop (-15%) is larger than the reg drop (-3%) because CPA improved across both segments: Brand $37 vs $40 (-7%), NB $63 vs $76 (-17%). This was an efficiency week.

## Trend context
FR has been in a tight 1,017-1,200 range for 8 weeks. W12 at 1,163 is the second-highest week after W11's 1,200. The trend is gently upward: W5-W8 averaged 1,069, W9-W12 averaged 1,137 (+6%). NB has been the growth engine, rising from 593 (W5) to 670 (W12). Brand has been volatile (418-549 range) with no clear trend. CPA peaked at $73 in W10 and has now dropped to $52, the lowest in 8 weeks.

## Relevant actions and events
1. OCI E2E launched 2/26. Only 3 weeks in. Too early for meaningful conversion lift, but the infrastructure is in place. Full impact projected Jul 2026.
2. bruneau.fr at 39-47% NB IS on Generic terms. This is the highest competitor IS in any EU5 market. Despite this, NB CVR improved +6% WoW and NB regs grew +3%. FR's NB is performing through the competitive pressure.
3. mirakl.com at 10% Brand IS, associated with -5% clicks and -14% CVR. The Brand click decline (-12% WoW) may partially reflect mirakl.com's continued presence.
4. Cross-market pattern: FR's -3% decline is the mildest among the 4 declining EU5 markets (DE -18%, IT -12%, ES -8%, FR -3%). FR's NB growth partially offset the Brand decline.
5. LY W12 was -18% WoW. TY's -3% is dramatically better than the seasonal pattern, suggesting structural improvement (OCI infrastructure, improved NB efficiency).

## YoY assessment
+14% YoY regs (1,163 vs 1,024). +59% YoY spend ($61K vs $38K). Brand +22% YoY, NB +8% YoY. NB CPA +65% YoY ($63 vs $38). The YoY spend growth (+59%) significantly outpaces reg growth (+14%), and NB CPA has nearly doubled. FR is buying growth. The question is whether OCI (launched 2/26) will bend the efficiency curve as it matures.

## Monthly projection
Projected 4,950 regs vs OP2 4,694 (+5%). Projected $296K spend vs OP2 $254K (+17%). Remaining 10 days: 6 weekdays, 4 weekend days. Used W12 daily rates (weekday ~185, weekend ~108) with flat seasonal assumption. LY W13 was +6% WoW, but OCI E2E learning phase (3 weeks in) may suppress any seasonal uptick, so applied flat rather than positive adjustment. FR is beating plan on regs but overspending by 17%. The ingester's 5,109 linear projection overstates because it doesn't discount for weekend days in the remaining window. The overspend is funding NB growth; if efficiency improves as OCI matures, the spend gap should narrow.

## Flags
- Spend overshoot: +17% vs OP2 spend is the largest overspend in EU5. This is sustainable only if regs continue to beat plan.
- NB CPA: $63 is +65% YoY. OCI is 3 weeks old and hasn't had time to optimize. This is the metric to watch over the next 8 weeks.
- bruneau.fr at 39-47% NB IS is a structural headwind. FR's NB is growing despite this, which is a positive signal, but the competitor ceiling is real.
- No data lag issues. Daily breakdown shows normal pattern with Sat (100) as the low and Mon (215) as the high.

## Suggested narrative angle
FR dipped -3% WoW as Brand clicks fell -12%, but NB grew +3% on improved CVR. CPA dropped to an 8-week low of $52 (-12% WoW). Monthly projection: +9% regs vs OP2 on +17% spend. OCI E2E is 3 weeks in; efficiency gains are the next milestone.
