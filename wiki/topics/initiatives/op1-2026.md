### 2026-05-06 — OP1 baseline review with Peter: switch to market-specific YoY

#### Source
hedy:lJsFwKc78UIFqqZeMlpo (Paid Acquisition OP1 Strategy and 2026/2027 Planning, 58 min)

Related: [meetings/brandon-sync.md](../../meetings/brandon-sync.md), [topics/tests/paid-app-mx](../tests/paid-app-mx.md)

#### What was said / what happened
Richard, Peter, Brandon reviewed Peter's OP1 spreadsheet. Two core problems surfaced: (1) 2026 actuals (Jan-Apr) in Peter's model were understating relative to the SSR-WBR doc — actuals are running ~20-25% higher than Peter's projections; (2) Peter's 2027 OP1 growth assumption was a flat 15% across all markets, not market-specific.

Brandon explained the baseline-discipline method: "I pulled all of the paid search statistics over the last three years. And in that way, I could have multiple years of comps... we don't really have a reliable trend here because there is nothing else external." Per Brandon on conservative forecasting: "It's easier to open it up. Yeah, and I think that's where I'm gunshot to and almost don't want to go crazy with January through April of this year, which have been great. Every other time that we shoot high, we end up missing."

Per Brandon on OP2 discipline: "OP1 has been to be aggressive. But if we include, if we say, oh, we're going to hit like a thousand percent year over year growth, whenever we get to OP2 and we're like, whoops, this was all wrong. It becomes really hard to downsize."

**App/paid discussion:** Peter's current split is 85% organic / 15% paid by market. Current Apple vs Google split for app is ~60% Google / 40% Apple. Brandon: "We've got, you know, we're counting on flat year-over-year budget from the SSR acquisition team who we know like really values efficiency... So then why don't we keep it as is for acquisition?" ASA platform full managed service is ~$130K/year ($100K consulting already paid, $30K platform fee not currently paid). Apptweak free tier stays if ASA spend stays under $25K/mo.

**Markets to keep / remove:** US, IT, DE, ES (app launched). CA, FR, JP, AU removed (no app). Italy and Middle East (Centric) are next launches. AU/MX excluded — no app.

**Registration goal structure:** Single registration goal for app (not split paid/organic). Peter submits combined with Yoav (free / organic). Richard's paid-app numbers are internal for Richard+Peter+Brandon tracking only.

**Event flighting:** Agreed to keep paid-app evergreen in SSR OP1 rather than front-load against events. Reserve event-driven spend ask for separate Go-Big engagement pitch.

#### Decisions
- OP1 registrations baseline uses historical multi-year YoY by market, not flat 15%
- App OP1 stays with SSR flat-budget assumption; ASA kept under $25K/mo free-tier cap
- Remove CA, FR, JP from forecast scope (no app); keep US, IT, DE, ES
- Single registration goal (paid+organic combined) submitted via Yoav; internal breakdown retained for tracking
- Paid-app stays evergreen in SSR OP1; event investment pitched separately as Go-Big

- Peter Ocampo — pull 2.5-year YoY by market + update OP1 spreadsheet with market-specific growth rates — 2026-05-08
- Peter Ocampo — verify 2026 actuals against SSR-WBR doc; reconcile the ~20-25% gap — 2026-05-08
- Richard — finalize CP1 submission for SSR (efficiency-based, flat budget) — 2026-05-08

### 2026-04-09 — Google Summit: marketing levers proposal for OP1

#### Source
hedy:vagAcJ6vqTRUWoLJhDZX (Google Summit Austin 2026, 153 min) + hedy:W4BfuXS0KnZ5QeZ8JJRe (Sid Acquisition session, 55 min) + hedy:4dQnNjazvfzOwbH75xJL (Adobe Health Review, 65 min) + hedy:DWvbZjVJZEgcCiE7bIef (Jasper AI demo, 113 min)

Related: [topics/tests/ai-max](../tests/ai-max.md), [topics/tests/baloo-shop-subdomain](../tests/baloo-shop-subdomain.md), [meetings/google-adobe-sync](../../meetings/google-adobe-sync.md)

#### What was said / what happened
Two-day Austin summit (4/8-4/9) across Google, Adobe, and internal OP1 planning. Key threads:

**Google ambition statement (hedy:vagAcJ6vqTRUWoLJhDZX):** "there's an opportunity to pull a few key marketing levers in order to increase the number of registrations generated from Amazon Business search campaigns by up to 40% at similar or same target ROAS or target CPC standards." Levers: (1) AI Max, (2) Smart Bidding Exploration (up to 19% more conversions at same ROAS), (3) elasticity testing, (4) search partner network reactivation (now safer with AI brand safety + smart bidding constraints; retail team turned it back on in H2 2025 after previous ban), (5) YouTube retargeting audiences, (6) first-party audiences + look-alikes (2027+).

**AI Max testing (hedy:vagAcJ6vqTRUWoLJhDZX):** three test methods proposed — single-campaign experiment (recommended: quickest, built-in tool, 50/50 split within campaign), multi-campaign subset (AWS did 15 non-branded campaigns), full account rollout (highest power, requires uncapping budgets). Team flagged OCI compatibility as a key question (keyword-level tracking on expanded matches is unclear). Open cannibalization concern: AI Max moves keyword matches across campaigns based on ad rank, which could shift attribution without growing incremental search. Negative keyword lists are contextual (AMX-level concern for Amazon Music/retail overlap).

**Internal OP1 brainstorm (hedy:MerPk0qtzBgEHgP0pBiO, 96 min):** big reframe — paid app should move from acquisition to traffic/engagement team because ~80% of install traffic is existing customers. Stacy: budget should come from Christine/Ann's team, not SSR. Action: Richard to write OP1 doc framing paid app as "traffic team investment" not "acquisition scraps." Separate initiative: LiveRamp enhanced match expected to ~double match rate (from ~20% post-retail-associate-expansion to ~40%). MCS vertical segmentation (tax, healthcare, etc.) of LiveRamp audiences discussed as OP1 candidate but operationally complex. Retargeting MCS-visitor-not-registered audience agreed as high-potential near-term lever.

**Sid acquisition overview (hedy:W4BfuXS0KnZ5QeZ8JJRe):** On-site placements drive >50% of registrations. Email contribution declining YoY due to fatigue. Push on M-Shop is new channel: only ~30% of audience is deliverable, highly constrained by consumer team scrutiny (16-20% of target population is reachable). AIOs/AI mode ads only in US, but AIM-reachable placements exist for intl. MTA/Rachel-Apache/HEMM attribution model was promised "after OCI" but has not been implemented through multiple model iterations.

**Adobe health (hedy:4dQnNjazvfzOwbH75xJL):** platform health score 64% (mostly green but with gaps). Alerts framework recommended for early detection (e.g., the "Ref Tag Q showing zeros" breakage Andrew caught). ASP spreadsheet feeds hitting file-size limits on recent-day refreshes. Hybrid optimization pitched: weighted multi-property objectives (registration + form start + form submit) but requires Adobe Analytics access + G-clid/EFID passthrough which remains blocked by our shadow-domain system.

**Jasper AI demo (hedy:DWvbZjVJZEgcCiE7bIef):** content team (Alexia) demonstrated Jasper used for EU5 SEO localization, master prompts, brand-voice workflows. Output grew from 8 white papers (2023) to 33 (2024) using Jasper. Richard's takeaway: could apply same validation framework to ad copy and Polaris LP localization.

#### Decisions
- Test AI Max in small-scale first before full rollout (verify OCI compatibility + landing-page behavior)
- Reconsider Search Partner Network (now safer with AI brand safety + smart bidding)
- Paid app budget ownership should shift to traffic/engagement team (to be formalized in OP1 doc)
- YouTube audience retargeting is viable near-term tactic
- Elasticity testing proposed as a portfolio-level OP1 test to find optimal ROAS targets
- Hybrid optimization with weighted objectives (reg=1.0, form start=0.5, form submit=0.2) needs data-signal threshold per portfolio before adoption

- Richard — run small-scale AI Max test on a low-risk campaign — by 2026-04-16
