# AEO / AI Overviews — Zero-Click & Answer-Engine Strategy

## Summary
AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) is Richard's Level 4 strategic track — the PS point-of-view on how paid search evolves as Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, and other answer engines displace click-based search. Forrester analyst Joe Cicman delivered a peer-reviewed brief to AB Marketing on 2025-12-22 framing the shift from click-centric to **answer-centric + proof-centric** metrics. Current priorities: (1) ungated content pilot to feed answer engines without killing lead-gen, (2) customer-interview-based dense case studies replacing third-party analyst investment, (3) evaluate Adobe LLM Optimizer / Profound for agentic traffic measurement, (4) synthetic-persona validation to reduce research cost. L4 POV deliverable target per brain.md: published POV with org-level visibility.

#### What was said / what happened
- Joe Cicman walked team (Brandon, Richard, Jen, David, Yun, Stacey, Andrew, Adi) through peer-reviewed brief framing AEO as a three-layer model: (1) answer layer (GPT/Gemini/Perplexity synthesize answers), (2) authority layer (what gets cited), (3) human layer (sales/consultative still matters post-discovery).
- Brandon's opener (quoted): "replace click-centric KPIs with answer-centric and proof-centric proxies." No mature zero-click KPIs exist yet — industry is creating them.
- Joe on agentic sourcing (quoted): "imagine that you have a supply chain platform... the operational metrics you're getting from that say it is for lack of this capability. That's all the metrics and process-level data that would be needed to, for instance, source that digital capability from some marketplace." B2B procurement bots sourcing autonomously — near-term possibility, not immediate.
- **Generational friction pattern (Jen's reframe + Joe's data)**: Millennials + Gen Z face the biggest procurement friction at "my organization's business process" (internal approval) — they don't know how to build the business case. Joe (quoted): "I am a millennial buyer and my work in procurement, I'm digitally comfortable, I know all the buttons, but I can't make a business case to anyone else in the office." Baby Boomers list that as their #3 problem or not a problem at all. Implication: AB should help millennials make the business case inside their own companies.
- **Gated vs ungated (Joe's resolution)**: run BOTH paths. Existing gated content stays; new ungated versions feed answer engines. Quoted: "I realized there was a world where you didn't have to make one or the other choice." Gated-form funnels will bleed traffic over time; ungated content will attract answer-engine discovery.
- **Content strategy pivot**: Joe proposes dense case-study pilot replacing third-party analyst reprints. Writing tools + existing customer advocates + interview prompts (before/pain/compelling-event/after/transition/advice-to-past-self) can produce deep case studies in the time it takes to write a landing page. Key: authenticity beats volume — bad case studies are "game of telephone" from boss→salesperson→customer.
- **Synthetic personas**: 80-90% accuracy validated vs primary research per Forrester practitioners. Can be constructed via answer engines + augmented with existing customer data.
- **Adobe LLM Optimizer**: Adobe built it for same problem. David to evaluate parallel to Profound (already onboarded by Yoab for AB monitoring US-only).
- **Content authority uncertainty**: unclear whether answer engines weight citation-over-time (stewardship) vs semantic vectoring. Joe recommends testing with known misconceptions to see which side wins.
- **Webinars declining, podcasts replacing** — Joe's observation via Mark Benioff saturation pattern. YouTube + Gemini makes any YouTube content inquirable, changing the platform not the format. Digital natives: AI conversational > social media > podcasts/YouTube. Gen X/Boomers: industry conferences + reports.
- **Quote from David**: "we're at a moment in time for our customers... there's a profound change happening across the marketplace and we're going to have to adapt at the same time." Richness over reach as the guiding philosophy — direct reference to the 1997 HBR article David cited.
- **Account-based expansion in federated enterprises** (Marriott hotel-chain example): Joe referred to Forrester's Renee Ariane for ABM strategies in multi-branch organizations. Adjacent to OP1-2026 ABM investment debate.
- **Enterprise sales nuance** (Joe on humans staying relevant): the prediction in 2015 of "death of the B2B salesman" proved wrong — salesforces grew, became consultative. Quoted: "the humans are going to do less and less lifting because the machines are going to do the lifting. But how do you get inside the head of your customer? Marketers deliver messaging at scale. Salespeople deliver grit."

#### Decisions
- Do not abandon gated content; run parallel ungated pilots to feed answer engines
- Prioritize customer-interview-based dense case studies over third-party analyst reprint dependency
- Begin synthetic-persona pilot using answer engines for initial build
- Evaluate Adobe LLM Optimizer alongside existing Profound rollout
- Build editorial guardrails (Joe quoted: "what if I treat it with the rigor that a pharmaceutical company or a financial products firm would, in terms of safety and truth") to protect AI-surfaced content quality
