## BR Justification
Amazon Business focuses on efficient new customer acquisition. When launching new markets, we research keyword costs and business trends to estimate launch CPAs. A component of this launch estimation is the need for flexibility to identify best-fit keywords and business-customer experiences. This learning phase creates inefficiency, but allows for necessary Customer Obsessed optimizations to create a foundation for highly efficient, long term approaches tailored to businesses. Long term, we expect CPAs to be higher than consumer due to lower conversion rates across a smaller qualified audience size & higher requirements (each registrant must submit 6 pages of information and be verified as a business), however, this is offset by their higher spend. We anticipate CPA of $79 to allow for initial discovery, and as data is gathered, will apply an iE%CCP threshold to all campaigns. iE%CCP threshold is determined by AB leadership and Finance to ensure long-term profitability (typically 50% iE%CCP).

### OP1 Big Rocks
#### Paid Search
Paid Search is the largest paid channel and the second overall contributing channel (Free Onsite ranks first), driving 25% of registrations for AB (763K Reg, 2023).
Brazil & Australia Expansion [$4.8M, 14k reg / .3 HC]
Paid Search was the sole paid channel to support Mexico launch and regardless of capped budget (-18% vs CA launch) successfully drove 2.2k reg within the first 3 months at +46% acquisition efficiency vs CA launch. Paid Search will continue supporting new market launches as the leading paid channel, to capture AB interest and bring awareness of the brand.
Secondary Language Support [$3.5M, 32k reg / .3 HC]
Current landing pages (MCS) require individual translation, which has constrained Paid Search ads to primary languages (ie. solely supporting German language in Germany). The Guest experience will help overcome this hurdle once the shopping experience is ungated. With this change, Paid Search will expand keywords to encompass all secondary languages (18 A.com supported languages WW), including English in all countries. Similarly, AWS has created a localization team for this exact purpose – driving +23% of sign-ups through English WW as a secondary language. We look to mimic the AWS model in standing up a Paid Search Localization expert to drive secondary language customer experiences (messaging, landing pages) and keywords, as well as managing the campaigns given the unique habits of this prospect subset. As a proof of concept, we are expanding Paid Search ads in DE to include English categories (office, food, beauty) by Jul 2024. OP1 calculations have assumed Jan 1 rollout.

Guest Experience [Reg Impact Pending / 0 HC]
Today there is a gap in experience as 58% of traffic has communicated product shopping intent, but MCS landing pages do not allow for a shopping or product engagement experience. Due to this, 85% of NonBrand (product) traffic drops off without starting registration. To access AB products, we currently require an upfront lengthy registration process (6-8 steps) and multi-hours/days verification for 20% of customers who go through manual verification. Our vision for Paid Search is to deliver a seamless experience where the intent expressed on Google is reflected directly in their AB experience, without any inputs required, allowing immediate visibility of AB value and meeting the expectations of the customer when searching product terms. We aim to execute this vision through the Guest experience which will allow us to take users’ search intent (e.g., “paper towels”) and drive them directly to a shopping experience, showing paper towel selection and pricing without requiring AB registration or verification. Q4 2024 we will test Guest V1 which allows AB Storefront access within minimal customer inputs (email, password, verify OTP). This reduces time to reach Storefront to 3 input pages, with checkout requiring Registration (remaining 3 pages). Ungated access to AB Storefront is expected in 2025, while still requiring Registration for checkout. Testing of Guest V1 is planned in Q4 2024 which will inform impact/goals.

Shopping Ads [$400k, 2.2k reg / .2 HC]
There are two primary Search ad types: Shopping Ads (85% of total Google clicks [Adthena]) and Text Ads (15% of clicks). AB has historically been constrained to Text Ads due to registration requirements. Shopping Ads are product specific, image-centric ads which display above Text Ads with an unlimited number of ad slots. B2B peers invest $1.7 in Shopping for every $1 invested in Text Ads, and in alignment, Shopping Ads have been core to the Consumer Paid Search approach and comprise half of their total iOPS. Shopping Ads, however, require publicly accessible PDPs/pricing visibility, as well as an ungated checkout option which has prohibited AB Paid Search efforts to date. The launch of Shopping ads will provide broader access to prospects at the top of Search Engine Results Page, as well as avoiding highly variable CPCs due to limitless listings (text ads are typically constrained to 3-4 ad spaces) which we estimate has an opportunity size of $486M annual 1yr OPS WW. Given tech requirements and reliance on ungated Guest, ETA is TBD Q3/Q4 2025. 2025 approach will be focused on US testing to ensure alignment with Consumer team and proof-of-concept.

iE%CCP constraints[YB1] [Reg Impact Pending / 0 HC]
Paid Search is constantly in demand as a primary method for capturing customers, which creates ever increasing CPCs. In 2024, we experiencing an extreme cost acceleration with EU NonBrand (product terms) +53% CPCs YoY (vs +34% ’23 YoY Q1), and even Brand +55-65% CPC across our largest markets (US, UK, DE) as competitors have pushed for 17% visibility across our “Amazon Business” terms. These increased costs coupled with decreased CCP (-3% Brand, -13% NB Q1 YoY) has limited ability to display ads/increase registration volume as we aim to achieve iE%CCP goals. In order to address this, we look to refine our iE%CCP measurement. In 2023 we enhanced measurement by implementing seasonality adjustments and accounting for fraud/abuse. Next we look to build on this by 1) researching segment churn rates and SPA growth rates to refine iE%CCP horizons (currently all segments use 3yr projections) and 2) accounting for secondary channel impact. For example, due to PS limited ability to audience target, current customers engage with Acq ads, but DSI related to HVA adoption isn’t captured. Similarly, NonBrand (product) ads are often the first exposure of prospects to AB, even when a click doesn’t occur. Updated iE%CCP will account for the increased OPS & awareness driven by these interactions. As the calculation becomes more refined, we are granularizing CCP information. We plan to send an obfuscated version of user-level CCP for use in automated bidding to increase weight toward high value prospects within cost constraints.

Google Bidding/OCI [Impacts 100% of OP1 PS budget, 115k Reg, 0 Marketing HC – Tech scoping separately]
Google’s automated bidding is unmatched in its capability to glean customer insights and utilize them at the time of search to manipulate a bid. Whereas outside systems can set a single bid per keyword with static bid modifiers based on time (ie. after 8pm) or day of week, there is no ability to parse the intent of the user. This is critical to a B2B use case as users’ intentions can be misrepresented when searching a B2B keyword. For example, the search “bulk hand fans” may represent the intent for one-time purchase related to their upcoming outdoor wedding. Google will help to sift through these intentions based on browsing-wide signals and conversion propensity at the time of search relative to the habits of the individual. As of May 7 legal review has required revert to static, single bids causing <30% reg loss. While we look for quick mitigation opportunities, OCI is the most advanced, long-term solution. OCI is compliant integration of Google bidding (restoration of 30% regs), and will further increase registrations through enhancement of conversion signals passed to Google. Whereas AB has historically provided a “1” flag representing a registration, we will be enabled to pass a user-level value assignment based on CCP. Algorithmic bidding will then aid in bidding toward highest value customers in addition to conversion likelihood. This is expected to drive +13% lift in registrations above previous Google levels. AB is aiding Central development for OCI support, which is currently in scoping (ETA TBD, ~Q1 2025). OP1 calculations have assumed rollout Jan 1.
