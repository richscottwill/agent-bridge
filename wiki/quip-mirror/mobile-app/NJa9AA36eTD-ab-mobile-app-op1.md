**App Store Optimization**

**Paid App Marketing [$2M, 18k reg, 505K installs, 0 HC]**
Mobile App Marketing is split between organic and paid tactics which are used to drive app installs with both prospects and existing customers. AB has implemented its organic strategy by leveraging App Store Optimization (ASO) practices to increase organic growth. To complement and accelerate growth, Paid App Marketing (PAM) will be used to promote Amazon Business within the Google and Apple app stores. Paid app marketing compliments our organic marketing strategy by increasing keyword search volume for competitive terms (such as b2b or shopping) while leveraging our strong performance with branded words (Amazon or Amazon Business). Throughout 2024, we are supporting Tier 1 events through Paid App campaigns (Google and Apple) and plan to accelerate insights through the implementation of AppsFlyer (in-app attribution solution) in Q3. AppsFlyer will enable comparative CPI, and performance testing across expanded ad locations to inform 2025 approach.

[Not inc. in Final] Paid App Marketing is primarily executed within the Google and Apple appstores however can be expanded to other paid channels such as Facebook, LinkedIn, Tiktok, or connected television (CTV). Advertising on Apple's and Google's platforms have unique benefits that provide us with access to different prospects and customers. **Apple Search Ads** (ASA) provides 4 primary locations in which our ads are displayed: 1) Today Tab, 2) Search Tab, 3) Search Results, and 4) Product Pages. Each of these locations offer unique benefits at different costs and will influence the cost per install (CPI) however they will only be seen by Apple users. **Google Ads** promotes the app across a range of Google properties like 1) Search, 2) Google Play, 3) YouTube, and 4) Google Display Network. Because Google Ads are displayed outside Google Play store, they are effective at reaching both Apple and Google users.

**Onsite ****Placement ****[$0, Impact Pending, 0 HC]**
Since the launch of the AB Mobile app, we have promoted its availability through Onsite placements on [AB.com][1] to existing customers. To improve performance of Onsite placements on AB.com, we will update our placements with updated graphics, copy, and leverage QR codes to increase engagement. We will expand our Onsite placements on A[.com][2] which will increase our visibility of the AB Mobile app to prospects and existing AB customers driving both acquisition and app adoption.

****\[Onsite\] Paid Search Retargeting ****[$0, Reg Impact Pending, 0 HC]
Through MLA research, we have discovered 37% overlap with Onsite prior to registration. This exemplifies a need to support users throughout their journey to incite confident registration. With broad, no-cost reach, Onsite provides an optimal option to create a sequential touchpoint. As our first cross-channel retargeting effort, we plan to expand support to all Paid Search engagers who have not yet registered by retargeting them with Onsite placements over a short-term period, allowing them another opportunity to engage AB.

****SMS ******[Budget Pending, Impact Pending - 10K reg / .5 HC]**
[Acquisition/Events] 3P events could help SSR to improve brand perception among small businesses, drive new registrations and engage existing customers to increase the share of procurement spend they make on AB. Regarding Acquisition, we foresee the opportunity to drive__10k new SMS registrations WW at year 1, assuming we participate on 4 events per year per country (one per quarter) with an average size of 3-5k business attendees and we hit ~10% conversion rate. In terms of risk, we are aware that events are timely to execute, need a specific playbook and preparation work that are not staffed to execute. We plan to test this channel in H2 in DE and US and make a recommendation to SSR leadership based on preliminary results.

[Engagement] SMS is a text messaging service that allows the exchange of short (160 characters) messages to our customers. SMS open rate is 98%, +7800 bps vs. email, likely due to SMS requiring explicit opt-in permissions from customers. We envision using the channel for highly targeted, time-bound campaigns as a hook for important notifications and marketing value programs will help drive higher conversion compared to other outbound channels.********At Amazon, non-household customer SMS must be sent using [AWS Pinpoint][3] and in partnership with Mobile App Marketing Team, we will onboard and experiment with SMS in H2'24 to share our 2025 strategy.

**H\(app\)y ****Business ****Day [Budget TBD, Impact Pending / .5 HC]**
The AB App is a key differentiator from our competitor and, as a result, represents an opportunity to highlight our app to prospects who value a best-in-class mobile experience while increasing engagement with our existing customers. H(app)y Business Day (HBD) will leverage exclusive offers and content to drive engagement in the AB App by incentivizing in-app engagement not available in the Amazon Shopping app (mShop) or through the phone's browser (mWeb). By providing app exclusive promotions and by highlighting features found in Business Settings, we will help our customers maximize the benefits of the AB Mobile App above other competing mobile surfaces. App adoption is strategic given it is an HVA with a DSI of 3.4% and drives adoption of other HVAs such as adding users, ATEP, etc. The launch of HBD could be used to drive growth during traditionally slower times (Q1, early Q2) or as part of the lead up to an established HVE. While each approach has benefits, launching in H2 would provide lift in registrations and visiting users, while encouraging customers to use the app for shopping and account management for the entire year. Testing of this mobile-first approach is being ideated within 2024 as part of a lead up for Tier 1 events.

Amazon Brazil similarly launched an "[App Day][4]" which drove +141% new customers to the app, and +321% first app purchase. Additionally, the increased interest in the Amazon App pushed its Apple App store ranking to #1 for the first time – increasing visibility further. Additionally, Amazon App had planned to test App Only Deals (akin to onsite "Deal of the Day"), however, the supporting team was dissolved due to resources prior to launch.

The Mobile Growth team (who led the mShop app marketing but are now disbanded) have conducted a number of experiments over the years in different markets and created the [App Exclusive Design Wiki][5] to identify best practices across marketplaces. Prior to their team being disbanded, they created a [weblab][6] which never launched but was intended to test the performance of an "App Only Deal" in lieu of the "Deal of the Day"

**[Amazon Brazil "App Day" ][4]**
Brazil launched first-app exclusive event 'App Day' on April 22-23, 2024.****The event featured over 30K App only deals, including coupons for new Prime customers and for app customers purchasing with Mastercard. [App Day][7] drove 172K new customers to the app (FSI, +141% vs BAU), with 101K placing a First App Purchase (FAP, +32% vs goal, +321% vs BAU). The Amazon Shopping app reached #1 in 'Top Free Apps' in the iOS' App Store for the first time and the App OPS SoS reached 70.8% the first day (68% over the 2-day period vs 49% BAU), the highest rate since the app launched in BR.

**[App Only Deals - CA: ][8]**
[mShop Weblab][9] (not launched) replaced the Deal of the Day with an App only deal. While this was not launched, it could be a great starting point to test the response to app only promos.

**[App only Flash deal][10] \(2016\)**
Re-ran high quality deals that did not sell out during Black Friday, Cyber Monday, and Thanksgiving and re-ran them only in the app.

 * Customers who visited the app campaign page average 1.25 more visit days in the app than those who did not
