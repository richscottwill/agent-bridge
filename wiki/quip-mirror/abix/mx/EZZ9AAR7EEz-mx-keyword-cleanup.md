# MX Audit – Keyword Cleanup Framework
**Objective**
Provide a clear framework for keyword cleanup in the MX Search account to:
* Reduce wasted spend on consistently underperforming terms.
* Streamline account structure and avoid duplication.
* Ensure alignment across stakeholders on thresholds and decision rules.
* Create a reference point for future audits and cross-market consistency.

**Thresholds Applied**
* **0 clicks: **
 * Signals irrelevance (impressions with no engagement).
 * Paused to protect CTR and Quality Score.
* **0 registrations with spend > $600 YTD**
 * Keywords below this spend threshold will remain active to allow more time to gather traffic and potential conversions.
* **Spend > $600 with CPA > $500**
 * Covers terms that convert, but at highly inefficient costs.
* **Duplicate keywords across match types \(exact / phrase / broad\)**
 * Removed underperforming keywords; kept outperforming.
 * Prevents budget from being spread thin across repetitive terms and avoids self-competition.
**Rationale**
* The focus is on keywords with meaningful spend and no results rather than low-volume terms, to avoid cutting prematurely.
* Cleaning up duplicates reduces noise, ensures budget consolidation, and keeps the structure lean.
* Most paused keywords still fell into multiple categories, reinforcing their consistent underperformance.
* Keywords can always be reintroduced if future search term data shows potential.
### **Brand Check Keywords**
* Keywords containing "Amazon" or other brand references flagged separately.
* To review jointly whether they should remain outside or be consolidated under Brand campaigns.
### **Next Steps**
* Share paused keyword list + thresholds applied.
* Add Brand keyword list for review and consolidation decision.
* Monitor search term reports for coverage gaps before further consolidation.
* Align thresholds across markets for consistency.
