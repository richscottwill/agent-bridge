**_Transition Update:_**

**DE Performance:** Green
 
DE has seen a +18% registration lift (86% to expectation). As of 12/27, OCI has driven +1,466 incremental registrations. DE was slightly below the US and UK uplift due to privacy impacts and greater click costs which limited efficient volume. 
 
Continued OCI utilization is expected to further improve algorithmic bidding performance across all countries but is particularly critical in markets with stringent privacy requirements. This was likely a contributing factor to longer-term DE performance variability and we will use this information to inform milestone timelines as we proceed to EU3 OCI rollout. 
 


**[DE Complete | US/UK Complete] Milestone 5: Transition to Google Bidding & Finalize Measurement**
DE: DE was transitioned to M4 on 11/11 with impact at +18% registrations, 86% to expectation. We saw unstable performance due to ABMA attribution changes on 11/13, impacting our learning algorithm and subsequent spending during W47. Due to spend variability (primarily driven by privacy impacts), we extended the test window from 12/8 to 12/27. NonBrand delivered +17% registrations at -37% CPA improvement, slightly below expectations (-14%). This was driven by privacy-related variability in the values passed through OCI. DE is unique given the high performance of desktop campaigns, but this privacy-driven variability combined with higher cost-per-clicks (+27% vs UK) limited the ability to capture incremental efficient volume at scale. Brand registrations increased +1% at -10% CPA improvement, in line with other OCI regions and expectations.
US: Overall, US performance reached 96% of our expectations, with +24% registration uplift. NonBrand performance results demonstrate success, aligning with expectations at +23% registrations at -45% CPA improvement. We expected Brand to deliver a 2% registration lift, given the lower opportunity for CPA improvement due to high customer intention. Brand delivered a +1% registration lift at -10% CPA improvement, slightly below target primarily due to increased competition from Walmart on Brand terms throughout 2025. 
UK: UK has reached 94% of expectations with a +23% registration lift. We experienced budget variance due to volatility in W39-40, which has been resolved and resulted in -38% NonBrand CPA improvement, slightly lower than US results which is attributed to EU DMA restrictions. Brand -14% CPA improvement contributed to +1% registration lift, aligned with lower CPA improvement expectation compared to NonBrand.
