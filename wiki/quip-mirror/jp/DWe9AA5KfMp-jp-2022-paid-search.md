# JP 2022 Paid Search
## 2022 Insights & Opportunities
### JP Callback page
[Hybrid LPs][1] were rolled out, allowing either self-registration or lead form submission. Analysis shows no cannibalization across self-registration acquisition, increasing overall registrations.

Performance

Test 1: Registrations grew +31.8% (327 vs. 431) with 259 additional leads (wk 19 vs 22). Among these leads, 82% are Micro and 18% qualified as CPS with 46.7% conversion rate (acquisition / calls completed) - 2-3 times higher than other marketplaces. By the end of June, the new Callback page drove 4,520 SSR registrations and additional 281 MQLs which converted to 187 (CPS: 28, Micro: 159) acquisitions.

Test 2: Callback vs. TryAB Landing Page Test
![image.png][2]Opportunity

Hybrid LPs should be used across PS activation to allow users choice in how they engage with AB; either self-registering or through phone contact.
### Non Brand Coverage
NonBrand (NB) terms have performed very poorly, with unsustainable CPAs, and have been paused until an alternative approach can be found.

Performance
Standard Performance: Jan-May brought 184 total registrations at a cost of $193k, a CPA of $1,050 (vs. Brand $32 CPA with 8.5k regs) . The top categories Office, General, and Automotive had CPAs ranging from $750 to $2,000.

Promo Test Performance: 4th Anniversary (2021) led 2 registrations with $5k CPA.

Opportunity
With broad, product and category interest, NonBrand serves as a method to drive registrations as well as brand awareness.

Text Ads: We believe relaunch of NB ads should be aligned with branding efforts and increased penetration of the market. Given the acquisition inefficiency, NB ads should be viewed as an awareness driver. We cannot run brand lift studies across Search, however, we can can measure the impact on brand search demand modeled vs actual [causal impact using bayesian structural time-series models - would require analytical resources].

New Ads: These will impact brand awareness even more highly due to the higher impact, visual ads included.

Google has launched 2 new ad types, for which Adobe is just rolling out support; [Performance Max][3] & [Discovery Ads][4]. These ad types will allow us to target users outside of the SERP based on keywords*. These keywords are utilized based on users' intent without the need for them to Search on Google.com. Combining the expansion of intent-based keywords and off-SERP reach will provide a broader pool to engage, and has highly reduced CPCs according to Google case studies. Testing is planned for EU/NA within 2022 to understand performance implications to AB.

Yahoo Branded Search Ads have also provided us with an opportunity to ensure further brand penetration and coverage of NB keywords. While BSAs are flat-fee, they must also coincide with NB keyword coverage. These may prove as an excellent option for branding, and in particular where a promo or strong CTA is available. Initial performance readout will be available by early Oct on the branded ad test (SBM/Anniversary Sale).
### Bidding Optimization
Opportunity
We have tested multiple bidding strategies through AMO (Adobe Media Optimizer) and Google including Adobe Auto-Bidding, Google tCPA Bidding, and Google Max Conversion Bidding with or without CPA cap to learn the different outcome and efficiency to maximize the performance. Since majority of our traffic and registrations are coming from Brand, we plan to test Google Target Impression Share Bidding which will enable us to set bids with the goal of showing our ad on the absolute top of the page. Even this automated bidding strategy expects to increase the ad spend but we anticipate observing the growth of Share of Impression and CTR in the same time to potentially drive more registrations.

Top IS was launched 2 weeks ago for JP, and we will share implications/performance as soon as they are available.
### Auto Verification (AVT)
Had been tested in 2022, but Google Audiences are too small & thus there are no current insights.

Performance
AVT has been tested in the US on audiences designated by Google as Employment > Company Size > Small (1-249 employees), Large (250-10k), and Very Large Employer (10k+). This approach has been successful, allowing 62k customers to immediately shop leading to higher activation rates (19% ActRate, +8% vs manual approval), while leading a defect rate 69% lower than other existing Allowlist programs.

Opportunity
We are currently working with the verification team to expand AVT across all Brand Core keywords (ie. "amazon business"). This will bypass the need for audience inclusion and provide a stronger experience to a broader set of customers.
