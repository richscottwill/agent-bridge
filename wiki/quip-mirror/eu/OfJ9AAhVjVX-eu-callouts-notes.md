### Week 22 (5/26 - 6/1)
**EU5: **Paid Search drove 4.4K registrations (+4% WoW, +5% YoY) with -3% WoW spend and $100 CPA (-7% WoW, 69% YoY), and 71% ie%CCP. We ended May at 2% above OP2 Spend, and -20% OP2 Registrations (each market seeing a decrease of 500bps to 2000bps MoM, and EU5 a decrease of 1170bps)
**WoW** we see an increase in registrations despite a 3% drop in Brand registrations, and an overall decrease in both ES and IT. The decrease in Brand is due to Corpus Christi holiday in DE/ES, and a spring bank holiday in the UK on 5/27. The increase WoW can be attributed to the overall 16% increase in NB registrations (each market increased aside from IT) primarily due to traffic in FR/ES (+26%/9% clicks respectively), and because of a mix of traffic/CVR in UK/DE (UK: +11% CVR/+15% clicks; DE: +28% CVR/+20% clicks). This 14% EU5 increase in NB clicks was possible because of a 12% decrease in NB CPC, which can be attributed to our switch away from manual bidding. (Cost and CTR was flat WoW for NB)
**YoY** aggregate EU5 data is skewed due to UK incrementality test last year. If comparing EU5 minus UK, we saw +23% Brand/-39% NB YoY. (Compared to last week's YoY numbers: +17% Brand/-55% NB YoY) Overall registrations improved from -26% last week vs. -12% this week. We are currently in a declining seasonality as mentioned by Giulia/Stacey in last year's callout, until September, but we seem to be closing the gap that manual bidding created. The last time we were at -12% YoY regs was on 5/7, a day after our switch to manual bidding.

### Week 20 (5/12 - 5/18)
**EU:**Paid Search drove 4.5K registrations (+3% WoW, -18% YoY) with +7% WoW spend and $99 CPA (+4% WoW, +6% YoY), and 68% ie%CCP. ie%CCP improved to 68% overall, but slight room for improvement at the market level, as UK/ES are above 80%, and DE at 58%.
**WoW** we attribute the increase in registrations to an 8% increase in clicks, more specifically a 15% increase in Brand clicks. We saw a decline in CVR last week due to our switch to manual bidding, and no improvement this week, with NB most affected. Brand registrations look okay, and the most we've seen since Week 10, likely due to the nature of bidding on Brand being easier to manage manually than NB, and us opening up capped Brand budgets across the board to take advantage of its higher CCPs. (Easier to manage because Brand CPCs are generally less than half of NB, and impression share not as limited as NB. Also NB more negatively affected by the change to manual bidding because it no longer takes advantage of Google bid strategies' 1st party to manage wasted spend more easily.) Although reg volume has increased for Brand, we still saw a 29% increase in CPA the last 2 weeks compared to previous period; compared to 48% increase on NB side.
**YoY** registrations for NB decreased by 43%, but Brand increased by 17%. This shows less that Brand is doing well, and more that NB has been largely affected by our switch to manual bidding. NB had been averaging about -27% YoY for NB regs before the switch, and the reason for the decline to -43% is due to +38% CPCs, leading to decreased investment (-32% spend, -51% clicks). Other metrics not related to cost increased YoY due to improvements (+20% CVR and +106% CTR to compensate for increased cost).

### **Week 17:**
**EU**:
* Paid Search drove 5.3K registrations (-4% WoW, -7% YoY) with -9% WoW spend and $73 CPA (-4% WoW, -10% YoY), and 55% ie%CCP. UK and FR were markets we've wanted to improve. UK continues to be at high, but with a slight WoW from 90% to 77%, but lower than last year's 103%. FR improved from 66% to 60%, in line with our target. Overall, ie%CCP improved, with opportunity to push DE, IT, and ES. EU5 is projected to end April with +1% planned spend and -6% planned registrations.
* WoW we've lowered spend by 8% with 4% decrease in regs, compared to last year where we were flat in spend and 17% decrease in regs WoW (-17% CVR WoW for both Brand and NB last year, vs. +12% Brand and -10% NB this year). The increase in Brand regs is most noticeable in the UK because of +30% WoW regs, but we saw a significant CVR increase in each market except IT (UK +29%, DE +9%, FR +14%, IT +3%, ES +13%). This increase in Brand CVR doesn't seem to be driven by changes we made last week, but may be related to investigation that ABMA is conducting. (Will have to confirm with them about whether any change was made in categorizing regs for reporting)
* YoY we are seeing -7% regs, which is the best we've seen since week 9, and the third best YTD. This can be attributed to Brand, which increased to similar YoY level as in February. This may be due to ABMA's investigation given the similarity in YoY change for both Brand and NB as in Feb, but not confirmed.
