## Verdict

**REVISE**

## The core problem

This document is written as a strategy narrative for Richard's own thinking. It is excellent at that. But it is not written for Kate. Kate reads for decisions, not trajectories. She needs to know: what are you asking me to support, what does it cost, what happens if it works, what happens if it doesn't, and when will we know. This doc answers the last three but never explicitly answers the first two.

The main body is approximately 3,500 words before appendices. Kate's two-page limit means she reads roughly 1,000–1,200 words. She gets through Purpose and most of 2026. Everything else is appendix material for Kate's purposes — she'll delegate the 2028–2030 read to her team if she cares.

## Required changes

### 1. Add an executive summary after Purpose (the single most important fix)

The first thing Kate sees after the title should be a 150–200 word block that answers:
- What is this document? (Five-year strategic outlook for Paid Search)
- What are the 2026 investment bets? (AI Max testing, Baloo Shopping Ads, F90 lifecycle, agentic team adoption)
- What is the proven foundation these bets build on? (OCI: 35K registrations, $16.7M OPS in 2025)
- What does Kate need to do? (Endorse the 2026 test plan. Be aware of the 2027+ trajectory. No incremental budget ask — these run within existing allocation.)
- When will we know if the bets are working? (AI Max: Q3 2026 read. Baloo: Q4 2026 quality data. F90: ongoing monthly.)

If there IS an incremental budget ask, state it. If there isn't, say so — Kate will assume there is one if you don't.

### 2. Cut the main body to two pages

Move 2028, 2029, 2030, and "Broader Agentic Evolution" to the appendix. They are scenario planning, not operational planning — the doc says so itself. The main body should be:
- Executive summary (new, ~200w)
- 2026 — Validate and Scale (tighten to ~600w, cut the "What we are watching" subsection to two sentences and move the detail to appendix)
- 2027 — Adapt to Platform Shifts (tighten to ~400w, this is the "if 2026 works, here's what's next" section)
- "What changes if we are wrong" consolidated section (~200w covering both years)

Total main body target: ~1,400 words. Kate reads the whole thing. Todd reads the exec summary and skims 2026.

### 3. Rewrite the Purpose paragraph to include the ask

Current opening: "This document projects the trajectory of Amazon Business Paid Search from 2026 through 2030."

This tells Kate what the doc IS. It doesn't tell her what it's FOR. Replace with something like:

"This document presents the Paid Search team's five-year strategic outlook and the four investment bets for 2026 that build on validated 2025 results. It requests leadership endorsement of the 2026 test plan — AI Max, Baloo Shopping Ads, F90 lifecycle engagement, and agentic team tooling — all within existing budget allocation. Each bet has defined go/no-go gates and a fallback position."

Then the thesis paragraph can follow.

### 4. Surface the "what changes if we are wrong" pattern as a decision table

Kate loves the intellectual honesty of the fallback positions. But they're buried at the bottom of each year section. Consolidate into a single table in the executive summary or immediately after 2026:

| Bet | If it works | If it doesn't | When we know |
|-----|------------|---------------|--------------|
| AI Max | +14% conversions, worldwide rollout 2027 | Continue OCI-optimized Search | Q3 2026 |
| Baloo Shopping Ads | New ad format, lower-friction registration | Monitor 90-day quality metrics | Q4 2026 |
| F90 Lifecycle | 3+ purchase rate from 31.7% → 35.4% | Channel already profitable, continue | Monthly |
| Agentic team adoption | One tool adopted by teammate | No sunk cost, infrastructure persists | Q4 2026 |

This is the single most Kate-friendly element you could add. She can read this table in 15 seconds and understand the entire risk profile.

### 5. Tighten the 2026 "What we know" section

The OCI paragraph, ad copy paragraph, Baloo paragraph, and F90 paragraph are each well-written but collectively too dense for the main body. Kate needs the headline numbers, not the methodology. For example:

Current (Baloo paragraph, 73 words): "Project Baloo launched early access on March 30, enabling unauthenticated catalog exploration before registration. This unlocks Shopping Ads for AB — a format previously unavailable because AB required authentication before product browsing. The US Q2 2026 broader rollout represents the single largest structural change to the registration funnel since in-context registration delivered 13,600 annualized incremental registrations at 100% probability via APT."

Kate version (~40 words): "Project Baloo (early access March 30, broader US rollout Q2 2026) enables unauthenticated catalog browsing, unlocking Shopping Ads for AB for the first time. This is the largest structural change to the registration funnel since in-context registration delivered 13,600 annualized incremental registrations."

The APT methodology detail and the authentication explanation are appendix material.

### 6. The "Broader Agentic Evolution" section is a separate document

This section restates market size projections, architecture patterns, and counternarratives already covered in the year-by-year sections. It reads like a research brief appendix, not a strategy narrative section. Either cut it entirely (the year-by-year sections already cover the relevant external context) or move it to Appendix C. It should not be in the main body under any circumstances — Kate will never reach it, and if she did, she'd recognize the repetition.

## What works well (non-blocking observations)

- The confidence labeling (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW) per year is excellent. Kate trusts this immediately because it signals intellectual honesty rather than false precision.
- The "what changes if we are wrong" framing in every section is exactly what leadership wants. The fix is surfacing it, not changing it.
- The voice is right. This sounds like Richard writing with conviction grounded in data. No hype, no hedging beyond what the confidence level warrants.
- The 2026 section is genuinely strong on substance. The problem is presentation for Kate's reading pattern, not content quality.
- Source attribution is thorough without being academic. The confidence-tiered citation appendix is a good model.

## Summary

The substance is strong — this is a well-researched, intellectually honest strategy document. The problem is structural: it's written as a 3,500-word narrative when Kate needs a 1,400-word decision document with appendices. The voice is right, the data is right, the honesty is right. The packaging is wrong for the audience. Add the executive summary, add the decision table, move 2028–2030 to appendix, and this ships.
