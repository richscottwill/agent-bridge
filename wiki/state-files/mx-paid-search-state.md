## Introduction

This document provides the trailing-week operational state of the Amazon Business Mexico Paid Search program. MX is Richard Williams's hands-on market, managed through Google Ads on the NA MCC (683-476-0964) under the DSAP parent MCC (873-788-1095). The program operates under an ie%CCP constraint model where Brand registrations at $21 CPA generate surplus Customer Cost to Pay that funds Non-Brand acquisition at higher CPAs. AB Finance assumed MX funding responsibility in March 2026. Lorena Alvarez Larrea replaced Carlos Palmos as the primary MX PS stakeholder on March 17, 2026. Keyword-level registration data is unavailable until Q3 2026; all Non-Brand performance analysis is directional.

## Goals

1. Achieve a blended CPA at or below $66 for FY26 (OP2 target), while maximizing registration volume within the ie%CCP constraint.
2. Maintain ie%CCP at or above 100% from Q2 2026 onward (Q1 operated at 77–99% as Brand surplus built).
3. Deliver 11,178 total registrations for FY26 against the OP2 plan ($542K spend), with upside scenarios projecting 13,948–15,051 registrations at $745K–$959K spend.
4. Complete the Polaris Brand landing page migration for MX per Brandon's priority order (AU > MX > DE > UK > JP > FR > IT > ES > CA > US-ES).
5. Resolve the automotive landing page ref tag overwrite issue that breaks paid search attribution.

## Tenets

1. **ie%CCP governs spend, not budget.** Non-Brand spend expands only when Brand CCP surplus permits. The constraint is mathematical, not discretionary.
2. **Efficiency over escalation.** When NB CPC rises, respond with bid strategy refinement and negative keyword routing — not budget increases.
3. **Evidence before scale.** No campaign structure change, keyword expansion, or landing page variant ships to full traffic without a measurement period.
4. **Stakeholder responsiveness builds trust.** Lorena is a new relationship. Timely, thorough responses to her requests compound into strategic partnership.

## Lessons Learned

Three operational insights from the trailing three-week window (W13–W15) inform the immediate tactical posture:

1. **The W14 NB CVR dip to 1.08% was entirely holiday-driven; W15's 1.32% confirms the W11 negative keyword restructuring remains durable.** NB CVR over the last 5 weeks tracked 1.57% (W11) → 1.38% (W12) → 1.46% (W13) → 1.08% (W14 Semana Santa) → 1.32% (W15). Excluding W14, the NB CVR holds steadily within the post-restructuring baseline of 1.3%–1.5%, validating that the negative keyword work is structurally intact. No bid strategy or keyword changes are needed on the NB side based on W15 alone. If W16 NB CVR maintains the 1.3%+ range, the team can turn attention from NB defense to NB expansion.

2. **Brand CVR at 8.08% in W15 is a rebound-driven high, not a new baseline.** W15's Brand CVR is 34% above the 8-week trailing average and is the highest in the trailing 8 weeks. This is consistent with a post-holiday pent-up-demand pattern — Mexican B2B buyers who deferred registrations during Semana Santa completed the funnel in the first clean week. The correct planning assumption for W16–W17 is a reversion to the 6.0%–6.5% Brand CVR range, which would return Brand CPA to the $19–$23 stability band and blended CPA to $55–$65. Treating 8.08% as the new run rate would overstate Q2 forecasts by ~30%.

3. **ie%CCP at approximately 132% in W15 is above the 100% Q2 target but does not require operational action because Brand surplus drove the overshoot.** W14's 126% ie%CCP was an NB-CPA-compression signal (NB CPA $159 on weak CVR). W15's 132% is the inverse: Brand registrations at 373 generated $33,570 in CCP surplus against total spend of $25,445 — the program is now funding NB capacity, not consuming it. The NB CPA question persists (W15 NB CPA $141.99 is still well above the $30 NB CCP), but the April budget pressure from W14 has eased materially. The April NB reduction plan can be paused pending W16 data rather than executed immediately.
