# CPC Benchmark Defense Playbook

Country leaders and cross-functional partners periodically challenge B2B paid search CPC by comparing it to consumer retail CPC. The comparison is structurally flawed but emotionally compelling. This playbook gives paid search leads a tested response framework, based on the March 2025 Australia challenge.

## The challenge pattern

A country leader sees consumer CPC running at $0.18 to $0.50 and B2B CPC at $5 to $8. They ask why the gap is so large and whether it signals inefficiency. The implicit question is whether the paid search team is overspending. The explicit ask is often "can we reduce CPC without reducing registration volume."

The challenge is legitimate in the sense that gross CPC differences deserve explanation. It is flawed in the sense that B2B and consumer CPC are not comparable — they reflect different keyword pools, different competitive dynamics, different product maturity, and different available ad formats.

## Why B2B CPC is structurally higher Four factors explain the gap, and all four should appear in the response. **Keyword competitive dynamics.** B2B keywords cover procurement, wholesale, bulk supply, and business services. These keywords command higher bids because each click has higher expected revenue and the bidder pool includes well-funded B2B platforms. Consumer keywords cover a broader surface with many low-bid long-tail terms. **Product maturity in-market.** A market that launched recently has less algorithmic signal for Google's bidding model to work with, which pushes CPC higher. Australia launched in June 2025; Mexico has been live longer; the US has years of history. Maturity difference alone can explain thirty to fifty percent of CPC variance across markets. **Ad format availability.** Amazon Business does not have access to Shopping Ads. Consumer retail does. Shopping Ads typically carry lower CPC than text ads for the same query intent, which pulls the consumer average down. Until AB gains Shopping Ads access (not on the 2026 roadmap), this gap is structural. **OCI maturity.** Once OCI rolls out, CPC drops materially. The US has seen non-brand CPC improve 45 percent post-OCI. Pre-OCI markets like Australia run at pre-optimization CPC until OCI launches there. Australia's OCI timeline is not yet confirmed. ## Response structure

Lead with acknowledgment: "You are right that the absolute numbers look wide, and I want to explain what drives it." Then walk the four factors above in order. Close with the concrete optimization plan — in Australia's case, the keyword audit on the top twenty by CPC/CPA, keyword-to-product mapping, and Polaris migration. Brandon's framing from March 23: focus on outlier keyword analysis, not broad reductions. CPC caps are a two-way door until OCI.

Do not agree to broad CPC caps without data. A cap applied across the full keyword set reduces impressions on high-converting keywords alongside low-converting ones, dropping registrations more than cost. Do not compare B2B CPC to consumer CPC in your response — that validates the flawed framing. Do not dismiss the concern. Acknowledge, explain, commit to a targeted optimization.
