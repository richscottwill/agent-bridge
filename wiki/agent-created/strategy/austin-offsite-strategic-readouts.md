# Austin Offsite Strategic Readouts

The paid acquisition team convened in Austin April 7-10 for OP1 planning, vendor syncs, and strategic workshops. Seven sessions produced content worth capturing. This document consolidates the key takeaways from each so the knowledge does not decay into individual Hedy transcripts.

## Adi Landing Page Optimization — April 8

Adi presented the landing page testing roadmap. Polaris brand migration is on track for AU, MX, and EU5. MCS now partnered to optimize the in-context registration start page. Mobile-specific friction points, particularly cropped hero images and non-localized headlines in CA, identified as the next test wave. The April 16 MCS decision to migrate all paid search LPs to Polaris flowed from this session — legacy PADESARJAD pages deprecated.

## OP1 Strategy Workshop — April 8

Ninety-six minutes of innovation brainstorming. Captured in full at [OP1 2027 Innovation Shortlist](op1-2027-innovation-shortlist). Key bets: MCS visitor retargeting, LiveRamp Enhanced Match, F90 activation channel, Paid App attribution shift, vertical-based audience segmentation, MIE, Baloo, deep linking with Ref tags.

## Sid Acquisition Channel Deep-Dive — April 9

Fifty-five minutes with Sid Sundaresan covering the full AB acquisition channel map. Captured at [Sid Acquisition Funnel Map](sid-acquisition-funnel-map). Key insight: on-site drives over fifty percent of new registrations, email declining year over year, push constrained at twenty percent of high-intent audience, AI search high-converting but no paid access. HEMM attribution model remains unimplemented despite multiple discussions.

## Google Search Summit — April 9

One hundred fifty-three minutes with Google reps. Three AI Max test methods surfaced. Smart Bidding Exploration showed nineteen percent lift in initial tests. Search Partner Network re-enabled for AB campaigns. YouTube retargeting capabilities discussed for the engagement account. AI Max sponsored ad inventory not yet attributable to AI slot visibility — AWS confirmed reporting gap.

## Jasper AI Demo — April 9

One hundred thirteen minutes on content workflow automation. Jasper demonstrated AI-powered content generation for ad copy, landing pages, and email. EU5 localization capability demoed. GRI analytics integration shown. Richard's takeaway: repurposing media team video assets is the highest-leverage near-term opportunity for the paid acquisition team, not net-new Jasper-generated content.

## Adobe Health Review — April 8

Sixty-five minutes with Jen Vitiello on Adobe health. Health score sixty-four percent. Alert volume and hybrid optimization patterns discussed. Adobe Bi-Weekly cadence confirmed. Post-OCI Adobe dependency expected to reduce; MIE (AI tool for paid search) positioned as the longer-term Adobe replacement path.

## Brandon Compensation Review — April 6

Eighteen minutes reviewing 2026 compensation and Annual Review outcome. Overall rating "Meets High Bar." LP strength called out. Growth area: visibility — consistent visibility across workstreams, lightweight mechanisms for timely communication, structured stakeholder documentation. Peer feedback flagged communication and project management. Same gaps as Forte 2025. Richard's January-April action plan should target these explicitly.

## What Austin did not resolve

Three items surfaced but were not closed. The Rachel/HEMM multi-touch attribution model still sits unimplemented despite multiple conversations. The APAC MCC access issue for OCI requires Google escalation. The Lena CPC challenge response framework remains reactive rather than proactive. Each belongs in the post-Austin action list.
