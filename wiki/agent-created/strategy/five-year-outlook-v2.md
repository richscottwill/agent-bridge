# Paid Search Five-Year Outlook: 2026–2030

## Purpose

This document presents the Paid Search team's five-year strategic outlook and the four investment bets for 2026 that build on validated 2025 results. It requests leadership endorsement of the 2026 test plan — AI Max, Baloo Shopping Ads, F90 lifecycle engagement, and agentic team tooling — all within existing budget allocation. Each bet has defined go/no-go gates and a fallback position.

The core thesis is twofold. Paid Search is transitioning from a keyword-driven acquisition channel to an automated, audience-centric engine — a shift already underway through OCI, campaign consolidation, and lifecycle engagement. Simultaneously, the broader agentic AI landscape is accelerating faster than most enterprise teams can absorb, and the PS team's existing multi-agent infrastructure positions it ahead of the adoption curve. The question is not whether these shifts happen, but how fast the external environment accelerates or constrains them.

## Executive Summary

OCI delivered 35,196 incremental registrations and over $16.7 million in OPS across US, UK, and DE in 2025. That validated foundation supports four 2026 bets, each with a clear read date and fallback:

| Bet | If it works | If it doesn't | When we know |
|-----|------------|---------------|--------------|
| AI Max (US Q2 test) | +14% conversions (Google aggregate), worldwide rollout 2027 | Continue OCI-optimized Search — proven foundation remains | Q3 2026 |
| Baloo Shopping Ads (US Q2 rollout) | New ad format, lower-friction registration at scale | Monitor 90-day quality metrics; revert to auth-first if quality degrades | Q4 2026 |
| F90 Lifecycle Engagement | 3+ purchase rate from 31.7% to 35.4% (+366 bps) | Channel already profitable ($765K iOPS 2025), continue at current scale | Monthly |
| Agentic team tooling (Level 3) | One tool adopted by a teammate, proving scalability | Infrastructure persists for team use; no incremental cost if adoption is slower than planned | Q4 2026 |

Beyond 2026, the outlook becomes progressively speculative. 2027 depends on 2026 test results. 2028 through 2030 is scenario planning grounded in external market signals — not operational commitments. The full speculative horizon is in Appendix C.

## 2026 — Validate and Scale (HIGH confidence)

Investments are committed, tests are designed, and the team is executing.

OCI is the proven foundation. Seven of ten markets run at 100% deployment as of late March 2026 — US, UK, and DE since Q4 2025, with FR, IT, ES, and JP dialed to 100% on March 30-31. CA targets April 7. AU and MX have no confirmed timelines and neither MCC has been created. The validated pattern — phased 25/50/100% rollout with seasonality-adjusted baselines — is replicable. The remaining markets are execution, not experimentation.

The UK ad copy test (January 29 through March 2) delivered 86% higher CTR and 31% more registrations at HIGH confidence, validating the shift from bulk and wholesale messaging to price, quality, and selection. EU4 translations are complete. Campaign consolidation continues as structural preparation for AI Max.

Project Baloo (early access March 30, broader US rollout Q2 2026) enables unauthenticated catalog browsing, unlocking Shopping Ads for AB for the first time. This is the largest structural change to the registration funnel since in-context registration delivered 13,600 annualized incremental registrations.

F90 Lifecycle Engagement extends PS from acquisition into activation. The Engagement channel drove $765K in iOPS in 2025 and $329K OPS at 644% ROAS during Prime Day 2025. Legal SIMs are in progress for the 3+ purchases targeting criteria.

AI Max US testing is planned for Q2 2026. Google's published aggregate shows 14% more conversions at similar CPA for early adopters (source: Nav43, 2025). AB's conversion event — completed registration with business verification — is structurally different from e-commerce purchase. The test will follow the same phased methodology as OCI.

Two external signals to monitor in 2026: AI Overviews now appear on 25 to 48% of Google queries and reduce organic CTR by 35 to 61% where they appear, though transactional queries like B2B registration appear partially protected (sources: Stackmatix, Conductor/Seer, 2026 Q1). Google launched Shopping Ads in AI Mode in January 2026 — currently US-only, Shopping only, no B2B registration format (source: SearchEngineJournal).

The agentic capability target is one tool adopted by a teammate (Level 3 gate). The WBR callout pipeline, already producing 10-market callouts, is the leading candidate.

## 2027 — Adapt to Platform Shifts (MEDIUM confidence)

Projections depend on 2026 test results we do not yet have.

By mid-2027, the team will have 12 months of AI Max data, full-year OCI results from all 10 markets, Baloo's impact on registration volume and quality, and the first AB-specific measurement of AI Overview appearance rates. These four data points determine whether 2027 accelerates or pivots.

If AI Max delivers, 2027 is worldwide rollout following the same phased methodology. AI Max replaced Dynamic Search Ads entirely in late 2025 (source: CasaccioMedia) and operates as keyword-free search using website content as the primary signal. The consolidated campaign structure built in 2026 becomes the input AI Max needs to perform.

The agentic capability target shifts to strategic authority (Level 4). The AEO POV, queued in 2026, should be published and influencing team strategy. The PS team's existing multi-agent infrastructure — wiki pipeline, callout pipeline, coaching agent, context management — positions it ahead of the enterprise adoption curve. Gartner projects 75% AI adoption in marketing by 2027 (source: Gartner 2024), but also predicts over 40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled by end of 2027 due to escalating costs (source: Gartner via McKinsey). The teams that survive build for sustainability, not hype.

Two signals to watch: whether Google extends AI Mode ads beyond Shopping to B2B registration formats, and whether procurement AI agents begin intermediating B2B discovery. Gartner projects $15 trillion in AI-intermediated B2B spending by 2028 (source: DigitalCommerce360, November 2025) — an aspirational projection, not a measurement. If procurement agents start recommending business supply platforms, AB's registration flow needs to be agent-discoverable.

### Downside across the speculative horizon (2027–2030)

The incremental approach means no single year's bet is existential. If AI Max underperforms worldwide, the team retains a proven OCI and consolidated Search foundation. If AI Overviews erode B2B search volume faster than expected, the lifecycle channel and Demand Gen (CPC of $0.39 versus $2.43 for keywords in 2025) become the primary growth vector. If the agentic AI cancellation wave hits, the PS team's infrastructure is low-cost, text-file-based, and does not depend on enterprise platform purchases. Each stage delivers standalone value regardless of what follows.

## Appendix C: Speculative Horizon (2028–2030)

Confidence: LOW. This section is scenario planning, not operational planning.

### 2028 — Structural Transition

The keyword-to-audience transition accelerates. If AI Max and Baloo perform as hypothesized, campaign structure simplifies dramatically — fewer campaigns, more signal-driven, with OCI providing the first-party conversion data that algorithmic systems need. The team manages outcomes (registration volume, CPA, post-registration purchasing) rather than inputs (keywords, bids, ad copy).

The broader agentic landscape reaches mainstream adoption for disciplined implementers. By 2028, 33% of enterprise software will incorporate agentic AI capabilities (source: Gartner via NXTCloud). The agentic AI market roughly triples from 2026 levels, with multi-agent systems commanding 53% market share (source: MarketsAndMarkets). The PS team transitions from Stage 2 (agent-executed, human-approved) to Stage 3 (agent-autonomous with guardrails) in the Five Stages framework.

Two signals to watch: whether UCP or ACP extends to B2B registration (neither currently supports it), and whether Google introduces outcome-based billing models. The UCP architecture — where AI agents complete transactions end-to-end — creates the technical foundation for outcome-based pricing, but no product exists today.

### 2029–2030 — Directional Vision

If the full trajectory holds, PS campaigns operate primarily on predicted registration probability signals rather than keyword matching. The distinction between search, display, and agent-mediated discovery blurs. The team manages a portfolio of automated campaign types and the "paid search" line item may become "agent visibility and conversion budget."

The agentic capability reaches Stage 4 (agent-orchestrated) by 2029 and approaches Stage 5 (self-improving system) by 2030. Multiple agents coordinate across functions — cross-market launches, competitive response, automated reporting — with the human role narrowing to strategic direction and quarterly goal-setting. The agentic AI market is projected at $52-57 billion by 2030-2031 (source: MarketsAndMarkets), roughly 6x the 2026 level.

Three signals worth monitoring across this horizon: whether agent-to-agent commerce materializes for B2B (procurement agents negotiating with marketing agents), whether the PS team's headcount model changes (same team, expanded scope), and whether Google ships outcome-based billing for registrations. None of these exist today. The methodology that validated OCI in 2025 is the same methodology that will validate whatever 2030 requires — evidence over intuition, phased rollout, go/no-go gates.
