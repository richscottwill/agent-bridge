# UK W13 Analysis Brief

## Registration summary
1,531 regs (+14% WoW), $86K spend (-5% WoW), $56 CPA (-17% WoW). Brand 577 (+31% WoW), NB 954 (+6% WoW). This is the highest weekly total in the 8-week window, 9% above the 8-week mean of 1,403.

## Why registrations changed
CVR was the primary driver: blended CVR jumped to 4.28% (+17% WoW), the highest in the 12-week window. Brand CVR recovered sharply from 2.62% to 3.37% (+29% WoW), reversing the multi-week decline that had pushed Brand to an 8-week low in W12. NB CVR also improved from 4.58% to 5.12% (+12% WoW). Total clicks were flat to slightly down (-2% WoW, 35,761 vs 36,476), so this was entirely a conversion story, not a traffic story. Spend fell -5% WoW ($86K vs $90K) as NB spend dropped -10% ($51K vs $57K) while Brand spend rose +5% ($35K vs $33K). The CVR surge compressed CPA across both segments: Brand CPA fell from $75 to $60 (-20% WoW), NB CPA from $64 to $54 (-16% WoW).

## Trend context
UK had been remarkably stable at 1,325-1,465 across W5-W12, with a slight downward drift from W9 (1,465) through W12 (1,338). W13 at 1,531 breaks above that range decisively. The Brand recovery is notable: Brand regs had declined from 605 (W5) to 442 (W12), a 27% drop over 8 weeks. W13's 577 Brand regs reverses roughly half of that decline in a single week. NB has been more stable (720-954 range), and W13's 954 is the highest NB total in the window. The CVR spike (4.28% blended) is well above the 8-week range of 3.67-3.91%. This needs monitoring to determine if it's a new baseline or a one-week anomaly.

## Relevant actions and events
- Change log shows tROAS increases on NB campaigns through W11-W12 (GA tROAS 12.5% to 13.75%, PV tROAS 12% to 13.2%, GA Top budget $2.2K to $2.5K on 3/16). These efficiency-focused adjustments may have reduced lower-quality NB clicks while improving CVR.
- Brand Plus budget was cut from $1.2K to $1K on 3/16. Despite this, Brand regs surged +31% WoW, suggesting the CVR improvement is organic demand, not spend-driven.
- Ad copy test Phase 2 launched 3/6 across all NB campaigns. W13 is the third full week post-launch. The sustained NB CVR improvement (4.58% to 5.12%) may reflect continued ad copy gains.
- OCI remains at 100% since Sep 2025. NB efficiency is mature.
- weareuncapped.com at 24% Brand IS since Dec 2023. Despite this, Brand CVR recovered from 2.62% to 3.37%. The competitor's impact may be stabilizing rather than worsening.
- No UK holidays in W13. Clean read.

## YoY assessment
+117% YoY regs (1,531 vs 704). +82% YoY spend ($86K vs $47K). NB regs +286% YoY, Brand +26% YoY. NB CPA improved -24% YoY ($54 vs $71). The YoY comp is strong because W13 2025 was weak (704 regs, -1% WoW LY). But even adjusting for the soft comp, the structural improvements are real: OCI was not live in W13 2025, the ad copy test had not run, and NB campaigns were less mature. The WoW pattern diverges from LY: TY +14% vs LY -1%, suggesting TY momentum is genuine and not seasonal. The YoY improvement is accelerating: W12 was +95% YoY, W13 is +117% YoY.

## Recommended W14 spend
$86K (maintain W13 level). UK is well ahead of OP2 on regs (+20%) while spending 14% less. The spend strategy is OP2 targets with OCI mature. April OP2 spend is $108K, implying ~$25K/week. W13's $86K run rate is below the monthly OP2 pace, so there's no reason to cut. If CVR sustains at 4.28%, the efficiency gains justify maintaining current spend. If CVR reverts toward the 3.7% 8-week average, CPA would rise to ~$65, still well below OP2's $89 implied CPA.

## Flags
- CVR spike: 4.28% blended CVR is 10% above the 8-week high (3.91% in W5) and 17% above the 8-week mean (3.76%). If this is a one-week anomaly, W14 regs could revert to ~1,350-1,400.
- Brand recovery: 577 Brand regs vs 442 LW is a +31% bounce. Brand CVR at 3.37% is the highest since W5 (3.28%). This partially reverses the 8-week Brand erosion trend, but one week doesn't confirm a reversal.
- NB CVR at 5.12% is the highest in the 12-week window. Combined with the ad copy test Phase 2 (launched 3/6), this may represent a structural improvement rather than noise.
- No data lag issues. Daily breakdown shows normal weekday/weekend pattern (168-245 range).

Brand bounced back +31% WoW after hitting an 8-week low in W12, while NB hit its own 8-week high. YoY: +117% regs on +82% spend.
