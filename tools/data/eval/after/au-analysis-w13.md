# AU W13 Analysis Brief

## Registration summary

AU drove 207 registrations (-16% WoW), the lowest weekly total in the 13-week trend. Spend fell to $24K (-13% WoW). CPA ticked up to $118 (+3% WoW), ending a 3-week improvement streak. March is projected to close at ~1,030 regs and ~$125K spend at $121 CPA. (vs. OP2: -15% spend, -5% registrations)

## Why registrations changed

The decline was CVR-driven, not volume-driven. Total CVR fell 12% WoW (4.24% to 3.73%) while clicks only declined 4% (5,785 to 5,557). Both segments dropped in tandem: Brand CVR fell 14% (8.54% to 7.37%) and NB CVR fell 10% (2.87% to 2.58%).

Brand regs dropped 18% (119 to 98) almost entirely on the CVR compression. Brand clicks were down only 5% (1,393 to 1,329). W12's Brand CVR of 8.54% was the highest in the 13-week trend; W13's 7.37% is a reversion toward the 8-week average of 7.5%. The W12 spike was likely noise or early promo sitelink novelty wearing off.

NB regs fell 13% (126 to 109) on the same CVR pattern: clicks down 4% (4,392 to 4,228), CVR down 10%. NB CPA held flat at $187 because CPC fell 10% WoW ($5.37 to $4.81), offsetting the CVR decline. NB CPC has now declined 7 consecutive weeks from the W6 peak of $6.82, a cumulative 29% reduction.

The daily pattern reveals a front-loaded week: Mon-Tue delivered 103 regs (50% of the weekly total) while Wed-Sat averaged only 20 regs/day. Saturday was particularly weak at 8 regs, the lowest single day in the available daily data. This mid-week collapse suggests a disruption event, likely the Polaris URL migration or residual .co.uk mismapping impact.

## Trend context

This is the 6th consecutive week of declining registrations (328 in W7 to 207 in W13, -37% cumulative). The first phase (W7-W10) was post-promo normalization. W10-W12 appeared to stabilize at 240-251. W13 breaks below that floor, suggesting a new factor beyond the promo wind-down.

The 8-week registration trend: W6: 257, W7: 328, W8: 257, W9: 256, W10: 251, W11: 241, W12: 245, W13: 207. W13 is 21% below the 8-week average of 262.

CPC continues its steady decline (7 consecutive weeks on NB), confirming the Adobe bid strategies are working. But the volume trade-off is steepening: W13 lost 16% of registrations while only saving 13% on spend, pushing CPA up for the first time in 3 weeks.

## Relevant actions and events

- Polaris URL migration (in progress): Lena confirmed full switch from old MCS to new Polaris pages. Implementation ongoing with Alexis and Lisa Moussa. The mid-week performance collapse (Wed-Sat) aligns with potential URL transition disruption. If keywords were pointed to new URLs mid-week, the bid strategies would need to re-learn conversion patterns on the new landing pages.
- .co.uk URL mismapping (flagged 3/15): Alexis flagged AU keywords redirecting to .co.uk URLs. Status of fix unclear. If affected campaigns were paused or underperforming during W13, this compounds the Polaris migration impact.
- Adobe bid strategy transition (ongoing since W3): NB CPC down 29% cumulative from W6 peak. The strategies continue to find lower-cost traffic, but the CVR decline in W13 suggests they may be reaching diminishing returns on efficiency, or the landing page changes are disrupting learned conversion signals.
- New acquisition promo sitelink ("20% off first order, up to AU$50"): Live since before W12. Brand CVR reverted from 8.54% to 7.37%, suggesting the W12 CVR spike was novelty-driven rather than sustained promo impact.
- Lena challenged CPC of $6 avg on 3/19: W13 CPC came in at $4.39, well below the $6 figure Lena referenced. The bid strategies have already addressed this concern.

That implies ~$34K/week over 4.3 weeks. Starting at $25K gives room to ramp if the Polaris migration stabilizes and CVR recovers, without overcommitting while landing page disruption may still be in play.

## Flags

- Registration 8-week low: 207 regs is 21% below the 8-week average of 262. This will draw WBR attention. The narrative should frame it as landing page migration disruption on top of the post-promo baseline, not a structural decline.
- Saturday collapse: 8 regs on Mar 28 is an outlier. Investigate whether campaigns were paused or URLs were broken on Saturday specifically.
- CVR reversal: Both Brand and NB CVR dropped 10-14% WoW after 3 weeks of stability. If this persists in W14, it points to a landing page quality issue rather than bid strategy noise.
- Spend 32% below 8-week average: $24.4K vs $35.6K avg. The bid strategies are compressing spend faster than planned. March will close ~$23K under OP2 spend.
- NB CPC 7-week decline streak: $6.82 to $4.81 (-29%). The bid strategies are clearly working on cost compression. The question is whether they can find volume at these lower price points.
