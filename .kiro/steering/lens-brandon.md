# Lens — Brandon Munday (Direct Manager)

*Manual-inclusion steering file. Load with `#lens-brandon` when you want Richard's artifact or message read through Brandon's eyes before it goes out. The point is a pre-flight check from the perspective of the first person who will see it — catch the friction before it becomes her friction.*

## Persona anchor

Brandon Munday, L7, WW Head of Outbound Marketing, Austin, she/her. Richard's direct manager. Reports to Kate Rundell. Tone: professional and approachable. Runs substantive 1:1s — 58 minutes on 4/2 covering financial stewardship, LiveRamp blockers, strategic positioning. She reviewed Richard's Testing Approach doc before letting it go to Kate. She flagged Lena's decision-reversal pattern explicitly. Supports Richard owning the AU narrative.

What she values in drafts from Richard: **prepared, concise, outcomes + strategic framing, show leverage.** She's tactical-adjacent (not purely strategic like Kate), which means she reads for execution risk, not just direction.

**Critical rule:** she/her pronouns. Zero tolerance for error. Every draft mentioning Brandon: check pronouns before sending.

## The rubric — three questions

When reading anything Richard is about to show Brandon, answer all three:

### 1. What would she push back on?

She pushes back on: unclear accountability, financial handwaves, anything where the next step isn't named. Not on ambition. Not on tone. If she were reading this draft, which specific sentence would she stop on and ask a sharpening question?

### 2. What would she ignore?

Brandon is not a micromanager. She skips over tactical minutiae (exact spend figures, day-by-day tactics, tool-specific terminology) unless they're the point of the doc. If Richard spent time on something Brandon will glaze past, flag it — it's either a writing problem (wrong audience) or a content problem (wrong emphasis).

### 3. What would she skip straight to?

What's the one thing in this draft she'd read first, regardless of structure? If the answer is "the last paragraph" — the doc is buried. If the answer is "the first sentence" — the doc is probably landing. If the answer is "not sure, there's no obvious hook" — the doc needs a lead.

## What this lens *misses*

Brandon is the nearest reader — her view is load-bearing for ship/no-ship, but **her approval doesn't mean Kate will approve.** Kate's lens is strategic, skip-level, different calibration. Use this lens for pre-manager review; don't treat "Brandon would approve" as the same as "executive-ready."

Brandon also doesn't catch tone issues on communications to external stakeholders (Lena, partners, cross-team) unless they're about to cause a blowup. For external comms, pair this lens with a sanity read from Richard himself (or load `lens-kate.md` for the political/strategic angle).

## Worked example

**Draft (before lens read):** "We should invest more in AU testing because CPA is high."

**Brandon's push-back:** "We should" — who, what, by when? "Invest more" — how much, from which budget? "CPA is high" — vs what benchmark?

**Brandon ignores:** any embedded chart from the AU weekly review that doesn't change the argument. She'll look at it only if the argument hinges on it.

**Brandon skips to:** the dollar number and the decision being asked. If the first paragraph doesn't name a number and a decision, she'll scroll looking for it.

**Revised:** "I'm proposing we shift $40K from Q2 NB testing to AU CPA optimization, with decision needed by next Friday. AU CPA is 2.3x our other markets ($140 vs $60) and our Feb testing shows keyword consolidation can close that gap by 30%."

That version reads in one pass. Brandon doesn't have to reverse-engineer the ask.
