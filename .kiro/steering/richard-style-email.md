# Richard Williams — Email Style Guide






## Email (Operational / Coordination)
- **Structure:** Context sentence → action/ask → offer to help or flag risk
- **Length:** 2-6 sentences typical. Rarely more than 2 short paragraphs.
- **Data:** Inline, specific (dollar amounts, PO numbers, dates)
- **Attachments:** Referenced naturally — "have the keyword data attached here"
- **Purpose:** Move work forward, unblock people, coordinate logistics






### Reply Patterns
- When replying to a numbered list, uses the same numbering: "1-3: ..." / "4-5: ..." / "6: ..."




### Redirect Patterns
- When forwarding or redirecting, always name the person and briefly explain why: "Looping in Yun-Kang — she owns the redirect reporting for MX."






### Example (actual — MX invoice)
"Hey all, here are the two invoices in question if that's helpful. (most up-to-date from the platform) Like Carlos mentioned, it would be helpful if January's invoice ($65K) could be paid with PO MS-20200908, and December's invoice ($39K) could be split between PO MS-18899171 and remainder to PO MS-19697814. Definitely let us know if you need any work from our side to resolve."






## Email (Analytical / Update)
- Purpose: Share findings, explain performance, provide recommendations
- Length: Longer — can be a full page with data tables

 


### Structure
- Summary → data → interpretation → recommendation






### Data Presentation
- Tables, percentages, comparisons (vs OP2, YoY, test vs control)
- Always includes "so what" — doesn't just present data, interprets it






### Confidence & Caveats
- Flag data limitations: "2 weeks of data — directional only" or "LOW confidence, <100 events"
- Separate what you know from what you recommend: findings first, then interpretation, then ask


## Email Writing Craft






### Stakeholder Management (Lena, Nick)
- Numbered responses matching their numbered asks
- Leads with what's been done, then addresses what's next
- States positions as recommendations, not demands
- Proposes alternatives when pushing back: "Also can you confirm you'd rather switch all to the new pages rather than doing a 50/50 traffic split? (I'd recommend sending half of the traffic to MCS and half to Polaris-style pages with different reftags, and this wouldn't lengthen the implementation.)"
- Flags risks and trade-offs plainly

Example (actual — reply to Lena): "In terms of the responses you laid out below, thank you yes we will not be bidding on perishables/alcohol moving forward. We did a check on the keywords as well as adding negatives. On the point about landing pages; there's a risk to performance, and we'll still want to plan to move to paid search specific pages in the future, but we can definitely test."






### Openers & Framing
- "I wanted to reach out because..." — natural opener for external/new-thread emails. Warmer than jumping straight into the topic.
- "heard about X as a solution" — when asking about something new, frame it as something you heard about, not something you deeply researched. Lower-stakes positioning that invites the other person to teach you.
- When asking questions, validate your understanding first ("From what I can tell..."), then ask if you got it right. Don't fire cold questions without showing you did some thinking.






### Transitions & Audience - "In terms of" as a transition phrase; "Also" to add a secondary point within the same paragraph - Conditional offers: "Definitely let us know if you need any work from our side" - "we" not "I" when requesting resources or materials from external partners. Team framing, not individual. - Keep internal questions out of external emails. Audience size drops, regional availability, internal blockers — those go to Abdul or the internal thread, not to the vendor rep. 



### Data & Reference Patterns
- Parenthetical data drops throughout
- Recommends with reasoning attached
- References shared docs/attachments rather than re-explaining inline
- When redirecting, names the person and briefly explains why






## Common Draft Failures






### Tone & Language Failures






#### Naming & Specificity
| # | Failure | Fix |
|---|---------|-----|
| 1 | Teams instead of people | Name the person: "I'll connect with Yun-Kang on redirect reporting." Unknown? Say so. |
| 2 | Vague timelines | Specific: "by EOW" or "target Friday 4/11." Uncertain? Best estimate + flag dependency. |






#### Stakeholder & Register
| # | Failure | Fix |
|---|---------|-----|
| 4 | Political subtext missed (Tier 3: Lena, Nick) | Check memory.md. Tone: measured, in-control. Brandon's private reads inform tone but never surface. |
| 5 | Corporate jargon ("delta," "scoping," "leverage") | Plain language. Simpler word wins. |
| 6 | "Love" in emails | Never. "I'd appreciate" or ask directly. |






### Formatting & Structure Failures
| # | Failure | Fix |
|---|---------|-----|
| 3 | Em-dashes (—) | Replace with commas, periods, colons, parentheticals. Scan every draft. |
| 7 | Wrong sign-off | Two lines: "Thank you," then "Richard." Never one line. |
