# IT W12 Analysis Brief

## Registration summary
1,273 regs (-12% WoW), $78K spend (-17% WoW), $61 CPA (-6% WoW). Brand 924 (-10% WoW), NB 349 (-16% WoW). Projected: 5,650 regs, $381K spend vs OP2 5,563 regs, $384K spend (+2% regs, -1% spend).

## Why registrations changed
CVR drove the decline more than clicks. CVR fell -8% WoW (2.43% vs 2.65%) while clicks fell -4%. Both segments declined: Brand CVR -7% (2.86% vs 3.08%), NB CVR -11% (1.74% vs 1.96%). NB was hit harder on both CVR and clicks (-5%). The CVR-led decline is notable because W11 was a strong rebound week (1,443 regs, 2.65% CVR) after W10's dip (1,157 regs, 2.12% CVR). W12 is a partial giveback of the W11 bounce.

## Trend context
IT has been volatile: 1,157-1,443 range over the last 4 weeks (W9-W12), with a 286-reg swing between the low and high. The 8-week average is 1,301; W12 at 1,273 is slightly below average. The pattern is oscillating rather than trending: W10 dip → W11 bounce → W12 partial giveback. CVR tells the same story: 2.12% (W10) → 2.65% (W11) → 2.43% (W12). IT lacks the stability of UK or FR.

## Relevant actions and events
1. OCI E2E launched 2/26. Three weeks in, no measurable conversion lift yet. Full impact projected Jul 2026.
2. Ad copy test ran Feb 19 - Mar 5 but produced low confidence results: +15% CTR directionally, but -97% clicks vs control. Volume was insufficient. The test needs redesign or longer duration.
3. Shopify/mondoffice on NB Generic with >10% IS, associated with -20% CVR on Generic terms. NB CVR at 1.74% is the lowest in the 8-week window. This competitor is a real drag on NB performance.
4. revolut.com appeared on Brand Core in W10, causing a -36% CVR drop. Brand CVR has partially recovered (2.86% in W12 vs the W10 trough) but remains below the W5-W6 baseline of ~3.0%.
5. Cross-market pattern: IT's -12% decline is the second-steepest in EU5 after DE (-18%). Unlike DE, IT has no data lag issue. The decline is real.

## YoY assessment
+4% YoY regs (1,273 vs 1,225). +30% YoY spend ($78K vs $60K). Brand +13% YoY, NB -14% YoY. NB CPA +93% YoY ($136 vs $70). The NB picture is poor: fewer regs at nearly double the CPA. Brand is carrying the YoY growth. LY W12 was -9% WoW; TY's -12% is slightly worse than the seasonal pattern.

## Monthly projection
Projected 5,650 regs vs OP2 5,563 (+2%). Projected $381K spend vs OP2 $384K (-1%). Remaining 10 days: 6 weekdays, 4 weekend days. IT has an unusual daily pattern where Sat (235) was the highest day in W12, so weekend rates are higher than typical EU markets. Used blended daily rates (weekday ~175, weekend ~195) with a -3% seasonal discount for mid-March softness. IT is tracking right on plan. The volatility (W10 dip, W11 bounce, W12 giveback) is averaging out to plan-level performance. OCI E2E is 3 weeks in with no measurable lift; the projection assumes no OCI benefit in the remaining 10 days.

## Flags
- NB CPA: $136 is +93% YoY and the highest NB CPA in EU5 relative to its YoY change. Shopify/mondoffice competition is the primary driver. OCI maturation is the only realistic path to improvement.
- NB CVR: 1.74% is an 8-week low. The Shopify/mondoffice -20% CVR impact on Generic is structural.
- Ad copy test: Low confidence results mean no actionable learnings. The test needs to be redesigned with sufficient volume allocation.
- Volatility: 286-reg swing over 4 weeks makes weekly reads noisy. Focus on monthly trajectory.
- Daily pattern: Sat (235 regs) was the highest day, Mon (125) the lowest. This is unusual and may indicate delayed conversion attribution from the prior week.

## Suggested narrative angle
IT gave back part of W11's bounce, declining -12% WoW on CVR compression across both segments. NB CVR hit an 8-week low (1.74%) under Shopify/mondoffice pressure. Monthly projection: +2% regs vs OP2, right on plan. The weekly volatility is noise; the monthly trajectory is stable.
