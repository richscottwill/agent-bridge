# Workstream 5: Algorithmic Ads

This document explains how the Paid Search team tested and scaled algorithmic ad formats — Demand Gen and the upcoming AI Max — to extend reach beyond traditional keyword campaigns. It provides the evidence base for the 2026 AI Max test. After reading, you should understand the proven Demand Gen results, the AI Max test design and guardrails, and the dependencies on the Modern Search workstream.

## What opportunity do algorithmic ads address?

Traditional keyword campaigns only reach users who search for terms in our keyword list. This creates a ceiling — we can optimize bids and copy within that ceiling (see Workstreams 1 and 2), but we cannot reach users whose search behavior signals business purchasing intent without matching our exact keywords. Algorithmic ad formats break through that ceiling by using Google's machine learning to identify relevant audiences and placements beyond manual keyword targeting.

By 2025, Demand Gen had matured into a proven channel after two years of iteration on mid-funnel ad formats and audience targeting.

## What did Demand Gen deliver?

Demand Gen ads drove cost-efficient traffic at a fraction of keyword campaign costs. The 2025 average CPC was $0.39 for Demand Gen versus $2.43 for keyword campaigns — an 84% reduction. This is not a volume play; it is an efficiency play that extends PS reach into mid-funnel visual placements where keyword campaigns cannot compete.

The efficiency gains compounded in Q4 2025. The channel delivered 53% more traffic YoY while spending 35% less, with CPC improving 58% YoY. More traffic at less cost — the channel scales without proportional budget increases (see Appendix: Q4 2025 Performance).

Prime Day 2025 validated Demand Gen at scale — $329K OPS at 644% ROAS, a 12x YoY improvement. This confirmed that visual ad formats deliver during peak events, not just in steady-state.

The Engagement account also enabled the Business Essentials launch through DG image placements. BSE validated that DG economics hold for new product launches — 52K visitors at $0.30 CPC in the first year. BSE video launched in January 2026, replicating the proven DG image approach with short video creative and increased image variations developed in collaboration with the Creative team (Raven Smith, CeCe Ramey). Early testing shows video CPCs in line with image asset CPCs at $0.30, indicating strong efficiency potential.

## What is AI Max and why does it matter?

AI Max solves the keyword ceiling problem differently than Demand Gen. Where DG extends reach through visual placements in mid-funnel surfaces, AI Max extends reach within search itself — showing text ads to users whose queries signal business intent without matching our exact keywords. A search for "office supplies for small company" could trigger an AB ad even if that exact phrase is not in our keyword list. This expands the addressable audience beyond what manual keyword targeting can reach.

AI Max requires consolidated campaign structures to function effectively — the Modern Search consolidation work (Workstream 2) directly enables this test. Without consolidated structures, AI Max cannot aggregate sufficient signal to optimize effectively.

## How is the team managing AI Max risk?

The team is developing the AI Max test design for a planned US test in Q2 2026, following the same measurement discipline used for OCI — clear baselines, phased rollout, incrementality benchmarks. Guardrail thresholds and rollout parameters are proposed in Appendix C; the team will finalize these when it commits to a launch timeline.

Two specific risks require guardrails. The first is cannibalization of existing keyword performance — AI Max's expanded matching could bid on queries already covered by existing campaigns, inflating costs without incremental registrations. The second is budget inflation from broad matching — without controls, expanded reach could consume budget on low-intent queries that drive clicks but not registrations. The guardrail design needs to detect both within the first two weeks of the test.

The team is coordinating with other Amazon teams (Hydra/Retail PS, Daniel Carmody's team) and Google (Mike Babich) to avoid internal auction overlap — if both teams run AI Max simultaneously without coordination, internal auction overlap could inflate CPCs for both. The dependency on Modern Search consolidation means any delays in campaign restructuring directly delay AI Max readiness. If the US test validates efficiency and incremental growth, the learnings will inform a broader worldwide rollout strategy.

|--------|-----------|-------------------|------------|
| CPC (2025 avg) | $0.39 | $2.43 | -84% |

The 84% CPC reduction reflects the fundamental difference in how these channels operate. Keyword campaigns compete in search auctions with direct competitors. Demand Gen places visual ads in mid-funnel placements (YouTube, Discover, Gmail) where competition dynamics are different and CPCs are structurally lower.

### Appendix C: AI Max test parameters (proposed)

The AI Max US test follows the same phased approach validated by OCI — start small, measure, expand only with evidence.

Scope: Begin with 1-2 high-volume US NB campaigns where consolidated keyword structures are already in place (prerequisite from Workstream 2). Brand campaigns are excluded. The initial campaign set should have sufficient conversion volume (30+ registrations/month) for the algorithm to learn within the measurement window.

Phased rollout: Phase 1 enables AI Max on the selected campaigns for 4 weeks. Phase 2 expands to all US NB campaigns for 4 weeks. Phase 3 evaluates worldwide expansion based on US results. Each phase has a go/no-go gate before proceeding.

Baselines: Capture 4-week pre-test baselines on CPA, registration volume, CPC, search term quality, and impression share for both the test campaigns and adjacent campaigns (to detect cannibalization).

Cannibalization guardrail: Monitor adjacent keyword campaigns weekly. If CPA on existing campaigns rises >10% with no corresponding increase in incremental registrations across the portfolio, pause AI Max and investigate query overlap. The signal is cost inflation without volume growth.

Budget inflation guardrail: Review query-level data weekly. If >30% of AI Max clicks come from queries with <1% CVR, tighten targeting or add negative keywords. The signal is reach expansion into low-intent territory.

Phase gate criteria: Proceed from Phase 1 to Phase 2 if incremental registrations are positive and blended CPA (test + adjacent campaigns) is within 120% of baseline. If blended CPA exceeds 120% for 7+ consecutive days, pause and investigate before expanding.

Coordination: Confirm with Hydra/Retail PS (Daniel Carmody) that no overlapping AI Max tests are running on the same query space. Confirm with Google (Mike Babich) that the campaign structure meets AI Max requirements.
