## Dimension-by-dimension

### First Paragraph Test (8/10)

The opening paragraph does three things well: it names the two formats (Demand Gen, AI Max), states the purpose (extend reach beyond keyword campaigns), and tells the reader what they'll walk away with (proven DG results, AI Max test design and guardrails, Modern Search dependencies). A reader scanning the first paragraph knows whether this doc is for them within 15 seconds.

What keeps it from a 9: the phrase "provides the evidence base for the 2026 AI Max test" is slightly passive. The doc does more than provide evidence — it lays out the test design itself. The purpose statement undersells the appendix, which is the most actionable part of the document.

### Shareability (8/10) This reads like a document you could forward to Kate's skip-level or a peer on the Hydra team and they'd follow the logic without needing a verbal walkthrough. The question-based headers create a natural narrative arc: what's the opportunity → what's proven → what's next → how do we manage risk. That structure carries a reader who doesn't live in this workstream daily. The cross-references to Workstreams 1 and 2 are handled cleanly — parenthetical, not disruptive. A reader who hasn't read those docs still follows the argument; a reader who has gets the connection. One friction point: the BSE paragraph in the Demand Gen section introduces Raven Smith and CeCe Ramey without context on why the Creative team collaboration matters to the reader. For someone outside the immediate team, those names are noise. For someone inside, they're useful attribution. Net: minor, doesn't break shareability. ### Actionability (8/10)

The AI Max test parameters appendix is where this doc earns its keep. Scope, phased rollout, baselines, two named guardrails with specific thresholds (CPA >10%, CVR <1% on >30% of clicks), phase gate criteria (120% blended CPA for 7+ days), and named coordination contacts. Someone could take Appendix C and build a project plan from it.

The Demand Gen sections are less actionable by design — they're retrospective evidence, not forward-looking instructions. That's appropriate for their purpose (building the case for AI Max), but it means roughly half the doc is "understand this" rather than "do this." For Kate reading the workstream detail behind the Testing Approach doc, the DG evidence is context, not action. It earns its place, but the actionability score reflects that the doc is split between evidence and execution.

One gap: the doc says "guardrail thresholds and rollout parameters are being defined" in the body text, then the appendix provides specific thresholds. The body text undersells what the appendix delivers. A reader who stops at the body (and some will) walks away thinking the guardrails are still TBD.

### Signal-to-Noise (7/10)

This is the weakest dimension, and it's what keeps the composite below 8.

The core issue: the appendix re-narrates data that already appears in the body. The Q4 2025 performance numbers (53% traffic, -35% spend, -58% CPC) appear in the "What did Demand Gen deliver?" section and then again in Appendix B with an interpretation paragraph that largely restates the body's conclusion. The Prime Day figure ($329K OPS, 644% ROAS, 12x YoY) appears in the body and again in the Appendix B interpretation. A reader who reads both encounters the same numbers and the same conclusions twice.

The appendix tables themselves are fine — they serve as reference lookups. But the interpretation paragraphs beneath them should add new insight, not restate the body. Appendix A's interpretation paragraph ("The 84% CPC reduction reflects the fundamental difference...") does add a new angle — explaining why the CPC gap exists structurally. Appendix B's interpretation paragraph mostly echoes the body with one new detail (LiveRamp integration, creative optimization as drivers). That one new detail is worth a sentence, not a full paragraph.

Specific signal-to-noise issues:

- Appendix B interpretation: "The 53% traffic increase at 35% lower spend means the cost-per-visit improved by approximately 60%" — this is arithmetic the reader already did when they read the body. Cut or compress to one sentence that adds the LiveRamp/creative driver detail.
- Appendix B interpretation: "Prime Day 2025 was the peak validation event within this period — Engagement campaigns delivered $329K in OPS at 644% ROAS, a 12x improvement over the prior year" — verbatim repeat of body text. Remove from appendix.
- The Appendix C "Scope" paragraph's parenthetical "(prerequisite from Workstream 2)" repeats a dependency stated in both the AI Max section and the coordination paragraph. Third mention of the same dependency.

None of these are fatal. They're the difference between a 7 and an 8 on this dimension — tightening the appendix interpretations would recover roughly 80-100 words of redundancy.

### Voice (8/10)

The writing is confident without being promotional. Sentences like "This is not a volume play; it is an efficiency play" and "More traffic at less cost — the channel scales without proportional budget increases" have a directness that reads as someone who owns the numbers explaining what they mean. No hedging, no corporate filler.

The question-based headers ("What opportunity do algorithmic ads address?") give the doc a conversational structure that works well for the audience — it reads like someone anticipating and answering the questions Kate would ask.

The AI Max risk section is particularly well-voiced. It names two specific risks, explains the mechanism of each, and states what the guardrail needs to detect. No hand-waving about "various risks" or "potential challenges."

One minor voice note: the phrase "indicating strong efficiency potential" at the end of the BSE video sentence is the one moment where the voice slips into marketing-speak. The rest of the doc lets numbers speak; this sentence editorializes where it could just state the fact ($0.30 CPC, matching image performance).

## Composite: 7.8/10

**Verdict: REVISE — does not ship at 7.8.**

The doc is close. Four dimensions at 8, one at 7. The gap is narrow and mechanical — it's a signal-to-noise problem in the appendix, not a structural or voice problem.

## Required changes

1. **Appendix B interpretation — cut the Prime Day repeat.** The sentence beginning "Prime Day 2025 was the peak validation event within this period" is a near-verbatim repeat of the body. Remove it entirely from the appendix interpretation. The body already made this point; the appendix table already contains the data.

2. **Appendix B interpretation — compress the arithmetic.** Replace "The 53% traffic increase at 35% lower spend means the cost-per-visit improved by approximately 60%. This is driven by improved audience targeting (LiveRamp integration) and creative optimization (image variations)." with a single sentence that leads with the drivers: "LiveRamp audience integration and creative optimization (image variations) drove the efficiency gain — 60% improvement in cost-per-visit." The reader doesn't need the arithmetic re-derived; they need the causal explanation.

3. **Body text — fix the undersell of guardrails.** The sentence "Guardrail thresholds and rollout parameters are being defined as part of the test design and will be documented when the team commits to a launch timeline" contradicts Appendix C, which already defines specific thresholds. Replace with: "Appendix C details the proposed guardrail thresholds and phased rollout parameters; the team will finalize these when it commits to a launch timeline." This directs the reader to the appendix instead of implying the work hasn't been done.

## Suggestions (non-blocking)

- The BSE paragraph's "indicating strong efficiency potential" could be tightened to "matching image-tier efficiency" — let the $0.30 number do the work.
- Consider whether the Workstream 2 dependency needs three mentions (AI Max section, risk section, Appendix C scope). Two is sufficient — one in the AI Max section to establish the dependency, one in Appendix C scope as a prerequisite. The risk section mention could reference the dependency without re-explaining it.
