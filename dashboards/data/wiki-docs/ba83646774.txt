# Paid Search Five-Year Outlook: 2026–2030

This document projects the trajectory of Amazon Business Paid Search from 2026 through 2030. It is written for Kate Rundell and Brandon Munday to support OP1 planning and multi-year investment decisions. After reading it, you will understand what is grounded in evidence, what is a committed bet, and what is speculative — and what we do differently if the speculative scenarios don't materialize.

The core thesis: Paid Search is transitioning from a keyword-driven acquisition channel to an automated, audience-centric engine. OCI, campaign consolidation, and lifecycle engagement are the first phase. AI Max, Shopping Ads via Baloo, and agentic operations are the second. The external environment — AI Overviews, agentic commerce protocols, procurement AI — will determine the pace. Our job is to build the infrastructure that compounds regardless of which external scenario plays out.

Confidence degrades honestly by year. 2026 is almost entirely grounded in validated results and committed investments (HIGH confidence). 2027 is grounded in 2026 outcomes with committed bets (MEDIUM confidence). 2028 through 2030 is scenario planning informed by external signals (LOW confidence). The appendix carries all supporting data, source citations, and methodology detail.

## 2026 — Validate and Scale

*Confidence: HIGH — investments committed, tests designed, team executing.*

### What we know

OCI delivered 35,196 incremental registrations and $16.7MM in OPS across US, UK, and DE in 2025. The US saw +24% registration uplift and approximately 50% NB CPA improvement. UK delivered +23% lift with 2,400 incremental registrations. DE delivered +18% lift with 749 incremental registrations. Seven of ten markets are at 100% OCI deployment: US, UK, DE, FR, IT, ES, and JP. The validated pattern — keyword-level end-to-end testing through phased 25/50/100% rollout with seasonality-adjusted baselines — is replicable across remaining markets. [source: eyes.md, Kate Testing Approach doc; confidence: HIGH]

Modern Search consolidation strengthened the data signals OCI requires to optimize. The UK ad copy test (Jan 29 through Mar 2) delivered +86% CTR and +31% registrations at HIGH confidence, validating the messaging shift from bulk/wholesale to price/quality/selection. That shift was grounded in the Sole Proprietor study (Aug 2025), which found 50% of SPs believed AB required bulk purchasing. EU4 translations are complete via GlobalLink. Campaign consolidation continues as structural preparation for AI Max. [source: eyes.md; confidence: HIGH]

Project Baloo launched early access on March 30. Unauthenticated catalog exploration before registration unlocks Shopping Ads for AB — a format previously unavailable because AB required authentication before product browsing. US Q2 2026 is the target for broader rollout. A noindex blocker (V2083026891) is flagged but not impacting SEO while behind VPN. This is the single largest structural change to the registration funnel since in-context registration, which delivered +13,600 annualized incremental registrations at 100% probability via APT. [source: current.md, eyes.md; confidence: HIGH for launch, MEDIUM for impact]

F90 Lifecycle Engagement extends PS from acquisition into activation. The goal is increasing non-SHuMA 3+ purchase rate from 31.7% to 35.4% (+366 basis points). The Engagement channel already drove $765K in iOPS in 2025 and $329K OPS at 644% ROAS during Prime Day 2025. Legal SIMs are in progress for the 3+ purchases targeting criteria. [source: Kate Testing Approach doc; confidence: HIGH]

The competitive landscape is intensifying. In the US, Walmart Business Brand impression share climbed from 25% to 55% at peak (W6 2026), pushing Brand CPA from approximately $40 to $65-77. Market-wide, competitor density has grown from one to two competitors per market in 2023 to three to five in 2026. The team's posture is efficiency over escalation — we do not chase impression share at unsustainable CPAs. [source: eyes.md; confidence: HIGH]

### What we are betting on

AI Max US testing is planned for Q2 2026. Google claims 14% more conversions at similar CPA for early adopters, but that figure is an aggregate across retail and lead-gen advertisers. AB's conversion event — completed registration with business verification — is structurally different. The test design has not been written yet. The approach will follow the same phased methodology used for OCI: clear baseline, controlled rollout, go/no-go gates. AI Max is not a replacement for OCI. It is a complementary layer that benefits from consolidated campaign structure and stronger data signals. [source: eyes.md, current.md; confidence: MEDIUM — no AB-specific data]

CA targets April 7 for full OCI deployment, with monthly registration impact expected by July. AU targets May 2026 pending MCC creation. MX has no timeline. These are execution, not experimentation — the methodology is proven, the question is operational readiness. [source: Kate Testing Approach doc; confidence: HIGH for CA/AU, LOW for MX]

The agentic capability target for 2026 is one tool adopted by a teammate — moving from "Richard uses agents" to "the team uses agents." Candidates include the WBR callout pipeline (already producing 10-market callouts), campaign link generator, or budget forecast helper. The constraint is not technical capability but team adoption of non-traditional workflows. [source: device.md, agentic-ps-vision.md; confidence: HIGH for capability, MEDIUM for adoption]

### What we are watching

AI Overview appearance rates on AB's top NB keywords. The AEO POV identifies this as the first measurement priority. Industry data shows AI Overviews reduce organic CTR by 35-61% where they appear, but B2B transactional queries ("create amazon business account") may be partially protected — AI Overviews rarely appear for transactional keywords. We do not have AB-specific data yet. The measurement plan — tracking top 50 NB keywords monthly — starts this year. Until that data exists, any projection about search volume shifts is speculation. [source: AEO POV, Stackmatix 2026, Conductor/Seer 2026; confidence: MEDIUM for industry data, LOW for AB-specific impact]

Google's AI Mode ads experiment. Google launched Shopping Ads in AI Mode on January 11, 2026, with formal announcement by Vidhya Srinivasan (VP Ads & Commerce) on February 11. Currently US-only, English, mobile and desktop, labeled "experiment." Ad placement logic is layered on semantic understanding of the entire conversation thread, not just a single query. No B2B registration ad format exists in AI Mode yet. [source: Ayko, SearchEngineJournal, PPC.land; confidence: MEDIUM — product exists, no B2B use case]

Baloo's impact on registration quality. Lower-friction registration could mean higher volume but lower intent. The measurement framework needs to track 90-day post-registration purchasing behavior, not just registration CPA. [source: internal analysis; confidence: MEDIUM]

### What changes if we are wrong

If AI Max underperforms on B2B registration campaigns, we continue with the OCI-plus-consolidation approach that is already delivering double-digit uplift. The downside is limited — we lose the test period investment but retain the structural improvements (consolidated campaigns, stronger data signals) that benefit any bidding approach.

If Baloo's unauthenticated browsing degrades registration quality, we tighten the funnel by adding qualification signals before the registration prompt. The Shopping Ads opportunity would narrow but not disappear.

If AI Overviews expand aggressively into B2B transactional queries in 2026 (earlier than expected), the mitigation is the AEO action plan: structured landing pages for AI extraction, early testing of ads-in-AI-Overviews when available, and accelerated investment in lifecycle channels less affected by zero-click. The F90 investment is already a hedge against this scenario.

## 2029 — Operate in the Hybrid Channel

*Confidence: LOW — scenario planning with multiple branching paths.*

### What we know (projected)

By 2029, the search landscape will have bifurcated. Some portion of business buyers will still use traditional keyword search — typing queries, clicking ads, navigating landing pages. Another portion will use conversational AI interfaces — asking agents for recommendations, receiving curated options, completing actions within the AI environment. The ratio between these two modes is unknowable today. What we can project is that AB PS will need to operate effectively in both modes simultaneously. [confidence: LOW for the ratio, MEDIUM for the bifurcation itself]

The agentic capability should be at Stage 4 — agent-orchestrated. Multiple agents coordinate across functions: cross-market campaign launches, competitive response playbooks, automated reporting and anomaly resolution. Human effort concentrates on strategic direction, stakeholder communication, and exception handling. The execution-to-strategy ratio target is 10/90. [source: agentic-ps-vision.md; confidence: LOW — depends on three years of compounding capability]

Three full years of AI Overview data will reveal the actual trajectory for B2B queries. The industry's initial panic (35-61% organic CTR reduction) will have been calibrated against AB-specific reality. The strategic response will be mature and evidence-based, not reactive. [confidence: MEDIUM for having the data, LOW for predicting the outcome]

### What we are betting on

The registration flow is fully agent-accessible. Business buyers using AI procurement tools can discover AB, understand the value proposition, and initiate registration through agent-to-agent interaction. This does not require AB to adopt UCP or ACP specifically — it requires structured data, API endpoints, and machine-readable content that any agent framework can consume. The investment is in making AB's registration flow a first-class citizen in the agentic web, not in betting on a specific protocol. [source: UCP/ACP analysis, AEO POV Level 4; confidence: LOW — directional, not specific]

Paid search bidding operates on conversation-intent signals, not keyword-level signals. If Google's AI Mode ads have expanded to B2B by this point, the bidding model will have shifted from "what keyword did the user type" to "what is the user trying to accomplish across a multi-turn conversation." OCI's first-party conversion signals become even more valuable in this model — they tell Google's system what a successful registration looks like, regardless of the query path that led there. [source: Google AI Mode architecture analysis; confidence: LOW]

The team's agentic infrastructure is a competitive advantage, not just an efficiency tool. Other marketing teams that started their agentic journey in 2027-2028 (after the hype cycle correction) are two to three years behind. The compounding effect of early investment — not just in tools but in workflows, guardrails, and institutional knowledge — creates a moat that is difficult to replicate quickly. [source: agentic-ps-vision.md; confidence: MEDIUM for the advantage, LOW for the magnitude]

### What we are watching

Protocol convergence or fragmentation. By 2029, the UCP/ACP landscape will have either consolidated into a dominant standard (like HTTP for the web) or fragmented into platform-specific silos (like mobile app stores). If convergence, AB needs to support the winning protocol. If fragmentation, AB needs a protocol-agnostic agent interface. We are watching for industry standards bodies, major retailer adoption patterns, and Google/OpenAI partnership or competition dynamics. [confidence: LOW — too many variables]

The regulatory environment for AI-intermediated commerce. Governments are likely to regulate AI purchasing agents — disclosure requirements, liability frameworks, consumer protection extensions to B2B. Any regulation that requires AI agents to disclose their recommendations or that holds platforms liable for agent-driven purchases will reshape the agentic commerce landscape. We are watching for EU AI Act enforcement patterns and US regulatory proposals. [confidence: LOW — regulatory timelines are unpredictable]

Internal organizational evolution. If Kate's org adopts agentic operations broadly (not just PS), the team's role shifts from "PS team that uses agents" to "the team that built the agentic playbook for the org." This is the Level 5 visibility play — demonstrating that the methodology scales beyond one function. [source: agentic-ps-vision.md; confidence: LOW for timing, MEDIUM for direction]

### What changes if we are wrong

If the search landscape does not bifurcate — if traditional keyword search remains dominant through 2029 — the keyword-plus-audience-plus-OCI approach continues to deliver. The agentic infrastructure investments still pay off through operational efficiency even if the external channel does not shift. The risk is over-investment in agent-accessible registration flows that few agents actually use. The mitigation is that those investments (structured data, API endpoints, machine-readable content) improve the human experience too.

If agentic capability stalls at Stage 3 (autonomous with guardrails) rather than reaching Stage 4 (orchestrated), the team still operates at significantly higher efficiency than 2026. The gap between Stage 3 and Stage 4 is coordination complexity, not fundamental capability. The team can operate effectively at Stage 3 indefinitely while building toward Stage 4 incrementally.

If the competitive advantage from early agentic investment is smaller than projected — because tools commoditize faster than expected or because competitors catch up quickly — the fallback is the five-workstream methodology itself. The methodology is the moat, not the tools. Competitors can buy the same agent platforms but cannot replicate the integrated system of OCI-plus-consolidation-plus-lifecycle-plus-UX-plus-algorithmic that compounds across workstreams.

## Broader Agentic Evolution — Parallel Track

*This section runs alongside the PS-specific projections. It covers the enterprise multi-agent landscape, agentic commerce protocols, and how Richard's Five Stages framework maps to the external trajectory.*

### The external landscape validates the direction

Enterprise adoption of agentic AI is accelerating faster than most technology cycles. Gartner reports that 52% of executives have agents in production as of early 2026, and 40% of enterprise applications will embed task-specific AI agents by end of 2026 — up from less than 5% in 2025. CrewAI's survey found 100% of surveyed enterprises planning to expand agentic AI in 2026, with 62% already in production. The agentic AI market is valued at $9.89 billion in 2026 and projected to reach $52-57 billion by 2030-2031. [source: Gartner, CrewAI State of Agentic AI 2026, MarketsAndMarkets; confidence: MEDIUM — adoption numbers are real, sustainability is uncertain]

The critical counterpoint: Gartner also predicts more than 40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled by 2027 due to escalating costs and unclear business value. Both projections can be true simultaneously — rapid adoption followed by rapid disillusionment is the standard technology hype cycle. The teams that survive the correction are those that built incrementally with measurable outcomes at each stage, not those that made large speculative bets on agentic transformation. Richard's approach — Stage 1 delivering value before investing in Stage 2 — is explicitly designed for this pattern. [source: Gartner via DigitalApplied; confidence: MEDIUM]

### Five Stages mapped to external trajectory

Richard's Five Stages of Agentic PS map to the broader enterprise agentic evolution, but on a faster timeline because the PS function is unusually well-suited to automation (data-heavy, rule-based, measurable).

Stage 1 (Agent-Assisted, current) corresponds to where most enterprise marketing teams are in early 2026 — using AI for content generation, data summarization, and workflow assistance. Richard's system is more sophisticated than most (persistent memory, multi-agent architecture, body system) but the functional output is similar: agents assist, humans decide and execute.

Stage 2 (Agent-Executed, 2026-2027 target) corresponds to where Gartner expects 40% of enterprise apps to be by end of 2026. Agents execute defined tasks with human approval. The difference is that Richard's system will have the guardrails, measurement framework, and rollback procedures that most enterprise deployments lack — which is why 40% of those deployments get canceled.

Stage 3 (Agent-Autonomous, 2027-2028 target) corresponds to Forrester's projection that 50% of enterprises will deploy multi-agent systems for real-time decision-making by 2027. Agents operate independently within defined parameters. This is where the sustainability question becomes critical — autonomous agents that make mistakes at scale are worse than manual processes. The guardrail architecture (CPA limits, anomaly detection, human escalation triggers) is the differentiator.

Stage 4 (Agent-Orchestrated, 2028-2029 target) corresponds to the multi-agent coordination that AWS Bedrock AgentCore and similar platforms are productionizing. Multiple specialized agents working together across business workflows. Richard's existing system (Kiro plus body system plus hooks) is a functional prototype of what AgentCore makes enterprise-grade. The gap is scale, reliability, and organizational trust.

Stage 5 (Self-Improving, 2029-2030 target) has no clear external parallel yet. Self-improving agent systems that run experiments on their own processes are discussed in research but not deployed in production marketing environments. This stage is aspirational. The autoresearch loop (agents optimizing their own context management) is the closest existing example, but extending it to campaign management requires a level of organizational trust and measurement sophistication that does not exist yet. [source: agentic-ps-vision.md, Gartner, Forrester, AWS; confidence: HIGH for Stages 1-2 mapping, MEDIUM for Stage 3, LOW for Stages 4-5]

### Agentic commerce protocols and AB's position

Two competing protocols define the emerging agentic commerce landscape. Google's Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP), announced January 11, 2026 by Sundar Pichai at NRF, is an open standard for AI agents to handle the full shopping journey from discovery through checkout. Launch partners include Shopify, Target, Walmart, Etsy, and Wayfair, with payment support from Visa, Mastercard, Stripe, and American Express. UCP enables purchases inside AI Mode and the Gemini app without visiting a product page. [source: Google Business; confidence: HIGH for product existence]

OpenAI's Agentic Commerce Protocol (ACP), launched September 29, 2025 with Stripe, powers Instant Checkout in ChatGPT. ACP is dialogue-based — conversational agents guide users through the buying process. The UCP-versus-ACP dynamic mirrors the "open web versus walled garden" tension that has defined digital commerce for two decades. [source: Atwix, MTSoln; confidence: HIGH for product existence]

Neither protocol currently supports B2B registration flows. AB's registration event — creating a free business account — is structurally different from a product purchase. Extending either protocol to support "create a business account" is architecturally possible but not on any published roadmap. The practical implication: AB should make its registration flow agent-readable (structured data, clear value proposition in machine-parseable format, API endpoints for account creation) without betting on a specific protocol. This is a 2027-2028 investment, not 2026. [source: protocol analysis; confidence: MEDIUM for direction, LOW for timing]

### The marketing-specific agentic forecast

Marketing is one of the fastest-adopting functions for agentic AI. Gartner projects 75% AI adoption in marketing by 2027. Forrester estimates 35% efficiency gains from generative AI in martech. Enterprise ROI from agentic AI averages 171%, with 74% of executives achieving returns within the first year. Survey data shows 72% of marketers are comfortable letting AI agents summarize data, and 80% would deploy agents for audience targeting and competitor analysis. [source: Gartner, Forrester, DigitalApplied, MagicBlocks; confidence: MEDIUM — survey data, not measured outcomes]

The B2B procurement AI forecast is more aggressive. Gartner projects that 90% of B2B buying will be AI-agent intermediated by 2028, pushing $15 trillion through AI agent exchanges. AI agents will outnumber human sellers 10x by 2028. McKinsey frames the future workforce as a triad of people, AI agents, and robots, with 57% of work hours technically automatable. [source: Gartner via DigitalCommerce360, McKinsey; confidence: LOW-MEDIUM — these are forecasts, not measurements]

We treat these projections as directionally correct but timeline-aggressive. The actual pace of AI-intermediated B2B procurement will depend on trust, regulation, and the practical challenge of making complex B2B purchasing decisions through agent interfaces. AB's position is to be ready when the shift accelerates, not to bet the strategy on a specific year.

## Appendix K: Contradictions and Unknowns

These are unresolved tensions in the source material that the main body acknowledges but does not resolve.

1. The 40% cancellation rate versus 40% adoption rate. Gartner says 40% of enterprise apps will embed agents by end 2026 AND more than 40% of agentic projects will be canceled by 2027. Both can be true — rapid adoption followed by rapid disillusionment. Implication: build for sustainability, not hype.

2. AI Overview CTR impact varies from 35% to 65%. The variance suggests measurement methodology differences across sources, not an actual range of outcomes. AB-specific data does not exist. Do not plan around a specific number.

3. B2B procurement AI is forecast, not measured. The $15 trillion and 90% figures are Gartner projections from November 2025. No one has measured actual AI-intermediated B2B purchasing at scale. The timeline could be 2028 or 2035.

4. UCP and ACP are consumer/retail protocols. Neither has a B2B registration use case. Extending them to "create a free business account" is architecturally possible but not on anyone's published roadmap.

5. PMax "45% of conversions" is Google-wide. AB's conversion event (business registration with verification) is structurally different from e-commerce purchase. Do not assume AB will see the same PMax contribution.

6. No AB-specific AI Max data exists. Google's 14% conversion lift is an aggregate across early adopters. B2B registration performance is unknown until Richard's Q2 test runs.
