## Writing style observations (for future MBR style guide update)

1. **Mechanism precision without over-specificity**: Richard prefers "using various levers to direct algorithms to more efficiently bid" over naming specific mechanisms that invite scrutiny. Broad enough to be accurate, specific enough to show understanding.

2. **Preferred phrasing reuse**: Once Richard validates a phrase ("improved query routing to higher-intent traffic"), he reuses it consistently across drafts. Don't vary for variety's sake.

3. **Decisive closers over hedging**: "We will no longer consider this a test" vs "continuing to monitor and assess." When the call is made, state it.

4. **Counterfactual framing**: Richard uses "without X, Y would have happened" to show impact. Scopes it explicitly ("MX paid search overall") when mixing segment-specific data with total-market outcomes.

5. **Constraint→response→outcome narrative**: The strongest framing starts with the external constraint (ie%CCP targets), then the response (bid strategy changes), then the outcome (sustained efficiency + growth). This is more compelling than results-first for flash audiences.

6. **H2 vs YoY comparison choice**: When YoY comps are inflated by ramp-up periods, Richard prefers comparing against the most recent mature baseline (H2). More defensible, less flashy.

7. **Sensitivity awareness in scoping**: Deliberately omitted AU from Brand LP Tests status because of Polaris urgency sensitivity with Lena's team. Covered AU Polaris in AU Updates instead.

8. **"helped us decrease" for partial attribution**: When a result has multiple contributing factors, uses "helped" to attribute contribution without overclaiming.

9. **Comma minimization**: Prefers max two commas per sentence in flash writing. Splits sentences rather than chaining clauses.

10. **Word count discipline**: Highlights ~100 words, Updates ~50 words, Project Statuses ~35 words.
