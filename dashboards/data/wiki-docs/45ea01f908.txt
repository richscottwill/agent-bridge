# DE W13 Analysis Brief

## Registration summary
654 reported regs (-46% WoW), $110K spend (-21% WoW), $168 CPA (+45% WoW). Brand 362 (-47% WoW), NB 292 (-44% WoW). DATA LAG: True performance likely 1,100-1,300 regs after correction. Projected: 5,900 regs, $624K spend vs OP2 6,129 regs, $658K spend (-4% regs, -5% spend).

## Why registrations changed (data lag caveat)
The -46% WoW headline is almost certainly a data lag artifact, worse than W12's lag episode. The daily pattern is the proof: Sun 16, Mon 31, Tue 20, Wed 44, Thu 211, Fri 201, Sat 131. The first four days averaged 28 regs vs Thu-Sat averaging 181. W12 had the same pattern (Fri/Sat at 32 avg vs 236 weekday avg), and the -18% headline was later assessed as -2% to -5% true decline. W13's lag is more severe, affecting Sun-Wed rather than just Fri-Sat.

Using Thu-Sat as the clean data (543 regs in 3 days, ~181/day), a full-week estimate would be ~1,200-1,300 regs. This implies a true WoW change of flat to +8% vs W12's lag-corrected ~1,300. The reported CVR collapse to 1.50% (-38% WoW) is also inflated by the lag: Thu-Sat CVR would be significantly higher than the blended figure.

## Trend context
DE's reported 8-week trend (1,200-1,510 for W5-W11, then 1,242 in W12 and 654 in W13) is misleading. W12 was lag-affected and later assessed at ~1,300 true. W13 is lag-affected and likely ~1,200-1,300 true. The underlying trend is probably stable in the 1,200-1,400 range, consistent with the W9-W11 pattern. CPA at $168 reported is meaningless under lag conditions; the true CPA is likely $85-$100 based on corrected reg volume against $110K spend.

## Relevant actions and events
1. Significant budget increases on 3/23: PV $600 to $1.5K, GA Top $1.5K to $2.6K, AMZ $2K to $3K, GA $3.3K to $4.5K, Generic $4.5K to $6K. Brand Core max CPC raised from $15 to $21 (65% of impressions were limited). These are aggressive expansions that should drive volume in W14 once data normalizes.
2. recht24-7.de at 14% Brand IS. No update on store closure status. The Brand Core max CPC increase ($15 to $21) is a direct response to impression limitations, potentially related to this competitor's presence.
3. hvocijid duplicate parameter issue remains under investigation. If this is suppressing conversion tracking, it compounds the data lag problem and makes the true performance even harder to read.
4. Data lag is now a recurring pattern: W12 Fri/Sat affected, W13 Sun-Wed affected. This is a systemic reporting issue, not a one-off.

## YoY assessment
Reported: -50% YoY regs (654 vs 1,306), +5% YoY spend. These numbers are meaningless under data lag. Lag-corrected estimate: ~1,200-1,300 regs vs 1,306 LY, which would be roughly flat to -8% YoY. LY W13 was -4% WoW. The YoY picture remains challenging: DE is spending more for similar or slightly fewer regs, reflecting the strong 2025 baseline.

## Monthly projection
Projected 5,900 regs vs OP2 6,129 (-4%). Projected $624K spend vs OP2 $658K (-5%). The projection requires significant lag correction. Reported MTD is 4,757 regs. W13's lag-corrected estimate adds ~550-650 regs to the reported 654, bringing adjusted MTD to ~5,300-5,400. Remaining 3 days: 2 weekdays, 1 weekend day. Using corrected daily rates (weekday ~200, weekend ~140): 2(200) + 1(140) = 540. Adjusted MTD ~5,350 + 540 = ~5,890, rounded to 5,900. Spend: MTD $576K + ~$48K remaining = $624K. CPA: ~$106. DE is tracking -4% vs OP2 on regs, the tightest EU5 market against plan. The 3/23 budget increases should help close this gap in the final days.

## Flags
- Data lag (critical): Two consecutive weeks of severe data lag. W12 affected Fri/Sat; W13 affected Sun-Wed. This is a systemic issue that needs pipeline investigation, not just patience.
- Budget expansion: The 3/23 changes are the most aggressive budget increases in the 12-week window. If data normalizes, W14 should show a volume step-up. If data lag persists, the spend increase will inflate CPA without visible reg gains.
- CPA streak: Reported CPA has risen 3 consecutive weeks ($112 to $168). After lag correction, the true CPA trend is likely flat to slightly rising ($100-$110 range). Still worth monitoring.
- OP2 gap: -4% regs vs plan is the weakest position in EU5. The budget increases are the lever to close this.

True performance is likely 1,100-1,300 regs, roughly flat WoW. Aggressive budget increases on 3/23 (Brand Core max CPC $15 to $21, NB budgets up 50-150%) should drive volume in W14. Monthly projection: -4% regs vs OP2, the tightest EU5 market against plan. The recurring data lag is now a systemic issue requiring pipeline investigation.
