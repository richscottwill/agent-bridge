# WW — W12 (Mar 15–21)

WW drove 15.8K registrations (-4% WoW), with -9% spend WoW. CPA improved to $72 (-5% WoW). March is projected to end at $5.2M spend and 67.8K registrations. (vs. OP2: flat spend, +11% registrations)

WoW the decline was concentrated in Brand (-7%) while NB held relatively steady (-2%). Eight of ten markets declined, with only UK (+1%) and US (flat) avoiding a dip. The steepest drops came from DE (-18%, though overstated by data lag), MX (-16%, CVR normalization after W11's spike plus Benito Juarez holiday), and JP (-16%, returning to post-MHLW baseline with fiscal year-end suppression). EU5 saw a broad pullback consistent with mid-March seasonality, as last year's W12 showed similar directional declines across the region. Brand declined in all five EU5 markets, the clearest cross-market signal. Spend fell faster than registrations across most markets, meaning CPA improved WoW in 8 of 10 markets. The efficiency trajectory is the positive signal underneath the volume softness.

YoY we spent +17% with +46% registrations (excluding AU, which has no prior year data). NB drove the bulk of the growth at +73% YoY, powered by OCI in US and UK and bid strategy efficiency gains in MX and CA. Brand grew +21% YoY. NB CPA compression continues to be the structural story: US NB CPA down 47% YoY, MX NB CPA down 50% YoY, CA NB CPA down 46% YoY.

Note: DE's -18% WoW is unreliable due to Fri/Sat data lag (32 avg regs vs 236 weekday avg). Adjusted, the true WW decline is likely closer to -2% to -3%. March is tracking +11% regs vs OP2 at matched spend, with US (+14%), MX (+68%), ES (+19%), and UK (+17%) leading the overperformance. JP is the only market tracking below OP2 on regs (-14%), a structural MHLW gap compounded by fiscal year-end.
