# Agentic Paid Search — Vision & Roadmap

## The Thesis

Paid search management is one of the most automatable functions in marketing. The work is data-heavy, rule-based, repetitive, and measurable — exactly the profile that AI agents handle well. Within 2-3 years, the teams that have built agentic infrastructure will operate at 3-5x the efficiency of teams that haven't. AB PS is building that infrastructure now.

This isn't about replacing people. It's about changing what people spend time on. Today, Richard spends ~60% of his time on execution (bids, keywords, campaigns, invoices) and ~40% on strategy (test design, stakeholder communication, cross-market playbooks). The goal is to invert that ratio.

The pattern is clear: context and triage are agent-assisted. Execution and creation are still manual. The next frontier is Stage 2 — agent-executed, human-approved — starting with campaign builds and invoice routing.

## The Five Stages of Agentic PS

### Stage 1: Agent-Assisted (NOW)
Agent handles context, triage, drafting. Human makes all decisions and executes.
- Morning routine, email drafts, meeting prep, WBR callouts
- Human effort: ~80% of pre-agent level

### Stage 2: Agent-Executed, Human-Approved
Agent executes routine tasks, human approves before they go live.
- Campaign builds from templates, keyword expansion from seed lists, invoice routing
- Human effort: ~50% of pre-agent level

### Stage 3: Agent-Autonomous with Guardrails
Agent operates independently within defined parameters. Human reviews exceptions.
- Bid adjustments within CPA guardrails, negative keyword management, anomaly detection
- Human effort: ~30% of pre-agent level

### Stage 4: Agent-Orchestrated
Multiple agents coordinate across functions. Human sets strategy and reviews outcomes.
- Cross-market campaign launches, competitive response playbooks, automated reporting
- Human effort: ~15% of pre-agent level

### Stage 5: Self-Improving System
Agents run experiments on their own processes, optimize without human intervention.
- The autoresearch loop applied to campaign management, not just context management
- Human effort: strategic direction only

## Why This Matters for AB

- 10 markets, 1 person hands-on (Richard) — agentic infrastructure is the only way to scale
- OCI already proved that algorithmic > manual for bidding — same principle applies to everything else
- Brandon's Annual Review feedback: "visibility" — agents free up time for the strategic work that creates visibility
- Kate's org is watching — if PS demonstrates agentic efficiency, it becomes a model for other marketing functions

## Risks

- Over-automation: agents make mistakes that humans would catch (guardrails are critical)
- Context drift: if the body system isn't maintained, agents operate on stale data
- Platform dependency: if AgentSpaces/DevSpaces changes, the system needs to be portable (portable-body/ exists for this)
- Perception: "Richard uses AI" could be seen as a shortcut rather than an innovation (frame as methodology, not tool)

The biggest risk isn't technical — it's perception. "Richard uses AI" needs to be framed as "Richard built a methodology that scales PS operations." The artifacts are the proof.
