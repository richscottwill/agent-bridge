# Forecast Model Multi-Signal Blend

The paid search forecast model was rewritten on April 14 to blend three signals — Bayesian posterior, year-over-year trajectory, and recent trend — rather than rely on seasonal-crushed Bayesian alone. This document captures the architecture, the reconciliation checks that caught the original bug, and the operational implications.

## Why the rewrite happened

The original model used a pure Bayesian projector with seasonal priors clamped between 0.8 and 1.2. For markets where the current year was diverging from the prior-year seasonal shape, the clamp crushed predictions toward historical averages even when recent actuals were trending differently. US week 14 projected 7,294 registrations against 9,236 actual, a 36 percent miss. AU week 15 was 47 percent off. The model was stuck, not responsive.

Separately, a COALESCE bug in the monthly and year-end projection derivation caused those numbers to sum only predicted weeks, ignoring actuals for completed weeks. US April projection understated by six thousand registrations. The fix cascaded through monthly, quarterly, and year-end aggregates.

## The three signals

The new model blends three signals at each projection horizon. The Bayesian posterior provides the base level, informed by historical patterns and priors. The year-over-year estimate takes last year's same-week value and applies the current year-to-date growth rate, producing a level-plus-trajectory view. The recent-trend estimate uses a six-week linear extrapolation to capture short-term momentum.

The blend weights are forty percent Bayesian, thirty percent YoY, thirty percent recent trend. The weights are empirical, tuned to minimize error against the last sixteen weeks of actuals across five markets. The blend is resilient: if one signal goes wrong (a seasonal distortion in the prior, a regime change in YoY, a noise spike in recent trend), the other two dampen the error.

## Seasonal prior unclamp

Ninety-eight seasonal priors were unclamped from the 0.8-to-1.2 range to their true historical values. Priors can now range from 0.6 to 1.4, reflecting actual seasonal variation. This removed the implicit smoothing that was dragging predictions toward historical averages.

## Prior weight cap reduction

The prior weight cap in the posterior update was lowered from 4 to 2. Previously, 170 weeks of low historical data could outweigh 4 weeks of fresh evidence. The new cap ensures recent data gets proportional weight, making the model responsive to regime changes.

## Regime change handling

Regime changes (Mexico Semana Santa, for example) now require an end date in the `ps.regime_changes` table. The MX Semana Santa regime was set to end 4/7, preventing the model from permanently suppressing predictions after the holiday. This is an operational discipline requirement — every regime change needs an end date or it will bias forecasts indefinitely.

## Reconciliation checks

Five invariant checks run at the end of `populate_forecast_tracker.py` on every execution. The monthly projection must be greater than or equal to completed-week actuals for the month. The year-end projection must be greater than or equal to year-to-date actuals. Weekly predictions more than fifty percent off actuals surface as informational warnings. Monthly sums must equal quarterly projections within a 0.1 percent tolerance. Aggregate market totals must match the sum of components.

The checks are warnings, not hard stops. They run every pipeline execution and catch the class of bug that produced the original COALESCE miss. Warnings go to the pipeline log; structural failures halt the downstream Excel update.

## Operational implications

The forecast is now responsive rather than stuck. The tradeoff is that weekly predictions can swing more week-to-week as new signals arrive. Treat single-week projections with the confidence interval visible in the dashboard, not as point estimates. The multi-week rolling average remains the stable reference for leadership reporting.

## Next Steps

1. Monitor prediction error each week; flag if any market exceeds twenty percent error for three consecutive weeks.
2. Review regime change end dates quarterly to ensure no stale suppressions remain.
3. Document the blend weights in the forecast tracker dashboard README.
