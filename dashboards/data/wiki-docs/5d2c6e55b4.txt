# PS Operations

The strategic work described in this document — OCI rollout, ad copy testing, audience expansion, landing page optimization, algorithmic ads — runs on top of a consistent operational foundation. This section documents the recurring work that makes the strategic work possible.

## The Operational Rhythm in Numbers

| Cadence | Activities | Approximate Team Time |
|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| Daily | Campaign monitoring, anomaly detection, bid checks | ~3-4 hrs/day (distributed) |
| Weekly | WBR prep, market reviews, team sync, stakeholder syncs | ~8-10 hrs/week (distributed) |
| Biweekly | Google sync, Adobe sync, MX sync | ~3 hrs/cycle |
| Monthly | Budgets, invoices, goals, reporting, competitive intel | ~10-15 hrs/month (distributed) |

**So what:** The seven-person team spends roughly 25-30 hours per week on operational work — monitoring, reporting, budget management, stakeholder syncs. This is the capacity cost of running 10 markets. The remaining capacity is what funds the five strategic workstreams. Operational efficiency directly determines strategic capacity: every hour saved on reporting or invoicing is an hour available for testing, analysis, and cross-functional partnership.

## Daily Operations

Daily monitoring covers spend pacing, CPA tracking, bid strategy health, and ad policy compliance across all active markets. This takes approximately 30-45 minutes per person per day and is the first line of defense against wasted spend.

## Weekly Operations

### WBR Callout Preparation
The Pre-WBR Customer Engagement meeting is the team's primary reporting mechanism to leadership. Each week:
1. Pull WW Dashboard data (processed via dashboard ingester)
2. Identify WoW changes >10% by market
3. Draft callouts with root cause analysis (seasonal, competitive, operational)
4. Update the Pre-WBR Callouts Quip doc
5. Present in the Pre-WBR meeting (Kate Rundell, Dwayne Palmer, Kristine Weber attend)

### Market-Specific Reviews
Each market owner conducts a weekly review focused on OCI performance, competitive shifts, and budget pacing. Market-specific detail is in the [Market Reference](market-reference).

### Team Sync (Weekly, Wednesday)
The Paid Acq: Deep Dive & Debate meeting is the team's internal performance review:
- Each team member walks through their market performance
- Budget pacing review across all markets
- Testing updates and go/no-go decisions
- Strategic discussion on upcoming initiatives
- Brandon provides direction and prioritization

## Biweekly Operations

| Meeting | Purpose | Key Outputs |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Google + AB Performance Sync | OCI status, account health, AI Max planning, ad copy results | Action items for account optimization, Google recommendations |
| Adobe Bi-Weekly Call | OCI reporting feed, Ad Cloud analytics, WW redirect reporting | Reporting alignment, data quality checks |
| MX Paid Search Sync | MX campaign performance, keyword strategy, LP coordination | Campaign adjustments, keyword additions/removals |

## Monthly Operations

### Budget Management
- R&O (Revenue & Operations) input for MX and AU
- Budget confirmations with finance (York Chen, BK Cho)
- Spend forecasting based on current pacing and seasonal patterns
- PO submissions for PAM US, MX, AU media spend

### Invoice/PO Coordination
- Google Ads invoicing for AU and MX
- PO matching and OFA approvals for Google Ads media spend
- Finance coordination for payment processing (York Chen, BK Cho)

### Kingpin Goal Updates
- Pull registration and CPA data from Andes
- Update goal tracker with Jan/Feb/Mar actuals
- Flag goals at risk with root cause and path-to-green

### Performance Reporting
- MBR narratives: Monthly performance summary with YoY and vs. OP2 comparisons
- Competitive landscape update: New competitors, IS changes, CPC trends
- Flash contributions: Paid Acquisition Flash (bimonthly, authored by Andrew Wirtz, distributed to ab-marketing)

### Competitive Monitoring
- Review auction insights data across all markets
- Track IS trajectory for key competitors (Walmart US, weareuncapped UK, bruneau.fr FR)
- Document CPC impact and strategic response
- Update competitor-intel.md with new entrants and trend changes

## Quarterly Operations

- **QBR narratives:** Quarterly performance deep dive with strategic context — each QBR covers all 10 markets with YoY trends and vs. OP2 comparisons.
- **Test portfolio review:** Assess all active tests and make scale/stop/iterate decisions. Q4 2025 review resulted in stopping JP Bulk LP (5.8% probability of outperforming control) and scaling the CA LP framework to EU5.
- **Budget reforecasting:** Update annual spend projections based on actuals, OCI impact, and market conditions.
- **Goal resets:** Adjust Kingpin targets based on actuals — e.g., JP targets adjusted after MHLW campaign ended 1/31.

The quarterly review is where scale-or-stop decisions are made. Q4 2025 review stopped JP Bulk LP (5.8% probability) and greenlit the CA LP framework for EU5 — both decisions that shaped the 2026 portfolio.

## Why This Matters

The operational backbone is what enables the team to run five concurrent strategic workstreams across 10 markets. Without daily monitoring, a bid strategy issue in AU could waste thousands before anyone notices. Without weekly WBR prep, leadership loses visibility into performance trends. Without monthly budget management, campaigns pause mid-flight. All of it is delivered by the same seven-person team that runs the strategic work.
