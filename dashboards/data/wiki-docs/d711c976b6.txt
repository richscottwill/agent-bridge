# How We Test

The PS team follows a consistent methodology across all workstreams: identify a problem, design a controlled test, validate the result, then scale what works. This discipline applies whether we are rolling out a new bidding strategy to 10 markets or testing a single headline change in one campaign. The methodology is the team's operating system — not a project.

## The Four Stages

### 1. Hypothesis and Baseline

Every test starts with a measurable claim. "OCI bidding will reduce Non-Brand CPA by 30% or more in the US" is a hypothesis. "We should try OCI" is not.

We establish baselines using prior-quarter data with seasonality adjustments so we can isolate the effect of the change from background noise. For OCI, this means comparing post-OCI CPA against what CPA would have been without OCI, accounting for seasonal registration patterns. For ad copy, we define test and control groups within the same campaign. For landing pages, we capture pre-change performance across a comparable time window.

For channel-level incrementality, we use Synthetic Regional Testing (SyRT) — the scientific gold standard for deriving ground truth. In Q2 2023, we ran a multi-arm SyRT across the US and UK to measure whether Paid Search drives incremental AB registrations, and whether Brand and Non-Brand keywords have different incrementality effects.

| SyRT Arm | Market | Test Period | Registration Lift | Statistical Significance | Incrementality |
|----------|--------|-------------|-------------------|--------------------------|----------------|
| Non-Brand | US | Q2 2023 | +16.4% | p<0.001 | 82-92% of NB registrations are incremental |
| Brand | US | Q2 2023 | Directionally positive | Not statistically significant | Lower incrementality — high-intent users likely to register regardless |
| Non-Brand | UK | Q2 2023 | Positive | Significant | Confirmed NB as primary incremental driver |

**So what:** Non-Brand drives 82-92% incremental registrations — the channel justifies itself. Brand is directionally positive but not statistically significant, meaning high-intent users would likely register regardless. This data directly informed how we allocate budget between Brand and Non-Brand. Paid Search is not just capturing demand that would have converted anyway.

### 2. Phased Rollout

We do not make big-bang changes. OCI launched at the keyword level (E2E testing), scaled to 25% of campaigns, then 50%, then 100%. Each phase has a measurement window and a go/no-go decision. This approach was validated in the US and has become the standard playbook for every subsequent market.

The phased approach applies across all workstreams:

| Workstream | Phase 1 | Phase 2 | Phase 3 |
|------------|---------|---------|---------|
| OCI Bidding | E2E keyword testing | 25% → 50% campaigns | 100% campaigns |
| Ad Copy | NB 50% campaigns (High CPA) | All NB campaigns | Brand Plus |
| Landing Pages | Individual page optimization | Market-level rollout | WW scale |
| Campaign Consolidation | One market (US) | EU5 | RoW |
| Audiences | LiveRamp suppression (US) | Engagement account (US) | F90 lifecycle |

For the current Polaris Brand LP rollout, we proposed a pre-post analysis framework for Japan with a defined success threshold: 45% CP improvement as the minimum acceptable result, with a documented rollback trigger if the lift falls below 35%. This gives the team a clear, data-driven decision point.

### 3. Measurement Framework

We use different measurement approaches depending on the test type, because no single method fits all situations:

| Test Type | Method | Example | Why This Method |
|-----------|--------|---------|-----------------|
| Bidding changes (OCI) | Actualized CPA vs. seasonality-adjusted baseline | US: +24% reg lift, ~50% NB CPA improvement | Isolates OCI effect from seasonal patterns |
| Ad copy | Test/control split within campaign | UK: +86% CTR, +31% regs (30-day test) | Requires sufficient volume and duration |
| Landing pages (quick-turn) | Pre/post causal analysis | CA Bulk CVR: 0.82% → 2.35% (+187%) | Fast iteration on specific page changes |
| Landing pages (structural) | Weblab (APT) | In-context reg: +13.6K annualized regs, 100% probability | Gold standard for large UX changes |
| Channel incrementality | SyRT (Randomized Control Trial) | US NB: +16.4% lift, p<0.001 | Scientific ground truth for channel value |
| Bid modifiers | Google Ads experiments + Bayesian PPR | IT location: 83.4% PPR, +18% regs | Probabilistic confidence for smaller tests |

We explicitly state confidence levels. The UK ad copy test ran 30 days with meaningful volume — confidence HIGH. The IT ad copy test had -97% clicks vs control — confidence LOW, insufficient volume. We do not overstate results.

### 4. Scale or Stop

If a test validates, we scale it. If it does not, we document the learning and move on. The learning always compounds.

**Tests we scaled:**
- OCI: US → UK → DE → CA, JP, FR, IT, ES (5 more markets in 2026)
- Ad copy: UK → EU4 translations completed via GlobalLink
- In-context registration: US → WW
- Campaign consolidation: US → EU5
- LiveRamp suppression: US → CA
- CA LP framework: CA → EU5 (2026)

**Tests we stopped:**
- Gated Guest: -61% registrations after 4 weeks. Paused, deep-dived, pivoted to in-context registration.
- JP App marketing: Incremental downloads confirmed but incremental registrations inconclusive.
- JP Bulk LP: 5.8% chance of outperforming control via Bayesian analysis. Clear stop signal.

**Tests we iterated:**
- JP Non-Brand: Audience layering showed Competitor subdomain audience at $700 CPA vs. $1,457 overall — informed next test design.
- CA carousel: -9% registration impact. Hypothesis: missing pricing. Waiting for ESI public pricing integration to retest.
- IT optimizations: Deep dive in Q3 identified elevated Brand CPA and YoY CCP decline. Targeted actions narrowed variance from -31% (Q3) to -17% (Dec 2025).

In every case, the learning compounds. The Gated Guest failure directly informed the in-context registration approach that delivered +13.6K annualized registrations. The JP NB audience test informed the Engagement account targeting strategy. The IT bid modifier test (83.4% PPR) informed the approach to OCI city-level optimization.

## Why This Matters

This methodology is not unique to Paid Search — it is the same discipline used across Amazon's experimentation culture. What is distinctive is how the PS team applies it across five concurrent workstreams, 10 markets, and multiple cross-functional partnerships simultaneously. The testing framework is the team's operating system. Every initiative in this document follows the same four stages, and every 2026 investment maps to a validated 2025 result produced by this framework.

The compounding effect is the key insight: campaign consolidation strengthens OCI signals, OCI enables smarter bidding on the audiences the Engagement channel reaches, and Baloo creates the friction-free landing experience that AI Max's dynamic page selection requires. No workstream operates in isolation because the methodology connects them.
