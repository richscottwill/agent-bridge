1. Add status indicators to Active Initiatives — each initiative needs a clear `[COMPLETE]`, `[IN PROGRESS]`, `[PROPOSED]`, or `[BLOCKED]` tag. Currently the reader has to infer status from prose.

4. Collapse "Recurring Meetings" into the stakeholder table or a frontmatter field. Three lines don't earn a section.

**Suggestions (non-blocking):**
- The W13 weekly data should be flagged as a single-week snapshot, not a trend. Add "(single week — volatile)" or similar.
- The CPC Challenge section is excellent narrative but could use a structured "talking points" block for agent extraction.
- Consider a "Key Metrics" structured block at the top (budget, OP2 target, CPA target, OCI status) for agent quick-read.

## Cross-Cutting Observations

**Voice compliance:** Articles 1, 2, and 5 match Richard's voice well — direct, data-forward, parenthetical asides, "so what" after every data point. Article 3 is close but slightly more neutral/encyclopedic than Richard's natural register. Article 4 reads more like synthesized Slack notes than a Richard-authored strategy doc — the voice is informational rather than analytical.

**Structural pattern:** The strongest docs (1, 2, 5) follow richard-style-docs structures almost exactly — Question → Setup → Results → Recommendation for strategy docs, Objective → Numbered Tactics → Scenarios for how-to docs. The weaker docs (3, 4) don't map cleanly to any of the defined doc-type structures.

**Recurring gap:** No article in the set includes `depends_on` or `consumed_by` in YAML frontmatter. This is a systematic gap that limits agent graph traversal. Article 1 has it; the rest don't.

**Economy pattern:** The MCC Structure table appears in both Article 1 and Article 2 identically. This is the kind of duplication the wiki-critic rubric flags — it should live in one canonical location and be referenced elsewhere.

**Accuracy pattern:** Articles 1-4 have Sources sections; Article 5 does not. The Slack-sourced article (4) has the most granular sourcing (specific messages, dates, channels). The strategy docs (1, 5) have the most risk of number staleness.

**The 8/10 bar:** Three articles clear it. Two don't. The two that don't (AU Market Wiki, Enhanced Match) share a common weakness: they're information captures rather than decision-enabling documents. The rubric's usefulness dimension rewards docs that help someone DO or DECIDE — both REVISE articles are stronger on UNDERSTAND than on DO.
