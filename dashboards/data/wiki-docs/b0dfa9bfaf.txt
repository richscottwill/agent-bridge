## Required changes

These three changes are required to clear the gate. The TODO markers are the binding constraint, but the other two are necessary to reach the 8/10 bar.

### 1. Resolve both TODO markers

**TODO #1 (line 51):** The $140 target is already cited in the Sources section. Either add an inline citation or remove the TODO marker. Suggested replacement:

> Replace:
> ```
> **AU CPA target:** $140. Historical AU CPA has run $120 to $180 depending on the week and keyword mix. Brand CPA typically runs around $80. Non-brand CPA typically runs $200 or higher.
> <!-- TODO: cite AU CPA target $140 source — eyes.md Market Health → AU or current.md -->
> ```
> With:
> ```
> **AU CPA target:** $140 (source: eyes.md, Market Health → AU). Historical AU CPA has run $120 to $180 depending on the week and keyword mix. Brand CPA typically runs around $80. Non-brand CPA typically runs $200 or higher.
> ```

**TODO #2 (line 106):** Either calibrate the 10% threshold from historical data and cite the source, or explicitly frame it as a provisional threshold. Suggested replacement:

> Replace:
> ```
> When total dashboard registrations diverge from total AU DB registrations by more than 10%, investigate the attribution pipeline before interpreting keyword-level CPA.
> <!-- TODO: cite attribution gap threshold — is 10% the right number or should this be calibrated from historical data? -->
> ```
> With:
> ```
> When total dashboard registrations diverge from total AU DB registrations by more than 10%, investigate the attribution pipeline before interpreting keyword-level CPA. The 10% threshold is a provisional starting point based on operator judgment; calibrate against historical divergence data once three or more weeks of paired totals are available.
> ```

### 2. Cut or compress the dead-weight sections

**"Next steps (historical context)":** Delete this section entirely. It contains four noun-only bullet items for steps that were due in March 2026. Retaining them "for provenance" is not a reference-doc function — provenance lives in git history or the draft-log, not in the published article.

**"Automation path":** Merge the unique content (standardize export template, configure column mapping, validate against manual pull) into the third paragraph of "How the data is sourced and refreshed" where the ingester is first mentioned. Delete the standalone section.

**"Delivery":** Merge the delivery cadence sentence ("delivered weekly before the AU sync meeting") into the "Transition context" section, which already discusses the handoff. Delete the standalone section.

Net effect: three sections removed, ~150 words cut, no information lost.
