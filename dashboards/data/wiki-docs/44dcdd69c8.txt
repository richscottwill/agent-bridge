### Important Findings the Synthesis Misses

These are in the section docs but absent from the synthesis. Some are acceptable compressions; others are gaps Kate should see.

**Acceptable omissions (detail belongs in section docs):**
- DE test-vs-control weekly data (W44/W45) -- too granular for synthesis
- IT ad copy results (LOW confidence) -- correctly excluded

**Gaps that matter for Kate:**

1. **No mention of the Gated Guest failure in the UX workstream section.** The methodology section mentions it in passing ("The Gated Guest experiment showed -61% registrations after 4 weeks"), but the UX workstream section does not tell the pivot story. kate-doc-ux.md tells the full arc: Gated Guest failed, deep dive, pivot to in-context registration, +13.6K regs. 2. **F90 Legal blocker is understated.** kate-doc-audiences.md says "LiveRamp 1P positive targeting approval is on track for US by end of April 2026, but expansion to additional regions is blocked pending resolution of Tumble service requirements." The synthesis says "Legal is reviewing audience-transmission solutions." Kate should know what is actually blocking RoW expansion.

3. **The operational capacity constraint is implicit, not explicit.** kate-doc-operations.md quantifies it: "~25-30 hours per week on operational work" for a 7-person team. The synthesis says "Both are delivered by the same seven-person team" but does not give Kate the number. She needs the capacity math to justify headcount or prioritization decisions.

4. **No mention of the hvocijid duplicate parameter issue.** kate-doc-oci.md flags this as a known issue affecting EU3 + existing markets. If Kate asks "what is the risk to the RoW rollout?" this is the answer. The synthesis should at least acknowledge it exists.

**Yes -- and this is its strongest quality.** The synthesis does three things the section docs cannot:

1. **The compounding argument.** The closing paragraph of the Investment Summary -- "campaign consolidation strengthens OCI signals, OCI enables smarter bidding on the audiences the Engagement channel reaches, and Baloo creates the friction-free landing experience that AI Max's dynamic page selection requires" -- is the single most important sentence in the entire doc set. 2. **The investment summary table.** This table is the payoff of the entire document. It maps 2025 validated signal to 2026 investment to expected impact for all five workstreams in one view. Kate can look at this table and understand the entire ask.

3. **The "How We Test" framing.** By leading with methodology before workstreams, the synthesis establishes that the team has a system -- not just a collection of projects. This is the strategic positioning that justifies PS as partners, not executors.

## Part 4: What's Missing for Kate Specifically

### 1. Executive Summary (CRITICAL)

Kate has 15 minutes. The document opens with a positioning paragraph that is good but not an executive summary. She needs the headline in the first 3 sentences.

**What's there:** "This document establishes the Paid Search team's testing methodology and 2026 investment roadmap."

**What Kate needs:** The results first, then the ask. Something like: "In 2025, the PS team's testing methodology delivered +35,196 incremental registrations and $16.7MM+ in OPS across three markets, while building five compounding workstreams now ready to scale worldwide. The 2026 roadmap: expand OCI bidding to five additional markets (full impact by July), launch Project Baloo (Shopping Ads unlock), and extend PS into lifecycle engagement via F90. Every 2026 investment maps to a validated 2025 result."

The current opening tells Kate what the document IS. It should tell her what the document SAYS.

### 2. OP2 Connection (IMPORTANT)

Kate needs to justify PS investment to Todd. The doc never mentions OP2 by name except in passing market performance references. It should explicitly connect:
- How the 2026 investments map to OP2 targets
- What the PS team's OP2 registration and OPS commitments are
- How the validated 2025 results de-risk the 2026 OP2 plan

### 3. Risk of NOT Investing (IMPORTANT)

The doc makes the positive case well. It does not make the negative case. Kate needs to answer Todd's implicit question: "What happens if we don't fund this?"

Missing risk framing:
- If OCI does not scale to RoW, those 5 markets continue on Adobe's deprecated bidding -- no CPA improvement, no reg uplift
- If Baloo does not launch, Shopping Ads remain unavailable for AB -- a channel that competitors can access
- If F90 does not launch, PS remains acquisition-only while the Engagement infrastructure sits underutilized
- If the team does not get capacity relief, 5 strategic workstreams compete with 25-30 hrs/week of operational overhead across 7 people

### 4. Capacity Constraint (IMPORTANT)

The synthesis says "Both are delivered by the same seven-person team" but does not quantify the constraint. kate-doc-operations.md has the number: ~25-30 hours/week on operational work. Kate should see this math:

- 7 people x 40 hrs = 280 hrs/week total capacity
- ~25-30 hrs/week on operations = ~10% of total capacity
- Remaining ~250 hrs/week funds 5 strategic workstreams across 10 markets
- That is ~50 hrs/week per workstream -- and each workstream spans multiple markets and cross-functional partnerships

This is the capacity argument for headcount or prioritization. Without it, Kate sees the output but not the constraint.

### 5. Acknowledgment of What's Not Working

The doc is relentlessly positive about results. Kate will respect honesty about challenges more than a highlight reel. The section docs contain honest assessments:
- DE missed OP2 by 4% (kate-doc-oci.md)
- JP is -47.5% vs OP2 with MHLW headwind (eyes.md / kate-doc-appendix.md)
- IT ad copy test had insufficient volume (kate-doc-modern-search.md)
- F90 is blocked by Legal (kate-doc-audiences.md)

The synthesis should have a brief "Challenges and Risks" section that acknowledges these. Amazon leadership docs that only show wins lose credibility.
