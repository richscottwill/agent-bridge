# FR W13 Analysis Brief

## Registration summary
1,191 regs (+4% WoW), $69K spend (+14% WoW), $58 CPA (+10% WoW). Brand 492 (+2% WoW), NB 699 (+5% WoW). Projected: 5,100 regs, $311K spend vs OP2 4,694 regs, $254K spend (+9% regs, +22% spend).

## Why registrations changed
Clicks drove the growth (+7% WoW) more than CVR (-3%). The segments diverged in mechanism: Brand grew +2% on CVR improvement (+4%, 2.23% vs 2.14%) despite -2% clicks. NB grew +5% on a +16% click surge (26,447 vs 22,833) but CVR fell -9% (2.64% vs 2.91%). The NB click surge is notable: this is the highest NB click volume in the 12-week window. However, the CVR decline means the click expansion is buying lower-quality traffic. NB CPA rose +17% WoW ($74 vs $64), reversing W12's efficiency gains. Brand CPA improved -9% ($35 vs $38).

## Trend context
FR has been in a 1,017-1,200 range for 9 weeks. W13 at 1,191 is near the top of the range, just below W11's 1,200. The upward drift continues: W5-W8 averaged 1,069, W9-W13 averaged 1,135 (+6%). NB regs have grown from 592 (W5) to 699 (W13), a steady climb. Brand remains volatile (418-545 range) with W13's 492 mid-range. CPA has oscillated: $73 peak (W10) to $53 trough (W12) to $58 (W13). The W12 CPA low was partially a spend pullback; W13's spend recovery (+14%) pushed CPA back up.

## Relevant actions and events
1. OCI E2E launched 2/26, now 4 weeks in. Revenue data exclusion added 3/23-3/31 for AMZ and Generic campaigns. This means the bidding algorithm is operating without conversion feedback for the final week of March, which could suppress bid optimization.
2. Budget increases in W12-W13: AMZ $1.2K to $2.1K (3/19), GA Top $1.2K to $1.8K (3/19), Generic CPT $140 to $150 (3/19), GA $1K to $1.2K (3/18). These explain the +16% NB click surge and +14% spend increase.
3. bruneau.fr at 39-47% NB IS on Generic. Despite this, NB regs grew +5% WoW. FR continues to grow NB through the competitive pressure, though at declining CVR.
4. Cross-market context: FR's +4% WoW recovery is moderate. UK (+14%) and IT (+8%) recovered more strongly from W12's mid-March softness.

## YoY assessment
+9% YoY regs (1,191 vs 1,088). +106% YoY spend ($69K vs $34K). Brand +3% YoY, NB +15% YoY. NB CPA +116% YoY ($74 vs $35). The spend-to-reg ratio is the worst in EU5: spending 2x more for 9% more regs. NB CPA has more than doubled YoY. OCI maturation is the only realistic path to bending this curve. LY W13 was +6% WoW; TY's +4% is slightly below the seasonal pattern.

## Monthly projection
Projected 5,100 regs vs OP2 4,694 (+9%). Projected $311K spend vs OP2 $254K (+22%). Remaining 3 days: 2 weekdays, 1 weekend day. W13 daily rates: weekday ~189, weekend ~124. Remaining: 2(189) + 1(124) = 502. MTD 4,609 + 502 = 5,111, rounded to 5,100. Spend: MTD $281K + ~$30K remaining = $311K. CPA: $61. FR is beating plan on regs (+9%) but the spend overshoot has widened from +17% (W12 projection) to +22%. The OCI revenue data exclusion (3/23-3/31) means the final week's bidding is unoptimized, which could inflate spend further.

## Flags
- Spend overshoot widening: +22% vs OP2 spend, up from +17% at W12. The NB click expansion is driving volume but at declining efficiency. NB CPA at $74 is the highest since W10's $73.
- NB CVR decline: -9% WoW to 2.64%. The +16% NB click surge came with lower-quality traffic. If CVR continues falling, the click expansion becomes uneconomical.
- OCI data exclusion: Revenue data excluded 3/23-3/31 for AMZ and Generic. The bidding algorithm is flying blind for the final week. This could suppress efficiency or cause erratic bidding.
- bruneau.fr: 39-47% NB IS is unchanged. FR's NB is growing through it, but the CVR ceiling is real.

## Suggested narrative angle
FR grew +4% WoW on a NB click surge (+16%), though NB CVR fell -9% as the expanded traffic converted at lower rates. Brand held flat. CPA rose to $58 (+10% WoW) as spend grew faster than regs. Monthly projection: +9% regs vs OP2 on +22% spend. The spend overshoot is widening. OCI is 4 weeks in with revenue data excluded through month-end, limiting bid optimization.
