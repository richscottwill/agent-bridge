## Introduction

This document provides the trailing-week operational state of the Amazon Business Worldwide Paid Search Testing program. The program spans five workstreams — OCI Bidding, Modern Search (Ad Copy), Audiences & Lifecycle, User Experience, and Algorithmic Ads — across 10 markets (US, UK, DE, FR, IT, ES, JP, CA, AU, MX). Richard Williams owns end-to-end test methodology, status tracking, and the Testing Approach document (v5, 8.4/10 PUBLISH verdict) pending Brandon Munday's review. The methodology follows a hypothesis → phased rollout → measurement → scale or stop framework. SyRT validated Non-Brand as 82–92% incremental, confirming the measurement foundation. The Testing Approach doc (v5) was scheduled for discussion at the April 16 meeting, which was canceled; the document remains valuable and needs to ship to Brandon independently.

## Goals

1. Ensure every active test across all five workstreams has a written status document by April 30, 2026, with zero undocumented tests.
2. Ship the Testing Approach document (v5, 8.4/10 PUBLISH verdict) to Brandon Munday for review by April 18, 2026.
3. Expand OCI to remaining markets — CA launch on April 7, AU target May 2026, MX timeline TBD — achieving 100% coverage of active markets by Q4 2026.
4. Complete the AI Max test design document for US Q2 2026 pilot by April 14, 2026 (currently 4 days overdue).

## Tenets

1. **Evidence before scale.** No test result ships to full traffic without a statistically valid measurement period. Confidence intervals matter more than directional signals.
2. **Phased rollout over big-bang.** Every test follows hypothesis → pilot → measure → expand or kill. This protects budget and preserves learning even when tests fail.
3. **Failures are data.** A failed test that produces a clear insight (e.g., Gated Guest at -61%) is more valuable than an untested assumption. Document failures with the same rigor as wins.
4. **Cross-functional partnership.** Testing at scale requires MarTech, Legal, Data Science, MCS, and market teams. The PS team designs and owns methodology; execution is collaborative.

## Lessons Learned

Three operational insights from the trailing four-week window (W11–W14) inform the immediate tactical posture:

1. **OCI produces consistent 18–24% reg uplift across geographies, confirming the methodology is market-agnostic.** US delivered +24%, UK +23%, DE +18%. The variance is narrow enough to project similar results for CA (launching April 7), AU (May 2026), and eventually MX. The implication: OCI expansion is not a question of whether it works but how fast the remaining markets can be onboarded. The binding constraint is MCC creation and Google Ads account setup, not methodology validation.

2. **Failed tests produce the clearest strategic signals when documented with the same rigor as wins.** Gated Guest's -61% conversion result was the most actionable finding in the UX workstream — it definitively eliminated a registration flow variant that multiple stakeholders had advocated for. The lesson is not that the test failed but that the failure, properly documented, prevented a WW rollout that would have destroyed conversion at scale. The Testing Approach document codifies this principle: every test gets a written status regardless of outcome.

3. **The Testing Approach document is the single highest-leverage artifact the PS team can ship, and it has been in draft for 4 months.** The v5 document scored 8.4/10 on the PUBLISH verdict. It is ready. The April 16 meeting with Kate was canceled, but the document does not require a meeting to ship — it requires Brandon's review and endorsement. Every week the document sits unshipped is a week where the PS team's testing methodology exists only in Richard's head and scattered Quip docs rather than in a leadership-endorsed, team-wide reference. This is the hard thing.

## Appendix F: Variance Bridge — Methodology Validation

The WW Testing program uses SyRT (Synthetic Randomized Testing) as the primary incrementality measurement framework. SyRT validated Non-Brand registrations as 82–92% incremental, establishing the measurement foundation for all NB-focused tests. OCI uplift figures (18–24% across US, UK, DE) are measured against pre-OCI baselines using a 4-week pre/post comparison with holdout groups where available. Ad Copy results use standard A/B testing with statistical significance thresholds (p < 0.05 for high confidence, p < 0.10 for medium). The IT Ad Copy test did not reach significance due to insufficient sample size (< 500 conversions in the test period).

The Testing Approach document (v5) formalizes these measurement standards and defines the hypothesis → phased rollout → measurement → scale or stop framework that governs all five workstreams. Until Brandon endorses the document, these standards exist as operational practice but not as formalized team policy.
