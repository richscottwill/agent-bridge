## Tenets

1. **Evidence before scale.** No test result ships to full traffic without a statistically valid measurement period. Confidence intervals matter more than directional signals.
2. **Phased rollout over big-bang.** Every test follows hypothesis → pilot → measure → expand or kill. This protects budget and preserves learning even when tests fail.
3. **Failures are data.** A failed test that produces a clear insight (e.g., Gated Guest at -61%) is more valuable than an untested assumption. Document failures with the same rigor as wins.
4. **Cross-functional partnership.** Testing at scale requires MarTech, Legal, Data Science, MCS, and market teams. The PS team designs and owns methodology; execution is collaborative.

## Lessons Learned

Three operational insights from the trailing four-week window (W12–W15) inform the immediate tactical posture:

1. **Polaris LP rollout produced its first validated positive result (+6% CVR in US) and its first production regression (Italy ref tag) in the same two-week window.** The US result (21-day pre/post March 24) is the first statistically-valid Polaris win. The Italy regression is operationally isolated (legacy restored within 24 hours of detection) but strategically costly: it demonstrates that the rollout process lacks an automated ref tag audit step. Every market rollout prior to Italy (US 3/24, EU3 preparations) could have contained the same defect undetected. The corrective action is a systematic ref tag audit applied to AU (next in the rollout queue per Brandon's priority order) before any further Polaris market launches. The US +6% CVR result is the evidence base that justifies continuing the rollout; the Italy regression defines the gate that precedes each market.

2. **OCI reached 8 of 10 markets at 100% with CA's 4/7 launch — the program is in its measurement phase, not its expansion phase, for the next 4–6 weeks.** FR/IT/ES/JP at 100% on 3/31 and CA at 100% on 4/7 close the first 2026 QBR goal. The remaining expansion work is AU and MX — both with no confirmed timeline and no MCC created. The binding constraint for both is MCC creation, not methodology. The 4/17 cross-MCC data exclusion incident (Sam Tangri → Jing Shi → Mike) surfaces a measurement-phase concern: Google's Smart Bidding prioritizes same-MCC learning, so US data exclusions can transiently distort UK/DE/FR performance for a day or two. This does not invalidate OCI uplift figures but does require acknowledgment in weekly readouts when US data exclusion events occur.

3. **The April 17 AI Tool Demo with 7 team members committing to adoption is the first Level-3 (Team Automation) traction the program has produced.** Adi (MX), Andrew (UK), Stacey (Polaris), Yun-Kang (Adobe NB), Dwayne (MCS), and Peter (Apps) each committed to a tool validation deliverable with a specific date range. Hedy captured the session. The Markdown-standardized output and shared SharePoint prompt repository are the structural decisions that make this adoption durable rather than a one-off novelty. If 4 of the 6 deliverables ship on schedule by early May, the tool graduates from "Richard's tool" to "PS team standard" — which is the L3 goal criterion.

## Appendix F: Variance Bridge — Methodology Validation

The WW Testing program uses SyRT (Synthetic Randomized Testing) as the primary incrementality measurement framework. SyRT validated Non-Brand registrations as 82–92% incremental, establishing the measurement foundation for all NB-focused tests. OCI uplift figures (18–24% across US, UK, DE) are measured against pre-OCI baselines using a 4-week pre/post comparison with holdout groups where available. Ad Copy results use standard A/B testing with statistical significance thresholds (p < 0.05 for high confidence, p < 0.10 for medium). The IT Ad Copy test did not reach significance due to insufficient sample size (< 500 conversions in the test period).

The Testing Approach document (v5) formalizes these measurement standards and defines the hypothesis → phased rollout → measurement → scale or stop framework that governs all five workstreams. Until Brandon endorses the document, these standards exist as operational practice but not as formalized team policy.
