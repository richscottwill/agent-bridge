# EU5 W13 Regional Analysis Brief

## Regional summary
EU5 total: 5,378 reported regs (-6% WoW), but DE data lag inflates the decline. Lag-corrected EU5 estimate: ~5,900-6,100 regs (+3% to +7% WoW). Spend: $400K (flat WoW). UK (+14%), IT (+8%), FR (+4%) recovered from W12 mid-March softness. ES was flat. DE reported -46% but is lag-affected. Combined projection: ~26,000 regs vs OP2 ~24,000 (+8%).

## Cross-market pattern: Recovery from W12 softness, except ES

W12 saw 4 of 5 markets decline. W13 reverses that: 3 of 4 non-lag markets recovered (UK +14%, IT +8%, FR +4%), ES held flat. The recovery is consistent with WW trends (+4.3% WoW) and suggests W12's mid-March softness was transient.

The recovery mechanism varied by market:
- UK: CVR-led (+17% blended CVR). Both segments improved on conversion quality, not click volume.
- IT: Click-led (NB clicks +23%). Volume expansion through budget increases, not conversion improvement.
- FR: Click-led (NB clicks +16%). Same pattern as IT: budget-driven NB expansion.
- ES: Mixed. NB clicks +13% offset Brand CVR decline (-11%). Net flat.
- DE: Unreadable due to data lag. Thu-Sat data suggests recovery to ~1,200-1,300 range.

Key distinction: UK's recovery was efficiency-driven (more conversions per click). FR, IT, and ES recovered through spend expansion (more clicks at similar or worse conversion rates). This explains why UK's CPA improved (-17%) while FR (+10%), IT (+16%), and ES (+17%) saw CPA increases.

## Spend efficiency diverged sharply

W13 split EU5 into two efficiency tiers:
- UK: Spend -5%, regs +14%. CPA improved -17% to $56. Efficiency compounding.
- FR/IT/ES: Spend +14-25%, regs +4-8%. CPA worsened +10-17%. Buying growth.
- DE: Unreadable. Reported spend -21% but lag-corrected CPA likely $85-$100.

The divergence is structural: UK has mature OCI (~30 weeks) and baked-in ad copy gains. FR/IT/ES have early OCI (4 weeks) and are expanding NB through budget increases rather than efficiency improvements. The spend-to-reg ratio will only improve as OCI matures in these three markets.

The exclusion is necessary for OCI calibration but creates a short-term blind spot.

## Regional flags

1. DE data lag is systemic: Two consecutive weeks of severe lag (W12 Fri/Sat, W13 Sun-Wed). This is no longer a one-off. Pipeline investigation is needed.
2. Spend efficiency split: UK improving, FR/IT/ES deteriorating. The gap will widen until OCI matures in the EU3.
3. OCI revenue data exclusion: FR/IT/ES bidding algorithms are blind 3/23-3/31. Watch for erratic spend or CPA in the final days of March.
4. ES spend overshoot: +35% vs OP2 is unsustainable. The reg beat (+21%) does not justify the premium. ES needs efficiency improvement, not more spend.
5. Brand Core bid increases: Both DE ($15 to $21) and IT ($19 to $24) raised max CPC on 3/23 to address impression limitations. W14 should show whether these unlock meaningful volume.
6. NB CPA inflation: 4 of 5 markets have NB CPA up 82-191% YoY. Only UK has improved (-24%). This is the defining structural challenge for EU5.

## Suggested regional narrative
EU5 recovered from W12's mid-March softness, with UK (+14%), IT (+8%), and FR (+4%) all gaining WoW. DE's reported -46% is data lag (two consecutive weeks now). ES was flat. The recovery split into two modes: UK improved through CVR efficiency (4.28%, 12-week high), while FR/IT/ES expanded through NB budget increases at declining efficiency. Monthly projections: 4 of 5 markets beating OP2 on regs, combined +9%. DE remains -4% vs plan, the tightest market. UK is the efficiency leader (+22% regs on -14% spend). ES's +35% spend overshoot is the biggest risk. OCI maturation in FR/IT/ES (4 weeks in, revenue data excluded through month-end) is the key lever for closing the NB CPA gap with UK.
