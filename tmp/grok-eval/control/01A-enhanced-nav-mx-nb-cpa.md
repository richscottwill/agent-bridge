## What's actually happening

Three things stacked:

1. **Spend stepped up, CVR didn't keep pace.** NB spend roughly doubled from Jan ($35k/4W) to now ($76k/4W). Regs scaled ~25% in the same window. We're buying more clicks at a higher CPC (Jan ~$1.00 → now ~$2.00) without proportional conversion lift — that's the primary CPA driver.

2. **W16 CVR compression is the acute problem.** 1.12% is the lowest week since W10, after four weeks in the 1.3–1.5% band. Daily breakdown:
   - Apr 11 (end of W15): 10 regs on $2.7k → $266 CPA, 0.68% CVR — single bad day that pulled W15 down
   - Apr 13: 14 regs on $3.2k → $229 CPA
   - Apr 18: 10 regs on $2.2k → $224 CPA, 0.81% CVR
   - CPC is flat (~$2.07), so this is conversion side, not auction.

3. **Against OP2, NB is the wrong-direction story in a market that's overall stretched.** OP2 Apr blended CPA target is $44; our blended is running well north of that and our NB-only is 4x the blended target. OP2 has been set against a very different CPA profile than we're currently delivering.

## The Yun-Kang hypothesis check

My W16 recovery prediction was wrong. The Beauty+Auto page lag theory doesn't hold — if it were page CVR stabilization, W16 should have been flat-to-up vs W15. Instead it got worse (1.32% → 1.12%). Two things I still need to pull before closing out the Quip thread:

- ABIX reftag attribution check for MX (Alex's Italy flag on 4/16) — if brand is cannibalizing NB attribution, that would explain NB-only degradation while blended regs hold.
- Bid/budget review across W15–W16 — spend held steady (~$20–21k/wk) but reg volume dropped 19%, so something on the targeting/match-type side may have shifted auction quality.
