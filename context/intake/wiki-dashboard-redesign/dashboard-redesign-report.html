1. Honest read

The wiki dashboard is a competent faceted search UI. It does one thing well — helping Richard find a wiki article by typing a keyword or clicking a market/topic/type filter. It does not do anything else a knowledge-base homepage is supposed to do. It doesn't tell him what the wiki is today (301 docs, 2 contradictions, 3 orphans, 18 stale). It doesn't show velocity (what got ingested this week). It doesn't surface demand (what did agents ask for and not find). It doesn't expose lineage (which raw sources compiled into which wiki pages). It doesn't feel like the projection engine — which answers "what is, what's trending, what's at risk, what's the confidence" before the user types anything. The wiki dashboard can get 70% of the way to that grammar through a single hero strip, a pinned ingest log, and three lint badges. The rest is value-add on top.

2. Principles from the research

These are the lenses the 50 suggestions come from. Each is tied to 3-5 sources. Content paraphrased for compliance; original phrasings are the property of the cited authors. Principles 1-11 are carried verbatim from the prior weekly-review / projection-engine redesign report to keep symmetry. Principles 12-17 are new, introduced by Karpathy's LLM Wiki gist and the AI-2027 / OpenBrain scenario.

  1. Density is the goal, not the enemy. Amazon WBR reviews 400-500 metrics in 60 minutes. The wiki homepage currently shows 1 number (doc count) on a page that could show 8. (Commoncog "Amazon WBR", Matt Ström "UI Density", Bloomberg Terminal.)
  2. The active title is the decision. "Wiki Search 301 docs · 49 archived" is a description. "Knowledge base: 301 docs, 18 stale, 3 orphans, 2 contradictions — lint pass needed" is a decision. (Urban Institute, Practical Reporting, MIT Sloan.)
  3. Sparklines everywhere you have numbers. Every hero number deserves its 6-week trace. Zero visual cost, +1 dimension. (Tufte sparkline theory, Observable "Big Insights Small Spaces", shadcn tables-sparkline.)
  4. Direct labels beat legends. The trust strip on projection.html labels pills directly ("WW 6/6", "US 2/6"). The wiki dashboard's filter pills already follow this — keep it, extend it to the new health pills. (Data Revelations, Evergreen.)
  5. Exception-driven reading. 198 DRAFT docs is background; 2 contradictions is foreground. Color encodes exception-ness, not category. (Commoncog WBR, SPC worldview.)
  6. Progressive disclosure, not pogosticking. The graph view, the health fan, the demand gap panel — all collapse by default. Essential first, detail on demand. (Interaction Design Foundation, Luzmo "State of Dashboards 2025".)
  7. A chart without a recommended action is a half-built chart. If the hero strip shows "3 orphans", the orphan chip opens the list with a "Lint: suggest parents" button. (Ethos App "Dashboards to Stories", Luzmo.)
  8. Small multiples for category comparison. 13 topics are easier to read as 13 panels on a shared Y than as a 13-row table. (Juice Analytics, Tufte via Jason Lockwood, Datawrapper small multiples.)
  9. Uncertainty as a fan, not a number. Wiki freshness rate isn't "64%", it's "64% ± a historical band of 58-71%". BoE fan chart form works here. (Wikipedia Fan chart, R Journal RJ-2015-002, BoE 1996.)
  10. Tabular numerics, always. Every count, every word count, every age needs font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums. (Jo Maendle on date alignment, MDN.)
  11. Invisible over visible (soul.md #5). The best intervention is one Richard doesn't consciously notice but that moves his scan time. Prefer structural over cosmetic. Filters for this.
  12. The wiki is a compiler artifact, not a search index. Karpathy's LLM Wiki gist makes this explicit: raw sources compile to wiki pages. The homepage should expose build health (what got recompiled, which pages went stale, which cross-refs broke) the way a CI dashboard exposes pipeline health. This isn't RAG — this is knowledge that compounds. (Karpathy gist, MindStudio compiler analogy, Denser.ai.) NEW
  13. Lint is a first-class operation. The three lint checks Karpathy names explicitly — orphan pages, contradictions, stale claims — are the wiki's health metrics. They belong on the hero strip, not buried. "Periodically, ask the LLM to health-check the wiki." The homepage surfaces the result of that health check at-a-glance. (Karpathy gist, Ghelbur Labs "what's missing".) NEW
  14. Index is content-oriented; log is time-oriented. Both are first-class. Karpathy: "index.md is content-oriented... log.md is chronological." The wiki-search homepage currently mashes the two into a single "recently updated" feed. Separating them into (a) content index organized by topic/category and (b) append-only ingest log gives both navigations their proper home. (Karpathy gist, Obsidian MOCs forum.) NEW
  15. Dual-audience (human reads, LLM parses). Wiki pages are consumed by two audiences — Richard scanning on a laptop, and kiro-server / rw-trainer / wiki-critic ingesting as context. The homepage should work for both. Human view: visual + chrome. Agent view: plain markdown with structured frontmatter. One toggle switches. (Karpathy gist "Obsidian is the IDE, the LLM is the programmer, the wiki is the codebase.") NEW
  16. Capability / calibration / drift as dashboard primitives (OpenBrain framing). AI-2027 treats AI systems as having observable capability (what can it do?), calibration (how confident is it?), and drift (what's changing?). Applied to a wiki: capability = coverage per topic, calibration = in-review-vs-published rate, drift = contradictions per week. These are the three numbers a reader should see before they see a search box. (AI 2027, TheNeuron AGI roadmap, arXiv Designing a Dashboard for Transparency.) NEW
  17. The wiki stays maintained because the cost of maintenance is near zero. Karpathy's thesis is economic, not aesthetic. Humans abandon wikis because bookkeeping is tedious. The homepage should make the maintenance cost (= lint pending + demand gap + stale count) visible so it doesn't silently grow. If orphans trend up for 3 weeks, that's a problem, not a rounding error. (Karpathy gist, Ghelbur Labs.) NEW
