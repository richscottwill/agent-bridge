# JP W12 Analysis Brief

## Registration summary
443 regs, -16% WoW. Spend $37K (-5% WoW). CPA $82 (+13% WoW). Brand 439 (-16% WoW). NB 4 (-20% WoW). Overall CVR 1.79% (-10% WoW). March projection: 1,950 regs at $160K spend ($82 CPA) vs OP2 2,360 regs at $164K ($70 CPA). -17% regs, -3% spend vs OP2.

## Why registrations changed
CVR-driven decline compounded by traffic loss. CVR fell -10% WoW (1.79% vs 1.97%) and clicks fell -7% (24.8K vs 26.7K). Both levers moved against performance.

Brand drove the entire decline:
- Brand regs -16% WoW (439 vs 522). Clicks dropped -12% (21.8K vs 24.8K) and CVR fell -4% (2.02% vs 2.11%). The click decline was the bigger factor. Brand CPA rose to $68 from $66.
- NB is statistically irrelevant: 4 regs vs 5 LW. NB clicks surged +56% (3,015 vs 1,934) but CVR collapsed -49% (0.13% vs 0.26%). NB CPA hit $1,642. These are noise-level volumes at 0-5 regs/week.

This is a Brand-only market. The -16% is entirely a Brand story: fewer people saw the ads (-12% clicks) and fewer of those who did converted (-4% CVR).

## Trend context
W12 breaks a two-week recovery and returns to the post-MHLW baseline:
- W2-W5: 973-1,047 regs (MHLW tail effect, 3.50% peak CVR)
- W6: 498 (MHLW fully gone, CVR crashed to 1.94%)
- W7-W9: 406-440 range, CVR 1.89-2.01% (post-MHLW baseline established)
- W10-W11: 527-532 (mini-recovery, CVR ~1.95%)
- W12: 443 (back to W7-W9 baseline)

The W10-W11 uptick (527-532) was a temporary bounce, not a new trajectory. W12 at 443 is squarely in the W7-W9 range. The post-MHLW baseline is ~430-440 regs/week at ~1.9-2.0% CVR.

CPA trajectory is concerning: $33 (W5, MHLW-inflated) → $65-67 (W6-W8) → $73-75 (W9-W11) → $82 (W12). CPA has risen every period since W5. The true post-MHLW CPA is settling in the $70-82 range and trending up as volume declines against relatively fixed spend.

## Relevant actions and events
- MHLW campaign ended 1/31. This was the dominant registration driver. The W5 spike (1,008 regs) was the last MHLW echo. Everything since W6 is the organic baseline. JP has not found a replacement demand source.
- Japan fiscal year end is March 31. Businesses closing books suppress new vendor onboarding in the final 2 weeks of March. The -12% Brand click decline and -4% CVR drop in W12 are consistent with fiscal year-end suppression beginning. LY W12 also dropped -28% WoW, confirming this is a seasonal pattern. W13 (Mar 22-28) will likely be softer still.
- New Yahoo competitors: shop-pro.jp at 12-15% IS on Yahoo Brand Core, axalpha.com at 10% IS on Google Brand Core. Combined competitive pressure is contributing to Brand click erosion. This is a structural headwind that didn't exist at this intensity 12 weeks ago.
- OCI E2E launched 2/26. Now ~24 days in. OCI impact on NB is irrelevant because NB volume is 0-5 regs/week. OCI's value in JP depends on whether it can improve Brand bidding efficiency, which is a different use case than US/CA.

## YoY assessment
+4% regs YoY (443 vs 427). Barely positive. Spend +17% ($37K vs $31K).
- Brand: +3% YoY (439 vs 426 implied). Marginal growth.
- NB: +300% YoY (4 vs 1). Meaningless at these volumes.
- Spending 17% more to get 4% more regs is a deteriorating efficiency story. CPA at $82 vs $73 LY (+12% YoY).

WoW pattern: TY -16% vs LY -28%. Both years saw a W12 drop, confirming seasonal fiscal year-end softness. TY's dip is less severe, which is the one positive signal.

LY W13 dropped further to 386 regs (-10% from LY W12's 427), then recovered in W14-W15 (498, 548) as the new fiscal year began. TY should follow a similar pattern: W13 soft, W14+ recovery.

## Monthly projection
21/31 days elapsed. MTD: 1,502 regs, $114K spend.

Remaining 10 days (Mar 22-31): 6 weekdays, 4 weekend days. No JP national holidays, but fiscal year-end suppression is the dominant factor.

Daily rate estimation from W12 actuals:
- Weekday avg: (108 + 77 + 82 + 60 + 44) / 5 = 74 regs/day, $6.8K spend/day
- Weekend avg: (35 + 37) / 2 = 36 regs/day, $3.0K spend/day

W12 already showed fiscal year-end suppression (Mon 108 was strong but Thu-Fri collapsed to 60 and 44). The remaining 10 days span the final week of the fiscal year. Apply fiscal year-end suppression factor.

LY pattern: W12 (427) → W13 (386), a -10% WoW decline. The final week of March (W13 equivalent) is historically the weakest.

Suppression adjustment: Apply -15% to weekday rates for Mar 24-28 (final full business week before fiscal year close) and -25% for Mar 31 (fiscal year-end day). Weekend rates stay flat (already low).

Adjusted daily rates:
- Mar 22-23 (Sat-Sun): 36 regs/day × 2 = 72
- Mar 24-28 (Mon-Fri): 74 × 0.85 = 63 regs/day × 5 = 315
- Mar 29-30 (Sat-Sun): 36 × 2 = 72
- Mar 31 (Mon, fiscal year-end): 74 × 0.75 = 56

Remaining estimate: 72 + 315 + 72 + 56 = 515 regs, ~$46K spend.

**Analyst projection: 2,020 regs at $160K spend, $79 CPA.**
vs OP2: -14% regs, -2% spend.

The -14% miss is structural. OP2 targets assumed either residual MHLW benefit or faster organic recovery. Neither materialized. The fiscal year-end suppression in the final 10 days will widen the gap from the current -6% MTD pace.

## Flags
- CPA anomaly: $82 is 28% above the 8-week average ($65). CPA has risen in 5 of the last 6 periods. Without a volume recovery or efficiency intervention, CPA will continue climbing as fixed spend spreads across fewer conversions.
- OP2 miss trajectory: -14% regs vs OP2 projected for March. JP is the only market among US/CA/JP tracking below OP2 on regs. The miss is MHLW-driven and structural.
- NB is not a lever: 0-5 regs/week. OCI E2E cannot fix JP's volume problem because the problem is Brand traffic, not NB efficiency. JP needs a demand generation initiative (equivalent to MHLW), not channel optimization.
- Fiscal year-end risk: W13 (Mar 22-28) and Mar 31 will likely be the weakest registration days of the month. LY W13 dropped -10% from W12. If TY follows the same pattern, W13 could land at ~400 regs.
- Competitive pressure intensifying: shop-pro.jp (12-15% Yahoo IS) and axalpha.com (10% Google IS) are a combined headwind on Brand clicks that didn't exist at this level 3 months ago.

## Suggested narrative angle
Post-MHLW reality check compounded by fiscal year-end. W12 confirms the organic baseline is ~440 regs/week, not the 527-532 seen in W10-W11. CPA at $82 is the highest in 8 weeks and trending up. March tracking -14% vs OP2 on regs. The MHLW gap has not been closed by organic demand or OCI (NB volume is negligible). Fiscal year-end suppression will make the final 10 days the weakest of the month. Competitive pressure from shop-pro.jp and axalpha.com is adding Brand click erosion.
