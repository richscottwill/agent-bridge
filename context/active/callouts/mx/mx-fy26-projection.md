# MX FY26 Annual Projection

Created: 2026-03-30
Based on: Q1 actuals through W13, LY seasonal shape, conservative Brand estimates (-10%)

## Key Assumptions

- ie%CCP = CPA / CCP. Lower = more efficient. 75% = tighter than 100%.
- CCP: Brand $90, NB $30 (verify from dashboard — values change)
- Brand CPA: $21 (stable, structural)
- Brand regs: Q1 actuals, Apr-Dec from LY seasonal shape × ~2.7x YoY growth, then -10% conservative haircut
- NB CPA at 100% ie%CCP: $134 (current operating point)
- NB CPA at 75% ie%CCP: $115 (improved from cutting expensive marginal regs)
- Brand spend is structural (~10x LY, not reverting)
- Q1: both scenarios use actuals. Scenarios diverge Apr-Dec.
- OP2 ($542K/11,178 regs) was built at Q1 100% ie%CCP, Q2+ 75% ie%CCP
- Finance allocation: $1.8M. OP2 spend plan reflects ie%CCP-constrained subset.

## Formula

ie%CCP target T: (B×21 + N×NB_CPA) / (B×90 + N×30) = T

At 100%: N = B × 0.663 (NB CPA $134)
At 75%: N = B × 0.503 (NB CPA $115)

## Key Takeaways

1. Brand outperformance is the driver. Q1 Brand ran ~2.7x above LY. Even with a -10% haircut, Brand projects ~9.4K regs vs OP2's estimated ~5-6K. This creates CCP surplus that funds NB.

2. The gap vs OP2 is mostly Brand. Both scenarios project 25-35% more total regs than OP2, driven almost entirely by Brand overperformance.

3. NB is the variable. S1 runs ~5.7K NB regs at $134 CPA. S2 runs ~4.6K at $120 CPA. The difference is ~1,100 NB regs and $214K spend.

4. Spend significantly exceeds OP2 in both scenarios. S1 needs $959K (+77% vs OP2). S2 needs $745K (+37%). Both are well within the $1.8M finance allocation but far above the $542K ie%CCP-constrained plan.

5. The practical constraint is PO/budget approval, not ie%CCP. If finance approves incremental NB spend, the regs are available. If not, actual results will land between OP2 and these projections.

## Risks & Sensitivities

- Brand growth moderation: if Brand regs slow to 2x LY instead of 2.7x, both scenarios lose ~1,500 Brand regs and corresponding NB capacity
- NB CPC trend: $1.95 and rising. If NB CPA drifts to $150+, S1 ie%CCP worsens and NB regs decrease
- CCP revision: if finance revises CCP downward again, ie%CCP tightens and NB capacity shrinks
- Brand CPA drift: if Brand CPA rises from $21 to $25+, Brand surplus shrinks, reducing NB headroom
- Seasonal events: Hot Sale (May/Jun), Prime Day (Jul) could spike Brand temporarily but not sustainably
