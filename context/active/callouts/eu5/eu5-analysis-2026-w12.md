# EU5 W12 Regional Analysis Brief

## Regional summary
EU5 total: 5,717 regs (-9% WoW), $399K spend (-15% WoW), $70 CPA (-7% WoW). Only UK held flat (+1%); DE (-18%), IT (-12%), ES (-8%), and FR (-3%) all declined. Combined projection: ~25,600 regs vs OP2 ~24,000 (+7% regs).

## Cross-market pattern: 4 of 5 EU5 markets declined WoW

This is the defining feature of W12. Four markets declined simultaneously, which demands a cross-market explanation before attributing to market-specific dynamics.

Possible shared factors:
1. Mid-March seasonality. LY W12 showed declines in 4 of 5 markets too: DE -2%, IT -9%, ES -12%, FR -18%. Only UK was different (LY -16% vs TY +1%). The direction is consistent with seasonal softness, though the magnitude varies.
2. Data lag. DE has confirmed data lag (Fri/Sat at 32 avg regs vs 236 weekday avg). No other market shows obvious lag in daily breakdowns, but if the reporting pipeline had a partial delay, it could inflate the WoW decline across markets.
3. OCI E2E infrastructure. FR, IT, and ES all launched OCI E2E on 2/26. Three weeks in, the system is in learning phase. If OCI E2E is temporarily suppressing auction participation or bid efficiency during calibration, it could explain synchronized softness in these three markets. DE and UK (OCI fully live) would be unaffected by this.
4. Duplicate hvocijid parameter issue. Under investigation across EU markets. If this is causing conversion tracking errors, it could suppress reported CVR without actual demand decline.

Assessment: The most likely explanation is a combination of mid-March seasonality (consistent with LY patterns) and OCI E2E learning phase in FR/IT/ES. DE's decline is overstated by data lag. UK's resilience is explained by mature OCI + ad copy test benefits.

## Spend efficiency improved everywhere

Every EU5 market saw spend decline more than regs, meaning CPA improved WoW across the board:
- UK: spend +2%, regs +1% (CPA +2%, slight exception)
- DE: spend -19%, regs -18% (CPA -1%)
- FR: spend -15%, regs -3% (CPA -12%)
- IT: spend -17%, regs -12% (CPA -6%)
- ES: spend -19%, regs -8% (CPA -12%)

FR and ES saw the largest CPA improvements. This pattern suggests the algorithms are pulling back on expensive clicks, which is healthy behavior even if it temporarily reduces volume.

## Regional flags

1. Universal Brand decline: All 5 markets saw Brand regs fall WoW. This is either seasonal, competitive, or a reporting artifact. Needs one more week of data to confirm.
2. DE data lag: The -18% headline is unreliable. Wait for Fri/Sat data to normalize before drawing conclusions.
3. NB CPA inflation: 4 of 5 markets have NB CPA up 30-93% YoY. Only UK has improved (-13%). OCI maturation in FR/IT/ES is the primary lever.
4. FR/ES overspend: Both markets are significantly over OP2 on spend (+17% and +28%). If reg beats don't hold, these become efficiency problems.
5. hvocijid duplicate parameter: Under investigation. If causing conversion tracking errors, the cross-market CVR softness may be partially artificial.

## Suggested regional narrative
EU5 delivered 5,717 regs in W12, with 4 of 5 markets declining WoW in a pattern consistent with mid-March seasonality (LY showed similar directional declines). UK was the exception, holding flat on NB strength. DE's -18% headline is inflated by data lag. Brand declined in all 5 markets, the clearest cross-market signal. Monthly projections: 4 of 5 markets beating OP2 on regs, with DE essentially flat vs plan after data lag correction. Combined EU5 projected at ~25,600 regs vs ~24,000 OP2 (+7%). OCI maturation in FR/IT/ES (launched 2/26) is the key lever for NB CPA improvement over the next 8-12 weeks.
