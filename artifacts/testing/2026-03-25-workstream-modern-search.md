# Workstream 2: Modern Search

*Progression: Fragmented campaigns → Device and Keyword Theme Consolidation → Research-Driven Ad Copy → Fueling Machine Learning & OCI*

## What We Did

### Campaign Consolidation

To align with Google's modern search best practices, the team consolidated device-specific campaigns and keyword themes across markets. Prior to consolidation, campaigns were fragmented across device types and narrow keyword themes, limiting the data available to each bid strategy. By consolidating, we provided the bidding algorithms with richer signal density, which directly supported the transition to OCI-powered bidding.

In the US, we implemented Responsive Search Ads (RSAs) and consolidated device-specific campaigns, reducing total campaign count and enabling more streamlined management. In EU5, we applied the same approach — UK and DE Product and Vertical campaigns have already been consolidated, with the remaining EU markets following the same playbook as OCI rolls out. The consolidation directly improved data density per campaign: fewer campaigns means more conversions per bid strategy, which means faster algorithm learning and more accurate CPA optimization.

**So what:** Campaign consolidation is not a cleanup exercise — it is a prerequisite for OCI and AI Max. Fragmented campaigns starve algorithms of signal. Consolidated structures let OCI optimize across a richer dataset, which is why the US saw +24% reg lift after OCI launched on consolidated campaigns. This structural simplification is also a prerequisite for AI Max testing (see Algorithmic Ads section), which requires consolidated campaign structures to function effectively.

### Research-Driven Ad Copy

The team incorporated learnings from the AB Sole Proprietor Experience study — a primary customer research initiative conducted in August 2025 — to develop evidence-based ad copy. The study surveyed Sole Proprietors in the US and UK about what matters most when choosing suppliers.

**What SPs said matters most:**
| Factor | US | UK |
|--------|----|----|
| Price | 31% | 22% |
| Product quality | 25% | 33% |
| Selection | 21% | 15% |

**What SPs said matters least:**
| Factor | US | UK |
|--------|----|----|
| Ability to buy in bulk | 27% | 19% |
| Location of store | 24% | 23% |
| Rewards program | 22% | 25% |

**Why SPs without B2B accounts didn't sign up:**
| Reason | US | UK |
|--------|----|----|
| Believed B2B required bulk purchasing | 50% | 49% |
| Savings wouldn't justify perceived costs | 31% | 26% |
| Business wouldn't qualify | 19% | 18% |

The critical insight: 50% of Sole Proprietors believed Amazon Business required bulk purchasing and was not free. Our existing ads reinforced both misconceptions.

### What Changed in Ad Copy

| Before | After | Rationale |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Online Bulk Purchasing | Smart Business Buying | Updated brand messaging |
| Online Wholesale Purchasing | For Businesses of All Sizes | Addresses qualification concern |
| Purchase at Wholesale Price | No Minimum Order Required | Addresses bulk misconception |
| Wholesale & Bulk Prices | Quantity Discounts | Reframes value without bulk framing |

We also added keyword insertion to headlines — only 6% of UK Brand ads and 23% of UK NB ads included keyword insertion previously. This allows Google to match user intent more precisely.

## What We Learned

### UK AMZ Portfolio (Phase 1 — Jan 29 to Mar 2, 2026)

**Pre/Post Comparison:**
| Metric | Before (Dec 27-Jan 28) | After (Jan 29-Mar 2) | Change |
|--------|----------------------|---------------------|--------|
| Impressions | 37,388 | 28,010 | -25% |
| Clicks | 5,308 | 6,778 | +28% |
| Cost | $19,253 | $20,072 | +4% |
| Registrations | 273 | 358 | **+31%** |
| CTR | 14% | 24% | **+70%** |
| CVR | 5.10% | 5.30% | +3% |

Despite 25% fewer impressions, the new ads drove 28% more clicks and 31% more registrations. The CTR improvement of 70% is the headline metric — the research-driven messaging resonated with the target audience at a measurably higher rate. Confidence: HIGH (30-day test, meaningful volume).

**IT (Phase 1 — Feb 19 to Mar 5, 2026):**
CTR directionally positive at +15%, but volume too low to draw conclusions (-97% clicks vs control). Confidence: LOW (insufficient volume). Need more time.

### Connection to Competitive Strategy

This test directly addresses competitive pressure. As competitors increase Brand CPCs (Walmart at 37-55% IS in US, weareuncapped at 24% IS in UK), the team's response is not to escalate bids but to make ads more compelling — driving higher CTR and CVR from the same or fewer impressions. This is a sustainable competitive advantage because it is based on customer research rather than budget.
