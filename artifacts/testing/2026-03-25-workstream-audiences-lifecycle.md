# Workstream 3: Audiences — Utilizing B2B Reach

*Progression: LiveRamp Suppression → Engagement Account Creation → Associated Accounts Integration → F90 Lifecycle Engagement*

## What We Did

Paid Search teams typically function as top-of-funnel acquisition engines, accepting platform limitations and relying on provided dashboards. This team operated differently by partnering directly with lifecycle strategy owners to extend Paid Search beyond acquisition into customer engagement.

### Phase 1: LiveRamp Suppression (Feb 2025)

We implemented LiveRamp suppression across US and CA campaigns, removing current AB customers from acquisition targeting to reduce wasted spend. This established the audience infrastructure for more advanced targeting — the plumbing that made everything else possible.

### Phase 2: Engagement Account (2025)

We built a dedicated Engagement account in partnership with the Traffic & Onsite Marketing team, creating a net-new channel that bridged top-of-funnel acquisition and lifecycle strategy.

The primary hurdle was Google's low B2B customer recognition rate — initially just 13% of our customer list could be matched to Google users. To overcome this, the team partnered with ABMA to ingest Associated Accounts (consumer-side emails linked to B2B users). This was a manual platform intervention that required cross-team coordination between PS, ABMA, and MarTech.

| Metric | Before Associated Accounts | After Associated Accounts |
|--------|---------------------------|--------------------------|
| Google match rate | 13% | 30% |
| Audience reach | Limited | More than doubled |

**So what:** The match rate jump from 13% to 30% more than doubled our addressable audience for engagement campaigns. This was not a Google feature — it was a cross-functional data integration that the PS team initiated with ABMA and MarTech.

### Phase 3: Engagement Performance (2025)

With the expanded audience, the Engagement channel drove $765K in iOPS in 2025 (source: Paid Acquisition Flash, Andrew Wirtz).

During Prime Day 2025, the Engagement campaigns drove 80K clicks at -10% cost YoY, generating $329K in OPS at a 644% ROAS — a 12x improvement over the prior year (source: Paid Acquisition Flash, May 2025). This validated that the Engagement channel could deliver at scale during high-value events.

The Engagement account also enabled the Business Essentials launch through Demand Gen image placements, delivering 52K visitors in the first year at a $0.30 CPC.

## What We Learned

1. **Platform limitations are problems to solve, not constraints to accept.** The 13% match rate was not a Google limitation — it was an Amazon data integration opportunity that the team solved through ABMA partnership.
2. **Engagement requires a different operating model.** Acquisition targets unknown prospects; Engagement targets known customers with lifecycle goals. Measurement, creative, and audience strategy are all different.
3. **Audience precision drives cost efficiency.** Demand Gen CPC in the Engagement account: $0.39 vs. $2.43 for keyword campaigns — 84% lower — because targeting was more precise.

## 2026: F90 Lifecycle Engagement

With the Engagement account proven, the team is navigating Legal SIMs to launch the F90 (First 90 Days) program.

**F90 targets:** Recently acquired, non-purchasing customers to complete 3+ purchases and adopt high-value actions.

**Goal:** Increase the percentage of non-SHuMA customers completing 3+ purchases from 31.7% to 35.4% (+366 bps YoY).

**Current status:** Legal is reviewing new audiences and audience-transmission solutions for use across Engagement ad types. The LiveRamp 1P positive targeting approval is on track for US by end of April 2026, but expansion to additional regions is blocked pending resolution of Tumble service requirements (per Brand & Paid Media Flash, Feb 2026).

This extends Paid Search's role from acquisition into activation — a fundamentally different operating model for the channel. If successful, it establishes PS as a lifecycle partner, not just a top-of-funnel engine.
